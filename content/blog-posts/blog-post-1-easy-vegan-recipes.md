## Introduction

Going vegan - or even just eating more plant-based meals - doesn't have to be complicated. I know because I've been there. When I first started experimenting with vegan cooking, I was overwhelmed by recipes that called for 15 specialty ingredients I'd never heard of. Nutritional yeast? Aquafaba? Tapioca starch? It felt like learning a new language.

But here's what I've learned after creating dozens of plant-based recipes: the best vegan meals are usually the simplest ones. A two-ingredient pasta that takes 13 minutes. A cookie dough you can eat straight from the bowl in 5 minutes. A bean salad that gets better the longer it sits in your fridge.

That's exactly what this list is - 10 easy vegan recipes that anyone can make, even if you've never cooked a plant-based meal in your life. Every recipe here uses ingredients you can find at any grocery store, and most are ready in under 30 minutes. Whether you're fully vegan, trying Meatless Mondays, or just curious about eating more plants, these are the recipes to start with.

## 2. Cozy Chunky Oatmeal

[INSERT IMAGE: recipe-images/cozy-chunky-oatmeal/]

This isn't your typical watery, sad oatmeal. This is thick, chunky, stick-to-your-ribs oatmeal that feels like a warm hug in a bowl. Top it with frozen blueberries, almonds, peanut butter, and a drizzle of agave, and you've got a breakfast that keeps you full for hours.

I make this at least twice a week during the colder months. It takes under 10 minutes and uses ingredients you probably already have in your pantry. Use plant milk instead of dairy and it's fully vegan.

- **Total time:** 7-12 minutes
- **Servings:** 1
- **Calories:** 450-550
- **Difficulty:** Absolute beginner

## 3. No-Egg Pasta Pomodoro

[INSERT IMAGE: recipe-images/no-egg-pasta-pomodoro/]

If you loved the two-ingredient pasta above, this takes it to the next level with a rustic pomodoro sauce. The trick is using both air-fried and stovetop tomatoes together - the air-fried ones get sweet and caramelized while the stovetop ones stay bright and acidic. That combination creates layers of flavor that taste like you spent way more time on it than you did.

The pasta is the same simple flour-and-water dough, so you're really just learning one extra skill here: a killer tomato sauce.

- **Total time:** 35 minutes
- **Servings:** 2
- **Calories:** 400-450
- **Difficulty:** Easy

## 4. Crispy Tofu Lettuce Wraps

[INSERT IMAGE: recipe-images/crispy-tofu-lettuce-wraps/]

If you've ever said "I don't like tofu," I need you to try this recipe before you write it off forever. The secret is pressing the tofu well and air-frying it until it's genuinely crispy on the outside. Then you coat it in a savory peanut sauce, pile it on crunchy carrot slaw, and wrap it all in cool lettuce leaves.

These wraps are light but surprisingly filling at 18-20g of protein per serving. They're one of my go-to dinners when I want something fresh, flavorful, and quick.

- **Total time:** 40 minutes
- **Servings:** 2-3
- **Calories:** 280 per serving
- **Protein:** 18-20g per serving
- **Difficulty:** Easy

## 10. Plant-Based Milk Four Ways

[INSERT IMAGE: recipe-images/plant-based-milk-four-ways/]

Once you make your own plant-based milk, you'll never look at store-bought the same way. This guide walks you through four different milks - hemp, rice, almond, and cashew - so you can find your favorite.

The surprise winner? Hemp milk. It has the most flavor and the most interesting taste. Rice milk gives off nostalgic horchata vibes. Almond is the most versatile for cooking and baking. And cashew is thick like heavy cream - perfect for sauces and coffee. Each one takes about 10 minutes of active time.

- **Active time:** 10 minutes per milk
- **Makes:** 3-4 cups per batch
- **Calories:** 30-150 per cup (varies by type)
- **Difficulty:** Absolute beginner

## Conclusion

That's 10 vegan recipes that prove plant-based cooking doesn't need to be complicated, expensive, or bland. Whether you start with a 5-minute cookie dough or take on homemade pasta, the important thing is just to start.

My advice? Pick one recipe from this list and make it this week. Just one. Once you see how easy (and delicious) it is, you'll naturally want to try another. That's how it happened for me, and now I have a whole collection of plant-based meals I make on repeat.
