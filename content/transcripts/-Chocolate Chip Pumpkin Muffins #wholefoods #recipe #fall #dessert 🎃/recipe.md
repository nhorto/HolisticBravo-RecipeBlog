### Description
These chocolate chip pumpkin muffins are perfect for fall and incredibly easy to make. You only need one large bowl, making cleanup a breeze. Made with whole food ingredients including pumpkin puree, oat flour, and optional extra protein from egg whites.

### Ingredients

**Dry Ingredients:**
- 150g all-purpose flour
- 70g oat flour
- 1g baking soda (approximately 1/4 tsp)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp pumpkin pie spice
- Mini chocolate chips (to taste - "you'll know when it's enough")

**Wet Ingredients:**
- 1 whole egg
- 1 can pure pumpkin puree
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 50g egg whites (optional for extra protein - can substitute with 1 additional whole egg)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. In a large bowl, combine all dry ingredients: all-purpose flour, oat flour, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice.
3. Add mini chocolate chips to dry ingredients (add until it looks right to you).
4. In the same bowl, add all wet ingredients: egg, pumpkin puree, olive oil, maple syrup, egg whites (or additional egg), and vanilla extract.
5. Mix everything together in the bowl until well combined.
6. If using a non-stick muffin pan, no preparation needed. Otherwise, use liners or non-stick spray.
7. Divide batter evenly among 12 muffin cups.
8. Bake at 425°F for 20-25 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
9. Let cool and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients
Pure pumpkin puree provides moisture, fiber, and fall flavor while keeping these muffins naturally sweet. Oat flour adds whole grain nutrition and a subtle nutty flavor. The combination of all-purpose and oat flour creates the perfect texture. Pumpkin pie spice brings warm autumn aromatics. Egg whites are optional but add extra protein without changing the flavor.

### Tips & Variations
- Invest in a food scale ($10) to make measuring easier and reduce cleanup from measuring cups
- If you don't have oat flour, blend rolled oats in a blender until fine
- Try adding chopped walnuts or pecans for crunch
- Swap chocolate chips for white chocolate or butterscotch chips
- Use pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling (which has added sugar and spices)
- Store in airtight container at room temperature for 3 days or refrigerate for up to a week
- These freeze beautifully for up to 3 months

### FAQ
1. **Can I make these without oat flour?** Yes, you can use all all-purpose flour (220g total), though you'll lose some of the whole grain nutrition.
2. **Why bake at such a high temperature?** The high heat creates a nice dome on top and sets the exterior quickly while keeping the inside moist.
3. **Can I reduce the sugar?** These are already lightly sweetened with just 2 tbsp maple syrup. The pumpkin and chocolate chips provide natural sweetness.
