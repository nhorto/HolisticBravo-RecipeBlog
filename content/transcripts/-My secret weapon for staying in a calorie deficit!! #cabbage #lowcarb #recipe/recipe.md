### Description

Cabbage is the ultimate low-carb, low-calorie BFF you didn't know you needed! At only 22 calories per cup, it's an excellent source of vitamins K and C, has anti-inflammatory properties, and improves digestion. This recipe shows three completely different ways to use one large head of cabbage: quick-pickled for gut health, transformed into savory "noodles" with Asian-inspired broth, and as a creative base for eggs in a basket. Perfect for meal prep and staying satisfied while in a calorie deficit.

### Ingredients

**For Pickled Cabbage:**
- Shredded cabbage (portion of the head)
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 cups hot water

**For Cabbage Noodles:**
- Shredded cabbage (large portion of the head)
- Large pot of salted water
- 1 tablespoon curry powder
- 1 tablespoon garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon ginger powder
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- 1 teaspoon cilantro flakes
- 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar (any vinegar works)
- 1 tablespoon low-sodium soy sauce
- Parmesan cheese (optional, or nutritional yeast for vegan)

**For Eggs in a Cabbage Basket:**
- Handful of shredded cabbage
- 1 whole egg
- Egg whites
- Sea salt and black pepper
- Toast or rice for serving

### Instructions

#### Recipe 1: Pickled Cabbage

1. **Shred Cabbage:** Shred a portion of the cabbage head and pack into a mason jar.

2. **Make Brine:** In a bowl, combine 1 tablespoon salt, 1 tablespoon sugar, 1 cup apple cider vinegar, and 2 cups hot water. Stir until dissolved.

3. **Pickle:** Pour brine over cabbage in jar. Close lid and refrigerate.

4. **Wait:** Let sit in fridge overnight before using.

5. **Use:** Lasts in fridge for about 2 weeks. Eat a spoonful in the morning for a metabolism jolt, or use as a topping on everything.

#### Recipe 2: Cabbage Noodles

1. **Prepare Pot:** Bring a large pot of salted water to boil.

2. **Add Seasonings to Water:** Add curry powder, garlic powder, onion powder, ginger powder, chili powder, red pepper flakes, cilantro flakes, red wine vinegar, and soy sauce to the boiling water.

3. **Add Cabbage:** Add shredded cabbage to the spiced water.

4. **Cook:** Let cabbage soak in the spiced water on the stove for about 30 minutes until fork tender but still slightly al dente.

5. **Serve:** Scoop cabbage "noodles" into a bowl. Add some of the flavorful broth. Top with shredded parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast for vegan option).

#### Recipe 3: Eggs in a Cabbage Basket

1. **Cook Cabbage Base:** Add a handful of shredded cabbage to a pan over medium-low heat. Cover and let cook for a few minutes until starting to soften.

2. **Create Well:** Make a little hole in the middle of the cabbage for the egg to sit in.

3. **Add Egg:** Crack one whole egg into the center well.

4. **Add Egg Whites:** Pour egg whites directly on top of the cabbage around the whole egg to hold everything together and add extra protein.

5. **Season:** Add sea salt and black pepper to taste.

6. **Cook:** Cover and cook until egg is set to your preference.

7. **Serve:** Place on top of toast or serve with rice or any grain you like.
