**Servings:** 1 serving

### Description
A simple holiday season breakfast that comes together quickly using tomatoes close to their expiration. Fresh eggs are cooked directly on halved tomatoes with bok choy microgreens on top, creating a nutritious and visually appealing meal.

### Ingredients
- 2-3 tomatoes (any variety)
- 3 fresh eggs
- Bok choy microgreens (or any microgreens)
- Pickled cabbage (for serving, supports gut health)
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Oil or cooking spray for pan

### Instructions
1. Heat a medium pan over medium heat. Lightly oil or spray the pan.
2. Halve the tomatoes.
3. Place tomatoes cut-side down in the pan.
4. Let tomatoes cook for 2-3 minutes to soften and release juices.
5. Crack 3 fresh eggs directly on top of the tomatoes in the pan.
6. Season eggs with salt and pepper.
7. Add bok choy microgreens on top of everything.
8. Cover the pan with a lid and cook until eggs are done to your liking (about 5-7 minutes for set whites and runny yolks).
9. Transfer to a plate.
10. Serve with pickled cabbage on the side for gut health and digestion support.
11. Enjoy immediately!

### About the Ingredients
This recipe is perfect for using tomatoes that are close to their expiration date. Cooking them cut-side down in the pan releases their natural juices and creates a flavorful base for the eggs. Bok choy microgreens add a peppery, nutrient-dense element. Pickled cabbage (or any fermented vegetables) provides probiotics that support gut health and aid digestion.

### Use Cases
Perfect for a quick breakfast during busy holiday mornings. Ideal for using up tomatoes before they go bad. Great for anyone looking to add more vegetables to their breakfast. Excellent for supporting digestive health with the addition of fermented foods.

### Tips & Variations
- Use any type of tomatoes you have on hand - cherry, roma, beefsteak, or heirloom
- Substitute any microgreens: radish, sunflower, pea shoots, or arugula
- Add garlic or herbs to the pan with the tomatoes
- Use sauerkraut or kimchi instead of pickled cabbage
- Top with hot sauce, salsa, or pesto for extra flavor
- Add cheese on top of eggs for richness
- Serve over toast or with crusty bread
- Can use spinach or kale instead of microgreens
- Adjust cooking time based on how you like your eggs (longer for hard-cooked yolks)

### FAQ
1. **Can I use regular greens instead of microgreens?** Yes! Baby spinach, arugula, or chopped kale all work great.
2. **What if I don't have pickled vegetables?** You can serve with fresh vegetables, avocado, or skip the side entirely. The main dish is still delicious and complete.
3. **Do I need to cover the pan?** Covering helps the eggs cook through more evenly, especially the tops and yolks. If you prefer, you can baste the eggs with the tomato juices instead.
