### Ingredients

**For the High-Protein Bagels:**
- 140g (about 3/4 cup) all-purpose flour
- 140g (about 3/4 cup) Greek yogurt
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- Everything bagel seasoning (optional topping)

**For the Egg Filling:**
- Egg whites (amount per bagel, to preference)
- Fresh spinach
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- Chili powder, to taste
- Smoked gouda cheese (or cheese of choice)

### Instructions

1. **Make the Bagel Dough:** In a bowl, combine 140g flour, 140g Greek yogurt, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 teaspoon baking powder. Mix until a dough forms.

2. **Shape the Bagels:** Divide dough into equal portions (4-6 bagels depending on size preference). Shape each portion into a bagel shape with a hole in the center.

3. **Add Topping (Optional):** Brush tops with water or egg wash, then sprinkle with everything bagel seasoning.

4. **Air Fry the Bagels:** Place bagels in air fryer at 350°F. Cook for 10 minutes, flip, then cook for another 10 minutes on the other side (20 minutes total).

5. **Prepare the Eggs:** Heat a small pan (sized to fit perfectly on the bagel). Add egg whites to the pan.

6. **Add Vegetables and Seasonings:** Add fresh spinach to the egg whites. Season with salt, pepper, and chili powder. Cook until eggs are set.

7. **Add Cheese:** Place a slice of smoked gouda (or your favorite cheese) on top of the eggs while still in the pan to melt slightly.

8. **Assemble:** Slice bagels in half. Place cooked egg and cheese between bagel halves.

9. **Meal Prep:** Make multiple sandwiches at once. Store assembled or store components separately for freshness.

### Tips & Variations

- Make a batch of bagels on Sunday for the entire week
- Can bake in regular oven at 350°F if you don't have an air fryer
- Store uncut bagels in airtight container for up to 5 days
- Freeze bagels for longer storage - toast directly from frozen
- Use whole egg instead of egg whites if not tracking calories closely
- Try different cheeses: cheddar, pepper jack, or feta
- Add tomato slices for extra flavor and nutrients
- Use flavored Greek yogurt for sweeter bagels (for breakfast without eggs)

### FAQ

1. **Do the bagels really taste like bagels?** They have a slightly different texture (more bread-like, less chewy) but are delicious and satisfying with way more protein.

2. **Can I use regular yogurt instead of Greek?** Greek yogurt is essential for the protein content and thick texture. Regular yogurt won't work.

3. **How do I meal prep these?** Either assemble fully and wrap individually, or keep components separate and assemble each morning for freshest results.
