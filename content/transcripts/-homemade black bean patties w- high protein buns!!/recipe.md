## Recipe Card

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 50 minutes
**Servings:** 4 burgers
**Calories:** Approximately 420-480 calories per burger (without toppings)

### Description
Never go back to store-bought veggie patties again! These homemade black bean patties are easy to make and delicious, paired with high-protein buns made from Greek yogurt and bread flour. A complete vegetarian burger experience that's healthier and tastier than store-bought options.

### Ingredients

**High Protein Buns:**
- 350g bread flour
- 350g Greek yogurt
- 1g baking powder
- Pinch of salt
- Black sesame seeds (optional, for topping)

**Black Bean Patties:**
- 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
- ¼ red onion
- ⅓ cup egg whites
- 1⅓ cup bread crumbs (panko recommended)
- 1 tbsp low sodium soy sauce

**For Assembly:**
- Pesto
- Shaved parmesan cheese
- Arugula
- Sliced onion
- Cooking spray

### Instructions

**For the High Protein Buns:**
1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. In a large bowl, combine bread flour, baking powder, and salt.
3. Add Greek yogurt to the flour mixture.
4. Mix until mostly combined, then use your hands to bring the dough together.
5. Knead until a smooth dough forms.
6. Cut the dough into 4 equal pieces.
7. Mold each piece into a bun shape.
8. Top with black sesame seeds if desired.
9. Bake at 350°F for 20 minutes.
10. Increase heat to 425°F and bake for another 10 minutes until the tops are slightly browned.

**For the Black Bean Patties:**
1. Drain and rinse black beans thoroughly.
2. Add black beans and red onion to a food processor.
3. Pulse until the onion and beans break up into chunks (stop and scrape sides as needed).
4. Add egg whites, bread crumbs, and soy sauce.
5. Pulse until the mixture is thick enough to pass the "upside down test" (holds together when turned upside down).
6. Place parchment paper on a cutting board (prevents sticking).
7. Form mixture into 4 decent-sized patties with your hands.
8. Heat a pan over low to medium heat and spray with cooking oil.
9. Cook patties covered for 3-4 minutes on each side until crispy and heated through.

**Assembly:**
1. Slice buns in half.
2. Spread pesto on the bottom bun.
3. Place black bean patty on top.
4. Add shaved parmesan, arugula, and sliced onion.
5. Top with the other half of the bun.
6. Serve with a side (roasted sweet potatoes recommended).

### About the Ingredients
**Greek Yogurt:** Replaces eggs and oil in traditional bread recipes, creating high-protein buns with a tender texture.

**Bread Flour:** Higher protein content than all-purpose flour, creates a chewier, more substantial bun.

**Black Beans:** The star of the patties - provides protein, fiber, and a meaty texture when processed.

**Egg Whites:** Acts as a binder to hold the patties together without adding fat from yolks.

**Panko Bread Crumbs:** Creates texture and helps absorb moisture for patties that hold together well.

### Tips & Variations
- Don't over-process the black beans - you want some texture, not a paste
- The mixture should be thick enough to hold together when you turn the bowl upside down
- Use parchment paper when forming patties to prevent sticking
- Cook patties covered on low-medium heat to ensure they cook through without burning
- Buns can be made ahead and frozen for quick burger assembly
- Customize toppings: tomatoes, lettuce, avocado, different cheeses
- Prep a batch of sweet potatoes at the beginning of the week for easy sides
- Sweet potatoes can be baked at 400°F for 50-60 minutes in oven or air fryer

### FAQ
1. **Why use bread flour instead of all-purpose flour?** Bread flour has more protein, which creates a chewier, more substantial bun that holds up better to burger toppings.

2. **Can I make the patties ahead of time?** Yes! Form the patties and refrigerate for up to 2 days, or freeze for up to a month.

3. **What's the "upside down test"?** If you can turn the bowl upside down and the mixture doesn't fall out, it's thick enough to form into patties.
