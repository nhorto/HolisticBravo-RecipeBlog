## Recipe Card

**Title:** Classic Shortbread Cookies
**Author:** Holistic Bravo

**Cook Time:** 12-15 minutes
**Total Time:** 1 hour 25 minutes
**Servings:** 24 cookies
**Calories:** Approximately 90 calories per cookie

### Description
These shortbread cookies turned out much better this time by taking time and letting the dough rest in the fridge for a whole hour. Perfect for sugar addicts who need something sweet at the end of the day - just a little something to signal to your body you're done eating for the day.

### Ingredients
- 1½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1½ sticks butter (room temperature)
- ½ cup powdered sugar

### Instructions
1. In a large bowl, combine room temperature butter and powdered sugar. Mix until well incorporated.
2. Add flour and continue mixing until a dough forms.
3. Shape the dough into a ball or disk.
4. Cover the dough and place in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour. This is crucial for the best texture.
5. Preheat oven to 325°F.
6. Remove dough from refrigerator and roll out on a floured surface to about ¼ inch thickness.
7. Cut into desired shapes using cookie cutters (gingerbread men work great!).
8. Place cookies on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
9. Bake at 325°F for 12-15 minutes until edges are just barely golden.
10. Remove from oven and let cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
11. Let cool completely before decorating or serving.

### About the Ingredients
True shortbread requires only three ingredients: butter, sugar, and flour. The high butter content creates that signature crumbly, melt-in-your-mouth texture. Room temperature butter is essential for proper mixing. Powdered sugar (rather than granulated) creates a finer, more delicate crumb. The chilling time allows the butter to firm up and the gluten to relax, resulting in tender cookies.

### Use Cases
Perfect for holiday baking, especially Christmas cookies. Great for gift-giving when decorated with icing. Ideal for afternoon tea or as an evening treat. Excellent base recipe for variations with different flavors. Perfect for anyone who needs a small sweet treat at the end of the day.

### Tips & Variations
- Don't skip the chilling time - it makes a huge difference in texture
- Room temperature butter is key - leave it out for 30-60 minutes before baking
- Don't overbake - cookies should be pale with just barely golden edges
- Roll dough between parchment paper to prevent sticking
- Add vanilla extract for classic vanilla shortbread
- Mix in lemon zest for citrus shortbread
- Dip cooled cookies in melted chocolate
- Add spices like cinnamon or ginger for spiced shortbread
- Press dough into a pan and cut into squares after baking for easier preparation
- Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks

### FAQ
1. **Why do I need to chill the dough?** Chilling firms up the butter, prevents spreading during baking, and allows the gluten to relax for a more tender cookie.
2. **Can I freeze the dough?** Yes! Shape into a log, wrap tightly, and freeze for up to 3 months. Slice and bake from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes to baking time.
3. **My cookies are too crumbly - what happened?** They may have been overbaked, or the dough wasn't mixed enough to bring the butter and flour together properly.
