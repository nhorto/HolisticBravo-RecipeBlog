## Recipe Card

**Title:** High Protein Pumpkin Chocolate Treats
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 0 minutes
**Total Time:** 40 minutes (including freeze time)
**Servings:** 8-10 treats
**Calories:** Approximately 80-120 calories per treat (depending on chocolate coating amount)

### Description
A 4-ingredient high-protein sweet treat perfect for Halloween or anytime you want a healthy dessert. The cottage cheese base tastes like pumpkin cheesecake and helps you hit your protein goals. These can be meal prepped for the week and stored in the freezer for whenever your sweet tooth strikes.

### Ingredients

**Base:**
- 1 cup cottage cheese
- ½ can (approximately ½ cup) 100% pumpkin puree
- 2 tbsp maple syrup

**Coating:**
- Chocolate (amount as desired for melting)
- Mini M&Ms (optional, for decoration)

### Instructions

1. Add cottage cheese to a blender and blend until completely smooth.
2. Add pumpkin puree and maple syrup to the blended cottage cheese.
3. Blend until well combined - the mixture should taste like pumpkin cheesecake.
4. Pour mixture into silicone molds or ice cube trays.
5. Place in freezer for approximately 30 minutes until firm.
6. Melt chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl or double boiler.
7. Remove frozen pumpkin treats from molds.
8. **Option 1:** Pour a small amount of chocolate on top of each treat for a light coating.
9. **Option 2:** Dip the entire treat into melted chocolate for a thick chocolate layer.
10. Add mini M&Ms or other decorations immediately after coating (optional).
11. Return to freezer to set the chocolate.
12. Store in freezer and enjoy throughout the week.

### About the Ingredients
**Cottage Cheese:** The protein powerhouse of this recipe. When blended smooth, it creates a creamy, cheesecake-like texture without the cottage cheese taste.

**Pumpkin Puree:** Make sure to use 100% pure pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling. Adds fall flavor, fiber, and nutrients.

**Maple Syrup:** Natural sweetener that complements the pumpkin flavor perfectly.

**Chocolate:** Your choice of dark, milk, or white chocolate for coating. The amount depends on whether you want a drizzle or full coating.

### Tips & Variations
- Blend the cottage cheese very smooth to eliminate any graininess
- The mixture tastes like pumpkin cheesecake before freezing
- Choose your chocolate coating level: light drizzle or full dip
- Pouring chocolate on top creates a thinner layer; dipping creates a thick shell
- Store in the freezer for best texture
- Make a batch at the beginning of the week for easy grab-and-go treats
- Experiment with different toppings: crushed nuts, coconut flakes, or sprinkles

### FAQ
1. **Can you taste the cottage cheese?** No! When blended smooth and combined with pumpkin and maple syrup, it tastes like pumpkin cheesecake.

2. **How long do these last in the freezer?** They can last several weeks in an airtight container in the freezer.

3. **Can I use Greek yogurt instead of cottage cheese?** You could, but cottage cheese provides more protein and creates a better texture when frozen.
