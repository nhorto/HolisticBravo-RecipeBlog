# 15-Minute Lazy Girl Pumpkin Gnocchi
Quick and easy homemade gnocchi made with leftover pumpkin and flour in a food processor - no kneading required! Ready in just 15 minutes with a simple garlic and olive oil sauce.

## Recipe Card

**Total Time:** 15 minutes
**Servings:** 1-2 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 400-450 calories per serving

### Description
This is the ultimate lazy dinner solution that doesn't compromise on taste. Using leftover pumpkin (or any vegetable "binder"), you can make fresh gnocchi in under 15 minutes with a food processor - no kneading, no rolling out, just cut and drop into boiling water. The simple sauce of olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper with reserved pasta water creates a silky coating. Top with parmesan and parsley for a restaurant-quality meal at home.

### Ingredients

**For the Gnocchi:**
- 162g pumpkin puree (about 1 cup)
- 175g all-purpose flour (about 1.5 cups)
- 1 teaspoon salt

**For the Sauce:**
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- 1 cup reserved pasta water (approximately)
- Fresh parmesan cheese, grated
- Fresh parsley, chopped

### Instructions

1. **Make the Gnocchi Dough:** Add pumpkin puree (162g), flour (175g), and salt (1 teaspoon) to a food processor. Pulse until mixture forms a cohesive ball of dough. The dough should be slightly springy to the touch.

2. **Boil Water:** Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil.

3. **Cut the Gnocchi:** Take the dough ball directly to the pot. Using a knife or bench scraper, cut small pieces of dough directly into the boiling water. No need to roll or shape - just cut and drop!

4. **Cook the Gnocchi:** The gnocchi will sink at first, then float to the surface when cooked (about 2-3 minutes). Use a slotted spoon to remove them. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water.

5. **Make the Sauce:** In a large pan, heat 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Sauté briefly until fragrant.

6. **Finish the Dish:** Add reserved pasta water to the garlic oil to create a light sauce. Add the cooked gnocchi and toss gently to coat. If sauce needs thickening, add 1 tablespoon corn starch or tapioca starch.

7. **Serve:** Top with fresh grated parmesan and chopped parsley.

### About the Ingredients

**Pumpkin Puree:** Acts as the "binder" for the gnocchi. Can use leftover baked pumpkin, canned pumpkin, or any similar vegetable puree.

**Flour Ratio:** Generally use slightly more flour than binder in grams. Don't need exact measurements - incorporate flour slowly until you get consistent, springy dough texture.

**Pasta Water:** The starchy water emulsifies with olive oil to create a silky sauce that coats the gnocchi perfectly.

### Tips & Variations

- **Other "Binders" to Try:** Sweet potato, regular potato, spinach, baked butternut squash
- **Measurements:** For cups: 1 cup binder to slightly more than 1 cup flour (doesn't have to be exact)
- **Texture Check:** Dough should be slightly springy to the touch - not sticky, not dry
- **Sauce Variations:** Add chili powder or smoked paprika for a kick
- **Thicker Sauce:** Add 1 tablespoon corn starch or tapioca starch to pasta water
- **Make it Creamy:** Use cashew milk for a creamier sauce similar to Alfredo
- **Storage:** Make extra and freeze on baking sheet before transferring to freezer bags

### FAQ

1. **Do I really not need to knead or roll out the dough?** Correct! The food processor does all the work, and you literally just cut pieces into the water.

2. **What if my dough is too sticky?** Add a bit more flour, one tablespoon at a time, until it reaches the right consistency.

3. **Can I use canned pumpkin?** Yes! Just make sure it's pure pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling with added sugars and spices.
