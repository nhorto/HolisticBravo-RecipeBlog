## Recipe Card

**Total Time:** 35 minutes
**Servings:** 4-6 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 180 calories per serving (crackers and hummus combined)

### Description
When you're craving a wholesome snack, these homemade crackers and hummus always hit the spot. Made with a combination of oat and all-purpose flour for better texture, these crackers come together quickly with minimal ingredients.

### Ingredients

**Rosemary Crackers:**
- ¾ cup oat flour (grind your own rolled oats!)
- ¾ cup all-purpose flour
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp fresh or dried rosemary
- A little less than ½ cup cold water (add slowly)

**Fresh Hummus:**
- 1 can chickpeas, drained
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp tahini
- 1 tsp sea salt

**Optional Toppings for Hummus:**
- Paprika
- Olive oil drizzle
- Fresh parsley

### Instructions

**For the Crackers:**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
2. In a bowl, combine oat flour, all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, and rosemary.
3. Add olive oil and mix until crumbly.
4. Slowly add cold water, a little at a time, mixing until a dough forms. Be careful not to over-saturate.
5. Roll out dough thinly on a floured surface.
6. Cut into desired cracker shapes or score into squares.
7. Transfer to a baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes until golden and crisp.
8. Let cool completely before serving.

**For the Hummus:**
1. Drain chickpeas and add to a food processor or blender.
2. Add olive oil, tahini, and sea salt.
3. Blend until smooth and creamy, adding a small amount of water if needed for desired consistency.
4. Transfer to a serving bowl.
5. Optional: Top with paprika, a drizzle of olive oil, and fresh parsley.
6. Serve with homemade crackers.

### About the Ingredients
Oat flour adds a wholesome texture and subtle nuttiness to the crackers. Pro tip: grind your own rolled oats for fresh oat flour at a fraction of the cost of pre-packaged! The combination with all-purpose flour creates the perfect crispy texture. Fresh rosemary elevates these simple crackers into something special. For the hummus, tahini is essential for authentic flavor and creamy texture.

### Use Cases
Perfect for afternoon snacks, party appetizers, or Mediterranean-themed meals. Great for meal prep - crackers stay crisp in an airtight container for days, and hummus keeps well in the fridge for up to a week. Ideal for packed lunches or healthy snacking at home.

### Tips & Variations
- Grind whole rolled oats in a food processor for fresh, inexpensive oat flour
- Roll the dough as thin as possible for extra-crispy crackers
- Experiment with different herbs: thyme, oregano, or za'atar
- Add garlic powder to the cracker dough for savory flavor
- For the hummus, add lemon juice for tanginess
- Try roasted red pepper or sun-dried tomatoes blended into the hummus
- Keep the dough cold for easier rolling and crisper crackers

### FAQ
1. **Can I use only all-purpose flour?** Yes, but the oat flour adds better texture and nutrition. If using only AP flour, use 1½ cups total.
2. **How do I store the crackers?** Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week. They should stay crispy if sealed properly.
3. **Can I make the hummus without tahini?** Tahini is traditional, but you can substitute with extra olive oil or a nut butter, though the flavor will differ.
