# Quick High-Protein Quinoa Bowl with Steamed Vegetables
A simple, healthy meal for when you don't feel like cooking but still need to hit your protein and calorie goals. Features quinoa, edamame, steamed bok choy and mushrooms, topped with eggs and a quick Asian-inspired sauce.

### Description
This is the perfect meal for when you wake up exhausted after a 12-hour shift and don't want to order takeout. It's simple, nutritious, and comes together in just 20 minutes. Quinoa and edamame provide plant-based protein, steamed vegetables add nutrients and volume, and eggs contribute additional protein. A quick homemade sauce with red wine vinegar, soy sauce, hot honey, and cilantro flakes brings everything together.

### Ingredients

**For the Bowl:**
- 1/2 cup quinoa (dry)
- Sea salt
- 1/2 cup edamame (frozen is fine)
- Bok choy (quantity to preference)
- Mushrooms (quantity to preference)
- 2 whole eggs
- Egg whites (for additional protein)

**For the Sauce:**
- Red wine vinegar
- Soy sauce
- Hot honey
- Cilantro flakes

### Instructions

1. **Cook the Quinoa:** In a pot, combine 1/2 cup quinoa with water and a pinch of sea salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 12-15 minutes until quinoa is cooked and fluffy.

2. **Cook the Edamame:** Add 1/2 cup edamame to the quinoa pot during the last 5 minutes of cooking, or cook separately according to package directions.

3. **Steam the Vegetables:** Wash bok choy and mushrooms. Place in a steaming pot with a little water. Cover and steam for 5-8 minutes until tender but still crisp.

4. **Make the Sauce:** In a small bowl, mix together red wine vinegar, soy sauce, hot honey, and cilantro flakes to taste. Set aside.

5. **Cook the Eggs:** Heat a pan over medium heat. Add 2 whole eggs and additional egg whites for extra protein. Cook to your preference (the recipe shows them with runny yolks).

6. **Use Your Free Time:** While everything cooks, clean up, load the dishwasher, vacuum, or do other quick tasks.

7. **Assemble the Bowl:** Place quinoa in a bowl (can use a small bowl to mold it for presentation). Add steamed vegetables around it. Top with cooked eggs.

8. **Serve:** Drizzle or serve the sauce on the side for dipping vegetables. Break that egg yolk and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients

**Quinoa:** A complete protein containing all nine essential amino acids. Provides sustained energy and helps keep you full.

**Edamame:** Young soybeans packed with plant-based protein and fiber, perfect for vegetarian protein goals.

**Bok Choy:** A nutrient-dense leafy green that steams quickly and provides vitamins A, C, and K.

**Hot Honey:** Adds a sweet-spicy element to the sauce that balances the salty soy sauce and acidic vinegar.

### Tips & Variations

- Steaming vegetables gives you free time to clean up or do other tasks
- Can substitute any vegetables you have - broccoli, snap peas, carrots all work
- Make extra quinoa and edamame for meal prep throughout the week
- Adjust egg whites amount based on your protein goals
- The sauce is your "favorite" - make a bigger batch and keep in fridge
- Can add tofu for even more protein
- Substitute tamari for soy sauce if gluten-free
- Regular honey works if you don't have hot honey

### FAQ

1. **Can I meal prep this?** Yes! Cook quinoa, edamame, and vegetables ahead. Store separately and reheat. Cook eggs fresh each day for best results.

2. **What if I don't have hot honey?** Mix regular honey with a pinch of red pepper flakes or sriracha.

3. **How do I make this even higher in protein?** Add more egg whites, extra edamame, or include baked tofu.
