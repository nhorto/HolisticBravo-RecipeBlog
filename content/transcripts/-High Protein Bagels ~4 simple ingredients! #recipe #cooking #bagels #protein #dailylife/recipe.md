## Recipe Card

**Title:** High Protein Bagels (Multiple Flavors)
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 16 minutes (8 min per side)
**Total Time:** 26 minutes
**Servings:** 2 bagels per batch
**Calories:** Approximately 180-220 calories per bagel (plain/everything), 250-280 calories per bagel (chocolate variation)

### Description
High-protein bagels that can be transformed into several different flavor combinations. The base is a simple 3-ingredient dough that you can customize with sweet or savory inclusions. Store uncooked bagels in the fridge for a week and pop them in the air fryer whenever you want a fresh, high-protein snack.

### Ingredients

**Base Recipe (makes 2 bagels):**
- 100g all-purpose flour
- 100g non-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt

**Matcha Cranberry Variation:**
- Base recipe
- 5g matcha powder
- 30g dried cranberries

**Double Chocolate Variation:**
- Base recipe
- 1 scoop chocolate protein powder
- ¾ cup mini chocolate chips
- Extra Greek yogurt (add 1 spoonful at a time until dough forms a solid ball)

**Everything Bagel Variation:**
- Base recipe
- Everything bagel seasoning (for topping)

### Instructions

**Base Dough:**
1. In a bowl, combine flour, Greek yogurt, baking powder, and salt.
2. Mix until a dough forms. It should be slightly sticky but workable.

**For Matcha Cranberry:**
1. Add matcha powder to the base dough and mix well.
2. Fold in dried cranberries.
3. Divide dough into 2 pieces.

**For Double Chocolate:**
1. Add chocolate protein powder to the base dough.
2. Mix in mini chocolate chips.
3. Add extra Greek yogurt 1 spoonful at a time until the mixture forms a solid ball (the protein powder will make it drier).
4. Divide dough into 2 pieces.

**For Everything Bagel:**
1. Make base dough with no additions.
2. Divide dough into 2 pieces.
3. Sprinkle everything bagel seasoning on top before air frying.

**Shaping & Cooking:**
1. Roll each piece of dough into a ball.
2. Poke a hole in the center with your finger and stretch gently to create a bagel shape.
3. Place in air fryer at 350°F.
4. Cook for 8 minutes on one side.
5. Flip and cook for 8 minutes on the other side.
6. Enjoy warm with butter and honey (for sweet versions) or your favorite toppings.

### About the Ingredients
**Greek Yogurt:** The protein star of this recipe. Non-fat Greek yogurt provides the protein boost while also acting as a binding agent that replaces eggs and traditional leavening.

**Flour:** All-purpose flour creates the bagel structure. The combination with Greek yogurt creates a unique "2-ingredient dough" base.

**Protein Powder:** For the chocolate version, adds even more protein. May require extra Greek yogurt to balance the dryness.

**Matcha Powder:** Antioxidant-rich green tea powder that adds a beautiful color and earthy flavor.

### Use Cases
- High-protein breakfast option
- Post-workout snack
- Meal prep friendly (store uncooked in fridge for up to a week)
- Customizable to sweet or savory preferences
- Satisfying alternative to regular bagels

### Tips & Variations
- Store uncooked bagels in the fridge for up to a week
- Air fry fresh whenever you want a warm bagel
- Best enjoyed warm
- The double chocolate version needs extra Greek yogurt due to the protein powder
- Serve sweet versions with butter and honey
- Serve savory versions with cream cheese, eggs, or your favorite toppings
- Get creative with inclusions: blueberries, cinnamon sugar, jalapeño cheddar, etc.
- If you don't have an air fryer, these can be baked in the oven at 350°F for about 20-25 minutes

### FAQ
1. **Can I store these?** Yes! Store uncooked bagels in the fridge for up to a week. Cook them fresh in the air fryer when ready to eat.

2. **Why does the chocolate version need more Greek yogurt?** The protein powder absorbs moisture, making the dough drier. Add yogurt a spoonful at a time until it comes together.

3. **Can I bake these instead of air frying?** Yes, bake at 350°F for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.
