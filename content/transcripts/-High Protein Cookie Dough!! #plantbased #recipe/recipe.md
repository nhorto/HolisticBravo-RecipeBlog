### Description
A quick, plant-based, high-protein cookie dough that's vegan, non-dairy, and completely safe to eat (no risk of salmonella!). Perfect for when you need a sweet pick-me-up after a long day. Takes only 5 minutes to make and you don't have to share it with anyone!

### Ingredients

- 100g almond flour
- 50g PB powder (or regular peanut butter, almond butter, or cashew butter)
- Water (add gradually)
- 1 tsp monk fruit sweetener (optional)
- Chocolate chips (about 1 heaping tbsp, or to taste)

### Instructions

1. In a bowl, combine almond flour and PB powder.
2. In a separate small bowl, add water to the PB powder a little at a time, mixing until creamy but still THICK.
3. Add the PB mixture to the almond flour.
4. Mix well until combined into a cookie dough consistency.
5. Add monk fruit sweetener if desired and mix.
6. Fold in chocolate chips.
7. Enjoy immediately! No baking required.

### About the Ingredients
**Almond Flour:** Provides healthy fats, protein, and a slightly nutty flavor. Creates the cookie dough texture.

**PB Powder:** Lower in calories and fat than regular peanut butter while maintaining protein content. Great for macros. Can substitute with regular nut butters if preferred.

**Monk Fruit Sweetener:** Optional zero-calorie natural sweetener. You can adjust sweetness to your preference or omit entirely.

**Chocolate Chips:** The classic cookie dough addition. Add as much or as little as you like - it's your sweet treat!

### Tips & Variations
- Mix PB powder with water separately before adding to almond flour for smoother consistency
- Keep the PB mixture thick - don't add too much water
- Use regular peanut butter, almond butter, or cashew butter if you prefer (just note the macros will be different)
- Add your favorite inclusions: candy pieces, sprinkles, M&Ms, etc.
- The recipe is completely customizable - make it how you like it!
- No sharing required (unless your dog wants some, but chocolate is toxic to dogs!)
- Store any leftovers in the fridge for a firmer texture

### FAQ
1. **Is this safe to eat raw?** Yes! There are no eggs or raw flour (almond flour is safe to eat uncooked), so there's no risk of salmonella.

2. **Can I use regular peanut butter instead of PB powder?** Absolutely! The recipe creator just prefers PB Fit powder for the macros (lower calorie/fat). Use whatever nut butter you like.

3. **How do I know how much water to add?** Add water gradually to the PB powder, mixing until it's creamy but still thick. It should be peanut butter consistency, not runny.
