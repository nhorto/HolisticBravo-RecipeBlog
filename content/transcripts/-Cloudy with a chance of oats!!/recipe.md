### Description
This oatmeal is the perfect cozy food when you're not feeling your best or just need something warm and comforting. Made with a low liquid-to-oat ratio for a chunky, thick consistency that fills you up and magically makes you feel better.

### Ingredients

**Base:**
- 1 cup rolled oats
- 1 cup water (or milk/milk substitute for creamier texture)
- Pinch of sea salt

**Toppings:**
- Frozen wild blueberries
- Sliced almonds
- Creamy peanut butter (generous amount)
- Agave nectar (for sweetness)

### Instructions
1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil in a pan over medium heat.
2. Add 1 cup of rolled oats to the boiling water.
3. Add a pinch of sea salt.
4. Stir constantly to prevent oats from sticking together.
5. Cook on medium to low heat until liquid is fully absorbed and oats reach desired consistency (chunky and thick).
6. Transfer oatmeal to a bowl.
7. Top with frozen wild blueberries.
8. Add sliced almonds.
9. Add a generous amount of creamy peanut butter.
10. Drizzle with agave nectar for sweetness.
11. Enjoy with your coffee!

### About the Ingredients
Rolled oats are whole grain and provide sustained energy, fiber, and essential nutrients. Using a 1:1 ratio of liquid to oats creates a thick, chunky texture - if you prefer it runnier, add more liquid. Frozen blueberries provide antioxidants and create a nice temperature contrast. Peanut butter adds protein, healthy fats, and incredible flavor. Sliced almonds provide crunch and additional healthy fats. Agave nectar offers natural sweetness.

### Tips & Variations
- For runnier oatmeal: Increase liquid ratio (1.5-2 cups liquid per 1 cup oats)
- For even chunkier texture: Use steel-cut oats
- For smoother consistency: Grind oats into flour before cooking
- Use milk or non-dairy milk instead of water for creamier oatmeal
- Try different nut butters (almond, cashew, sunflower seed)
- Swap agave for honey, maple syrup, or brown sugar
- Add cinnamon or vanilla extract while cooking
- Top with banana slices, chia seeds, or coconut flakes
- Make overnight oats version by soaking oats in milk overnight

### FAQ
1. **Can I use instant oats?** Yes, but they'll cook much faster (1-2 minutes) and won't have the same chunky texture. Rolled oats or steel-cut are best for this recipe.
2. **Why use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?** Frozen blueberries are available year-round, often more affordable, and create a nice temperature contrast with hot oatmeal. Plus, they're flash-frozen at peak ripeness.
3. **How can I make this higher in protein?** Add a scoop of protein powder to the cooked oats, increase peanut butter amount, or stir in Greek yogurt after cooking.
