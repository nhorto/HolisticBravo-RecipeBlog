### Description
A game-changing pasta recipe that uses tofu as a substitute for eggs, creating a high-protein, vegan-friendly pasta. Perfect for vegans who miss the protein boost that eggs provide in traditional pasta. The tofu acts as both a protein source and a binder for the flour, and you can't taste it at all once it's covered with your favorite sauce.

### Ingredients

**Pasta:**
- 200g flour
- Half a block of firm tofu

**Simple Sauce (as shown in video):**
- Minced garlic
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Olive oil
- Baby arugula and spinach
- Reserved pasta water

### Instructions

**For the Pasta:**
1. Drain the tofu and add to a large bowl.
2. Use your hands to break up and incorporate the tofu until it's well mashed.
3. Add 200g of flour to the tofu.
4. Continue using your hands to incorporate the flour and tofu together until a dough forms.
5. Knead until the dough comes together into a smooth ball.
6. Use a pasta mat or rolling pin to roll out the dough.
7. Cut into your desired pasta shape (fettuccine, spaghetti, etc.).
8. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil.
9. Cook the pasta for 3-5 minutes until al dente.
10. Reserve some pasta water before draining.

**For the Simple Sauce:**
1. While pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a pan.
2. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
3. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Add baby arugula and spinach.
5. Add cooked pasta to the pan with a splash of reserved pasta water.
6. Toss everything together until well combined.

### About the Ingredients
**Firm Tofu:** Replaces eggs as the protein source and binder in traditional pasta. Once cooked and sauced, you won't taste the tofu at all. Provides plant-based protein.

**Flour:** Creates the pasta structure. You can use all-purpose flour or experiment with other types.

**Reserved Pasta Water:** The starchy water helps bind the sauce to the pasta and creates a silky texture.

### Tips & Variations
- Use firm tofu for best results - extra firm may be too dry, soft tofu too wet
- Drain the tofu well before using
- The pasta dough should feel similar to traditional pasta dough
- You can't taste the tofu once it's cooked and sauced
- Sauce it however you like - marinara, alfredo, pesto, or simple garlic and oil
- Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried pasta (3-5 minutes)
- Reserve pasta water before draining - it's essential for sauce consistency
- This pasta can be made ahead and stored in the fridge or frozen for later use

### FAQ
1. **Can you taste the tofu in the pasta?** No! Once cooked and covered with sauce, you can't taste the tofu at all.

2. **What type of tofu should I use?** Firm tofu works best. It has enough moisture to bind the flour but isn't too wet.

3. **How long does homemade tofu pasta last?** Fresh pasta can be stored in the fridge for 2-3 days or frozen for up to a month.
