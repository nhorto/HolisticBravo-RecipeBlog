**Calories:** Approximately 340 calories per serving

### Description
We don't waste food in this household! This recipe shows you how to transform leftover pasta scraps (from making ravioli or other pasta projects) into a completely new, delicious meal. Anything you add to homemade pasta will enhance its flavors, and you can never go wrong with the combination of crispy mushrooms and pesto.

### Ingredients
- Leftover pasta (ravioli scraps, colored pasta pieces, or any pasta shapes)
- Package of oyster mushrooms (or mushrooms of choice)
- Minced garlic (to taste)
- Leftover pesto sauce (or fresh pesto)
- Pasta water (reserved, starchy)
- Salt (for pasta water)
- Olive oil (for frying mushrooms)

### Instructions
1. **Prepare the pasta water:** Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Salt it generously until your arm hurts - seriously salt it well!
2. **Cook the mushrooms:** Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and let them fry until nice and crispy.
3. **Add garlic:** Once mushrooms are crispy, add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
4. **Cook the pasta:** Add leftover pasta pieces to the boiling salted water. Cook until they float or are al dente.
5. **Make the sauce:** Add leftover pesto to the mushroom pan. Add some starchy pasta water to thin the pesto and create a sauce.
6. **Reserve pasta water:** Before draining, reserve at least 1 cup of the starchy pasta water.
7. **Combine:** Transfer cooked pasta directly to the pan with mushrooms and pesto.
8. **Toss and adjust:** Toss everything together, adding more pasta water as needed to create a glossy, cohesive sauce.
9. **Serve:** Plate immediately while hot and enjoy your "second dinner"!

### About the Ingredients
This recipe celebrates the zero-waste philosophy of using every scrap from pasta-making. Oyster mushrooms have a delicate, slightly sweet flavor and become wonderfully crispy when fried. The starchy pasta water is crucial - it helps the pesto cling to the pasta and creates a silky sauce. Homemade pasta scraps may look irregular, but they taste incredible.

### Use Cases
Perfect for when you've made fresh pasta and have leftover scraps or dough pieces. Great way to use up the odd-shaped pieces from ravioli-making or pasta trimming. Ideal for a quick weeknight meal using leftovers from a previous pasta project. Shows that "second dinner" can be even better than the first!

### Tips & Variations
- Works with any pasta scraps - ravioli edges, colored pasta trimmings, irregular shapes
- Any mushroom variety works: oyster, cremini, shiitake, or button mushrooms
- Don't crowd the pan when frying mushrooms - they need space to crisp up
- Make sure to salt the pasta water generously for maximum flavor
- If you don't have leftover pesto, use fresh pesto or even pesto from a jar
- Add red pepper flakes for heat
- Top with freshly grated Parmesan cheese
- Add baby spinach or arugula for extra greens
- Finish with a drizzle of good olive oil
- Can substitute other herbs like basil if no pesto available

### FAQ
1. **Can I use store-bought pasta instead of scraps?** Yes! Any pasta shape will work. Just cook according to package directions.
2. **What if I don't have leftover pesto?** Use jarred pesto, make fresh pesto, or substitute with olive oil, garlic, and Parmesan.
3. **Why is pasta water so important?** The starch in pasta water helps emulsify the sauce and makes it cling to the pasta better, creating a cohesive dish instead of dry pasta with separated sauce.
