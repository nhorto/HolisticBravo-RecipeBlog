### Description
This powerhouse breakfast proves that vegetarians can easily hit their protein goals. Creamy chocolate protein oatmeal is made by blending oats into flour and cooking with plant-based chocolate protein powder, then topped with frozen raspberries, sliced almonds, hemp seeds, and honey. Pair with spicy eggs for a complete, satisfying breakfast that keeps you full for hours.

### Ingredients

**For the Chocolate Protein Oatmeal:**
- 1/2 cup oat flour (or whole oats blended into flour)
- 1 cup water
- 1 scoop plant-based chocolate protein powder (Momentous brand recommended)
- Frozen raspberries
- Sliced almonds
- Hemp seeds
- Honey, to taste

**For the Spicy Eggs:**
- 2-3 eggs
- Spices/hot sauce to taste

### Instructions

1. **Prepare Oat Flour:** If using whole oats, blend them in a blender until they reach a flour consistency. You can also keep them whole if you prefer a chunkier texture.

2. **Make the Oatmeal:** In a pot, combine 1/2 cup oat flour with 1 cup water. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently.

3. **Add Protein Powder:** Once the oatmeal is cooked and creamy, stir in 1 scoop of plant-based chocolate protein powder. Mix well until fully incorporated.

4. **Top the Oatmeal:** Transfer to a bowl and top with frozen raspberries, sliced almonds, hemp seeds, and drizzle with honey.

5. **Prepare Spicy Eggs:** Cook eggs to your preference and season with spices or hot sauce for a kick.

6. **Serve:** Enjoy the chocolate oatmeal alongside the spicy eggs for a complete high-protein breakfast.

### About the Ingredients

**Oat Flour:** Blending oats into flour creates a smoother, creamier porridge texture that's almost like pudding. You can also use whole oats for more texture.

**Plant-Based Chocolate Protein Powder:** Momentous brand is highlighted as excellent. Provides significant protein boost while creating a dessert-like chocolate flavor.

**Hemp Seeds:** Packed with protein, omega-3s, and minerals. Adds a subtle nutty flavor and extra nutrition.

**Frozen Raspberries:** Provide antioxidants, fiber, and a tart contrast to the sweet chocolate oatmeal.

### Tips & Variations

- Blend oats for smoother texture, or leave whole for chunkier porridge
- Can substitute any plant-based protein powder flavor
- Top with any berries - blueberries, strawberries, or mixed berries work great
- Add chia seeds or flax seeds for extra omega-3s
- Can make it sweeter with maple syrup instead of honey
- Substitute regular dairy milk for water for even creamier results
- Make overnight version by soaking oats with protein powder overnight

### FAQ

1. **Can I use regular oats instead of oat flour?** Yes! The recipe mentions you can keep the oats whole if you prefer a chunkier texture.

2. **What if I don't have plant-based protein powder?** You can use any chocolate protein powder, though the protein count may vary.

3. **How do I get 53g of protein total?** The combination of oatmeal with protein powder, hemp seeds, almonds, and the eggs together reaches 53g.
