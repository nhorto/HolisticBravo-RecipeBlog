## Recipe Card

**Title:** Homemade McGriddle-Style Breakfast Sandwich

**Total Time:** 15 minutes
**Servings:** 2 sandwiches
**Calories:** Approximately 350-400 calories per sandwich

### Description
A homemade version of McDonald's McGriddle that takes less than 15 minutes from start to finish. Sweet pancake "buns" surround savory eggs and cheese for that perfect sweet-and-savory breakfast combination. So good you might forget to take a picture before taking a bite!

### Ingredients

**Pancake Buns:**
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 whole egg
- 1 tbsp monk fruit sweetener (or sugar)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Pinch of salt
- Water or milk of choice (add 1 tbsp at a time)

**Filling:**
- Eggs (1-2 per sandwich)
- Cheese slices

**Equipment:**
- Round silicone molds or egg rings

### Instructions

**For the Pancake Buns:**
1. In a bowl, combine flour, baking powder, monk fruit sweetener, and salt.
2. Add the whole egg and olive oil.
3. Add water or milk 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing until you reach a thick batter consistency (thicker than regular pancake batter).

**Cooking:**
1. Heat a pan over medium to low heat.
2. Place silicone molds or egg rings in the pan.
3. Pour pancake batter into the molds to create the "buns."
4. In separate molds, crack eggs for the filling.
5. Cover the pan to help the eggs and pancakes cook evenly.
6. Optional: Add a splash of water to the pan and re-cover to create steam - this helps the eggs set quicker and makes flipping easier.
7. Cook for 3-4 minutes on one side.
8. Carefully flip and cook for another 3-4 minutes on the other side.
9. Remove from molds.

**Assembly:**
1. Place one pancake "bun" on a plate.
2. Add a slice of cheese.
3. Top with the cooked egg.
4. Add another slice of cheese if desired.
5. Top with the second pancake "bun."
6. Enjoy immediately while warm!

### About the Ingredients
**Thick Pancake Batter:** The key is making the batter thicker than regular pancakes so it holds together as a "bun" and doesn't fall apart.

**Monk Fruit Sweetener:** Zero-calorie natural sweetener that provides sweetness without sugar. Can substitute with regular sugar.

**Eggs:** The protein-packed savory component that balances the sweet pancakes.

**Molds:** Silicone molds or egg rings ensure uniform circular shapes that look like the real thing.

### Tips & Variations
- Keep the batter on the thicker side so the pancakes hold up as buns
- Use molds or egg rings to get perfect circular shapes
- The steam technique (adding water and covering) helps eggs set faster and makes flipping cleaner
- Cook on medium to low heat to prevent burning
- From start to finish, this takes less than 15 minutes!
- Future variation idea: Use chocolate chip pancakes as the "buns" for an extra sweet version
- Add bacon, sausage, or veggie options for different protein choices
- Can meal prep the pancake buns and reheat when ready to assemble

### FAQ
1. **How do I get the pancakes to hold together like buns?** Make the batter thicker than regular pancake batter and use molds to create uniform shapes.

2. **What if I don't have silicone molds?** Use metal egg rings or even mason jar lids as makeshift molds.

3. **Can I make these ahead?** The pancake buns can be made ahead and stored in the fridge. Reheat and add fresh eggs and cheese when ready to eat.
