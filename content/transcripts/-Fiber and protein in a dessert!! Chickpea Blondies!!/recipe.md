## Recipe Card

**Title:** Chickpea Blondies with Aquafaba Whipped Topping
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20-25 minutes
**Total Time:** 35 minutes
**Servings:** 8-10 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 150-180 calories per serving

### Description
These chickpea blondies are a game-changer for healthy desserts! Using a whole can of chickpeas as the base, they're packed with fiber and protein. The best part? You can't taste the chickpeas at all! Top them with fluffy aquafaba whipped cream made from the chickpea water for a complete plant-based treat.

### Ingredients

**Blondie Base:**
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained (reserve the liquid)
- ½ cup maple syrup
- ⅓ cup nut butter of choice (or less than ⅓ cup PB powder + water)
- 1 tbsp vanilla extract
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp baking soda
- ½ tsp salt
- ⅓ cup almond flour
- Mini chocolate chips (optional topping)

**Aquafaba Whipped Topping:**
- Liquid from the can of chickpeas (aquafaba)
- 1 tsp lemon juice or vinegar
- 4 tsp powdered sugar (adjust to taste)

### Instructions

**For the Blondies:**
1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. Drain chickpeas using a strainer, reserving the chickpea water (aquafaba) for later.
3. Add drained chickpeas to a food processor or blender.
4. Add maple syrup, nut butter (or PB powder with water), vanilla extract, salt, baking powder, and baking soda to the food processor.
5. Blend until completely smooth - you will not taste the chickpeas.
6. Add almond flour and combine with all other ingredients until well mixed.
7. Pour batter into a prepared baking dish.
8. Top with mini chocolate chips if desired.
9. Bake at 350°F for 20-25 minutes.

**For the Aquafaba Whipped Topping:**
1. While blondies are baking, add the reserved aquafaba to a mixing bowl.
2. Add lemon juice or vinegar (helps form stronger peaks).
3. Whip the mixture alone until it begins to turn fluffy (about 5 minutes).
4. Gradually incorporate powdered sugar, 1 tsp at a time, whipping gently to get stiff peaks.
5. Store in the freezer for best results.

**To Serve:**
1. Let blondies cool slightly.
2. Cut into squares.
3. Top with aquafaba whipped cream.

### About the Ingredients
**Chickpeas:** The star ingredient provides fiber, protein, and a creamy texture when blended. You won't taste them at all in the final product.

**Aquafaba:** The magical chickpea water whips up into a fluffy, luxurious cream that's perfect for pancakes, hot chocolate, or as a topping for desserts.

**Maple Syrup:** Natural sweetener that adds moisture and sweetness without refined sugar.

**Nut Butter/PB Powder:** Adds protein and binds the mixture. PB powder is a lower-calorie option.

**Almond Flour:** Adds texture and helps bind the blondies while keeping them gluten-friendly.

### Tips & Variations
- If using regular peanut butter or cashew/almond butter instead of PB powder, use a full ⅓ cup
- The aquafaba whipped topping can be used on pancakes or in hot chocolate
- Store whipped topping in the freezer for best texture
- Don't skip the lemon juice/vinegar in the whipped topping - it helps create stronger peaks
- You can adjust the sweetness of the whipped topping by adding more or less powdered sugar
- Mini chocolate chips are optional but add great texture
- Store blondies in airtight container for up to 5 days

### FAQ
1. **Can you really not taste the chickpeas?** No! The maple syrup, vanilla, and nut butter completely mask the chickpea flavor. They just add texture and nutrition.
2. **How long does the aquafaba whipped topping last?** Store it in the freezer for best results. It can last several days to a week frozen.
3. **Can I use a different type of flour?** The recipe calls for almond flour specifically, but you could experiment with other nut flours or oat flour.
