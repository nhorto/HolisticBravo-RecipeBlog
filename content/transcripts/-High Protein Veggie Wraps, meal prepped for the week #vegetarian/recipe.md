**Servings:** 4-6 wraps (meal prep for the week)
**Calories:** Approximately 380-450 calories per wrap

### Description
Weekly meal prep made easy with these high-protein veggie wraps featuring Mediterranean flavors. Packed with tofu and black beans for protein, sundried tomatoes, kalamata olives, and feta for flavor, these wraps are baked to perfection and can be enjoyed throughout the week.

### Ingredients

**Protein Base:**
- 1 entire block of tofu
- 1 entire can of black beans
- Seasonings to taste

**Mediterranean Toppings:**
- Sundried tomatoes
- Kalamata olives
- Pepperoncini
- Feta cheese

**Spreads:**
- Red pepper hummus
- Pesto

**Wraps:**
- Large tortillas or wraps

### Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Drain and press tofu to remove excess moisture.
3. Drain and rinse black beans.
4. Crumble or cube the tofu and season to taste.
5. Season the black beans.
6. Lay out tortillas and spread red pepper hummus on one wrap and pesto on another (alternate or choose your favorite).
7. Layer the seasoned tofu and black beans on each wrap.
8. Add sundried tomatoes, kalamata olives, pepperoncini, and feta cheese.
9. Fold or roll the wraps securely.
10. Place wrapped tortillas on a baking sheet.
11. Bake at 425°F for 20 minutes until wraps are crispy and golden.
12. Let cool slightly before cutting.
13. Store in the refrigerator for meal prep throughout the week.

### About the Ingredients
**Tofu:** An entire block provides substantial plant-based protein. Season well for maximum flavor.

**Black Beans:** Adds fiber, protein, and a hearty texture. Pairs perfectly with Mediterranean flavors.

**Mediterranean Vegetables:** Sundried tomatoes, kalamata olives, and pepperoncini add briny, savory flavors and healthy fats.

**Feta Cheese:** Provides saltiness, creaminess, and additional protein.

**Red Pepper Hummus & Pesto:** Both serve as flavorful, protein-rich spreads that add moisture and bind the ingredients.

### Use Cases
- Weekly meal prep lunches
- Post-workout high-protein meal
- Vegetarian dinner option
- Grab-and-go breakfast
- Picnic or potluck contribution

### Tips & Variations
- Press the tofu well to remove moisture for better texture
- Season the tofu and black beans generously - this is where the flavor comes from
- You can use different spreads: regular hummus, tahini, tzatziki, or guacamole
- Experiment with different vegetables: roasted red peppers, spinach, artichokes
- Store wrapped individually for easy grab-and-go meals
- Can be eaten cold or reheated
- Baking the wraps makes them crispy and helps everything hold together
- Make a batch on Sunday for lunches throughout the week

### FAQ
1. **How long do these last in the fridge?** Up to 5 days when stored properly in airtight containers or wrapped individually.

2. **Can I eat these cold?** Yes! They're delicious both cold and reheated.

3. **Can I freeze these?** Yes, wrap individually and freeze for up to a month. Thaw in the fridge overnight before eating.
