### Description
This is pasta-making at its simplest and most accessible. With just 100g of flour and 50g of water, you can make fresh homemade pasta without expensive machines or complicated ingredients. Using basic kitchen tools (or even just your hands), you'll create beautiful hand-rolled pasta shapes that cook in minutes. Perfect for beginners who want to dip their toes into pasta-making without intimidation.

### Ingredients

**For the Pasta:**
- 100g all-purpose flour
- 50g water
- Extra ingredient: Love!

**For Serving (Optional Simple Sauce):**
- Starchy pasta water
- Parmesan cheese
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Store-bought or homemade sauce of choice

### Instructions

1. **Mix the Dough:** Combine 100g flour and 50g water in a bowl. Mix together using a simple fork until it starts to come together.

2. **Incorporate with Bench Scraper:** Use a bench scraper (or knife) to help incorporate the mixture more. Scrape and fold the dough together.

3. **Knead by Hand:** Use your hands to knead everything together. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes to get everything nicely incorporated. Don't over-complicate it!

4. **Rest the Dough:** Let the dough rest while you set up your work area (at least 5-10 minutes helps).

5. **Divide the Dough:** Cut your dough ball into 4 equal pieces.

6. **Roll Out:** Take one piece and roll it out with your hands into a long rope/log shape.

7. **Cut Pieces:** Using your bench scraper, cut the rope into small pieces (about 1-2 inches each).

8. **Shape the Pasta:** Take your gnocchi board (or use a fork). Place a piece of dough on it and use your thumb to roll it down, creating grooves. Alternatively, use the edge of your bench scraper to roll pieces up quickly.

9. **Boil the Pasta:** Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook for 2-3 minutes until they float.

10. **Serve:** Toss with store-bought sauce or make your own simple sauce with starchy pasta water, parmesan, salt, and pepper.

### About the Ingredients

**Flour and Water Only:** This creates a vegan pasta that's accessible to everyone. The lack of eggs makes it more shelf-stable and easier to work with for beginners.

**Bench Scraper:** A versatile tool that helps with mixing, cutting, and shaping. Only costs about $10 on Amazon with a silicone mat.

**Gnocchi Board:** A ridged board that creates grooves in pasta to help sauce cling. Not required - you can use a fork instead.

- Don't over-complicate it - this is meant to be simple!
- Can serve with any sauce - store-bought is perfectly fine
- Make your own simple sauce with just pasta water and parmesan
- Double or triple the recipe for meal prep
- This same technique works with different shapes

### FAQ

1. **Do I really not need eggs?** Correct! This is a traditional water-based pasta that's completely vegan and works beautifully.

2. **What if I don't have a gnocchi board?** Use the back of a fork to create ridges, or skip it entirely - the pasta will still taste great!

3. **Can I make this ahead of time?** Yes! Let shaped pasta dry on a floured surface for 30 minutes, then store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days.
