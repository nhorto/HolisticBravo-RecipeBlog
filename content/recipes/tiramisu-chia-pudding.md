FIELD: tips
========================================
- I always let the pudding set for at least 6-8 hours so the chia seeds fully absorb the liquid and create that thick, creamy texture
- Don't worry when the cocoa powder rises to the top after mixing - this is completely normal and it will settle as the pudding sets overnight
- I always give it a good stir about 10-15 minutes after putting it in the fridge to prevent the chia seeds from clumping at the bottom
- I use high-quality baker's white chocolate for the best flavor - cheap white chocolate chips can taste waxy and I notice the difference every time
- I like to store mine in individual mason jars or small containers for easy grab-and-go mornings
- If you prefer a thicker pudding, add an extra teaspoon of chia seeds; for a thinner consistency, I splash in a bit more almond milk
- I save the final dusting of cocoa powder for right before serving so it stays fresh and vibrant on top
- I've also tried swapping almond milk for oat milk or coconut milk for a slightly different flavor profile - both work great

FIELD: ingredientsOverview
========================================
The foundation of this pudding starts with 1 cup of almond milk, which keeps things light and low-calorie while providing the liquid the chia seeds need to expand and gel overnight. I use unsweetened almond milk since the white chocolate and monk fruit handle the sweetness, but any plant-based milk you love will do the job.

You'll need 1 tablespoon of chia seeds, which might not sound like much, but these tiny seeds absorb up to 12 times their weight in liquid. That's what creates the pudding-like texture without any cooking. I think chia seeds are seriously underrated - they're loaded with omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and plant-based protein, which means they slow digestion and help keep you full longer, preventing those mid-morning energy crashes.

The 2 pieces of baker's white chocolate get melted down and stirred into the almond milk mixture, adding a silky richness that makes this pudding feel genuinely indulgent. I never skimp on the white chocolate here - it's what elevates this from "chia seeds in milk" to something that actually tastes like tiramisu. A teaspoon of monk fruit sweetener rounds out the sweetness without adding any calories or spiking your blood sugar. Finally, my favorite part is the cocoa powder pulling double duty: 1 teaspoon gets mixed into the base for that deep chocolate flavor, and a second teaspoon gets dusted on top right before serving for presentation and an extra hit of cocoa.

FIELD: instructionsOverview
========================================
I start by melting 2 pieces of baker's white chocolate. You can do this in a small microwave-safe bowl in 15-second intervals, stirring between each one, until the chocolate is completely smooth and liquid. Alternatively, use a double boiler on the stovetop if you prefer. I find that white chocolate can seize up and turn grainy if it gets too hot, so be careful not to overheat it. You want a smooth, pourable consistency.

Pour 1 cup of almond milk into a jar or container, then add the melted white chocolate and stir until it's fully incorporated. The warmth of the chocolate will blend easily into the milk. Next, I add 1 teaspoon of monk fruit sweetener and 1 tablespoon of chia seeds. I give everything a thorough stir to make sure the chia seeds are evenly distributed and not clumping together at the bottom.

Add 1 teaspoon of cocoa powder to the mixture and stir it in. Don't be alarmed if the powder rises to the top initially - this is completely normal with cocoa. I just mix it in as best I can. It will continue to settle and integrate as the pudding sets in the fridge.

Cover the container and place it in the refrigerator. Here's a tip I swear by: come back in about 10-15 minutes and give it one more stir. This helps redistribute the chia seeds, which tend to settle and clump during the first few minutes. After that second stir, I leave it alone and let it work its magic overnight, or for at least 6-8 hours.

When you're ready to enjoy your tiramisu chia pudding, give it a gentle stir to check the consistency - it should be thick, creamy, and pudding-like. I dust the remaining 1 teaspoon of cocoa powder over the top for that classic tiramisu look and an extra burst of chocolate flavor. Grab a spoon and dig in.

FIELD: faq
========================================
**Can I use regular sugar instead of monk fruit sweetener?**
Absolutely. I use monk fruit here because it adds sweetness without calories and doesn't spike blood sugar, which is great for steady energy. But regular sugar, honey, maple syrup, or agave all work perfectly fine. Just add to taste, keeping in mind that the white chocolate already brings some sweetness to the mix.

**How long does this keep in the fridge?**
I find this pudding stores beautifully for up to 5 days in the refrigerator, making it ideal for meal prep. Just keep it in an airtight container or sealed jar. The texture may thicken slightly over time as the chia seeds continue to absorb liquid, so I splash in a bit of almond milk and stir before eating if it gets too thick.

**Can I use dark chocolate instead of white chocolate?**
You can, and it will create a deeper, more intense chocolate flavor - closer to a chocolate mousse vibe than tiramisu. I prefer white chocolate because it gives that signature creamy, vanilla-like sweetness that's closer to traditional tiramisu, but dark chocolate is a delicious alternative if that's what you have on hand.

**What if my chia seeds clump together?**
This usually happens when the seeds aren't stirred well enough in the first few minutes. The fix is simple: I stir thoroughly when I first combine everything, then come back 10-15 minutes later and stir again before leaving it overnight. If they've already clumped, break up the clumps with a fork and add a splash more almond milk.

**Is this recipe good for post-workout recovery?**
I think it's a great option. The carbs from the white chocolate and cocoa help restore glycogen after training, the chia seeds support hydration since they absorb and retain liquid, and the healthy fats provide sustained energy. It's gentle on digestion too, which is exactly what I want after a tough workout.

FIELD: recipeCardIngredients
========================================
- 1 cup almond milk
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 2 pieces baker's white chocolate
- 1 tsp monk fruit sweetener
- 1 tsp cocoa powder (mixed in)
- 1 tsp cocoa powder (topping)

========================================
FIELD: recipeCardInstructions
========================================
1. Melt 2 pieces of white chocolate until smooth.
2. Combine almond milk, melted white chocolate, monk fruit sweetener, and chia seeds in a jar and stir well.
3. Add 1 tsp cocoa powder and mix (powder may rise to top initially).
4. Refrigerate overnight or at least 6-8 hours, stirring once after 10-15 minutes.
5. Dust with remaining 1 tsp cocoa powder before serving.
