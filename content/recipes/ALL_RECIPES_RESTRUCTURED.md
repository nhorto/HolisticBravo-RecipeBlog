# Two-Ingredient Chocolate Protein Cosmic Muffins

**Created:** February 9, 2026
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

These incredibly simple high-protein muffins require just egg whites and protein powder, making them perfect for a quick, healthy dessert or snack with minimal calories.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

When Holistic Bravo shared these cosmic muffins, the internet went wild - and for good reason. Sometimes the best recipes are the simplest ones, and these two-ingredient protein muffins prove exactly that. With just whipped egg whites and your favorite protein powder, you can create fluffy, cloud-like muffins that deliver serious protein without the guilt.

What makes these truly special is how light and airy they are. By whipping the egg whites to stiff peaks and gently folding in protein powder, you're essentially creating a protein-packed meringue that bakes into the most satisfying little treats. At only 50 calories per muffin, you can enjoy all five and still come in under 250 calories total. Life is all about balance, which is why Holistic Bravo adds a few dark chocolate M&Ms halfway through baking - because sometimes you need a little something extra.

These are perfect for meal prep, post-workout snacks, or when you want something sweet but don't want to derail your goals. They're vegetarian, naturally gluten-free, and take just 15 minutes from start to finish.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Only 2 base ingredients needed - egg whites and protein powder
- Incredibly low calorie at just 50 calories per muffin
- High protein content keeps you satisfied and supports muscle recovery
- Ready in just 15 minutes with minimal cleanup
- Naturally gluten-free and can be made with plant-based protein powder
- Light, fluffy, cloud-like texture that's unlike any other muffin
- Perfect for meal prep - make a batch and enjoy throughout the week

## Tips

- Make sure egg whites are at room temperature for best whipping results
- Whip the egg whites until they form stiff peaks - this is crucial for the fluffy texture
- Fold in the protein powder gently to preserve all those air bubbles
- Don't overmix when adding the protein powder - gentle folds are key
- The optional dark chocolate M&Ms are added halfway through baking so they don't sink
- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days
- These taste great cold straight from the fridge or at room temperature

## Ingredients

The magic of this recipe is in its simplicity. Egg whites are pure protein with virtually no fat or carbohydrates, making them ideal for high-protein, low-calorie baking. When whipped to stiff peaks, they create an airy, cloud-like texture that's absolutely heavenly. You'll need 200 grams of egg whites, which you can buy pre-separated in cartons at most grocery stores for convenience.

The protein powder adds both flavor and a serious protein boost while keeping the recipe vegetarian-friendly. Holistic Bravo uses Momentous plant-based chocolate protein powder, but any protein powder you love will work beautifully - whey, casein, or plant-based all create delicious results. Try vanilla, strawberry, or peanut butter flavored protein for variety.

The dark chocolate M&Ms are completely optional but highly recommended. They provide a small indulgence and hit of chocolate that makes these feel like a real dessert rather than just a protein snack. If you want to keep them even healthier, swap for sugar-free chocolate chips.

## Instructions

1. Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and preparing your muffin tin with liners or non-stick spray.

2. Add 200 grams of egg whites to a large, clean mixing bowl. Make sure the bowl is completely free of any grease or oil, as this can prevent the whites from whipping properly. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, whip the egg whites on high speed until they form stiff peaks. This usually takes 3-5 minutes. You'll know they're ready when you can lift the beaters and the peaks stand straight up without drooping.

3. Once you have beautiful stiff peaks, it's time to add the protein powder. Add one scoop of your favorite chocolate protein powder to the whipped egg whites. Using a rubber spatula, gently fold the protein powder into the egg whites. Use a gentle folding motion from the bottom of the bowl up and over - you want to preserve as much of the air you just whipped in as possible. The mixture should be light and fluffy, not deflated.

4. Divide the mixture evenly among 5 muffin cups. The batter will be very light and airy, almost like a mousse.

5. Bake at 350°F for 5 minutes, then remove from the oven. If you're using dark chocolate M&Ms or chocolate chips, add them to the tops of the muffins now. This prevents them from sinking to the bottom and ensures they stay on top for that pretty presentation.

6. Return the muffins to the oven and bake for another 5 minutes, for a total baking time of 10 minutes. The muffins should be lightly golden and set to the touch.

7. Let cool slightly in the pan, then transfer to a wire rack. These are delicious warm or cold!

## FAQ

**Can I use whole eggs instead of just egg whites?**
You can, but it will significantly increase the calorie and fat content. The beauty of this recipe is that egg whites keep these ultra-low calorie while still packing in the protein. Whole eggs would also make them denser and less cloud-like.

**What if I don't have a muffin pan?**
No problem! You can make this as one large "muffin" or cake in a small baking dish. Just adjust the cooking time as needed, checking for doneness around the 15-minute mark.

**Do I need to use plant-based protein powder?**
Absolutely not. Any protein powder works beautifully - whey, casein, or plant-based. Choose your favorite brand and flavor. Just know that different protein powders may slightly affect the texture and taste.

**Why are mine deflating after baking?**
This can happen if the egg whites weren't whipped to stiff enough peaks or if you overmixed when folding in the protein powder. Make sure to whip until stiff peaks form and fold very gently.

# High-Protein Vegetarian Breakfast Bowl

**Updated:** February 9, 2026

A powerhouse breakfast featuring homemade chocolate chip protein bagels and a loaded omelet, delivering 87g of protein to keep you full for 6+ hours.

These aren't your typical bagels - they're packed with Greek yogurt and protein powder, creating a dense, satisfying bread that's loaded with protein. Paired with a hearty three-egg omelet topped with feta cheese and creamy avocado, this breakfast delivers an impressive 87 grams of protein.

This is the kind of breakfast you eat when you have a long day ahead and don't want to think about food again for hours. It's perfect for those who train hard, work demanding jobs, or simply want to start their day feeling completely satisfied. Holistic Bravo swears by this meal, noting that after eating it, food doesn't even cross her mind for six hours.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Delivers an impressive 87g of protein from vegetarian sources only
- Keeps you full and satisfied for 6+ hours
- Features homemade protein bagels that are surprisingly easy to make

## Tips

- Make the bagels ahead and freeze for quick breakfasts throughout the week
- Air fryer cooking times may vary by model - check at 15 minutes to avoid overcooking
- Don't skip flipping the bagels halfway through for even cooking
- Room temperature ingredients mix together more easily for the bagel dough
- Add vegetables to your omelet for extra nutrients and fiber
- Cook the omelet on medium-low heat for the creamiest, most tender eggs
- If you don't have an air fryer, these bagels bake beautifully in a regular oven

## Ingredients

This recipe is divided into two components: the chocolate chip protein bagels and the loaded omelet.

For the bagels, you'll use a combination of bread flour and Greek yogurt as the base. The Greek yogurt not only provides protein but also creates a chewy, satisfying texture without any yeast or rising time needed. One scoop of protein powder boosts the protein content significantly - use any flavor you like, though vanilla or unflavored works especially well with the chocolate chips. The baking soda and salt help the bagels rise and develop flavor. Mini chocolate chips add sweetness and make these feel like a treat rather than just health food.

The omelet starts with three whole eggs plus 50 grams of egg whites for extra protein without additional fat. A chunk of feta cheese adds salty, tangy flavor and even more protein. Half an avocado provides healthy fats that help keep you satiated longer and round out the meal with creamy richness. A sprinkle of sea salt brings all the flavors together.

## Instructions

**Making the Chocolate Chip Protein Bagels:**

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 140 grams of bread flour, 180 grams of Greek yogurt, one scoop of protein powder, 1 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix everything together until it forms a cohesive dough. The mixture will be thick and slightly sticky - this is perfect.

2. Fold in 2 tablespoons of mini chocolate chips throughout the dough, distributing them evenly.

3. Divide the dough into two portions and shape each into a bagel form. You can either roll the dough into a rope and connect the ends, or form it into a ball and poke a hole through the center, stretching it slightly to create the bagel shape.

4. Preheat your air fryer to 350°F. Place the bagels in the air fryer basket, making sure they're not touching.

5. Cook for 10 minutes on one side, then carefully flip the bagels over and cook for another 10 minutes on the other side. The total cooking time is 20 minutes. The bagels should be golden brown and feel firm to the touch when done.

6. Remove from the air fryer and let cool slightly while you prepare the omelet. You can also make these ahead and store them in the fridge or freezer.

**Making the Loaded Omelet:**

1. In a bowl, whisk together 3 whole eggs and 50 grams of egg whites until well combined and slightly frothy.

2. Crumble a chunk of feta cheese into the egg mixture. The amount is up to you - use as much as you like.

3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter or cooking spray. Pour in the egg mixture.

4. Cook the omelet, gently lifting the edges occasionally to let uncooked egg flow underneath. When the eggs are mostly set but still slightly wet on top, fold the omelet in half.

5. Slide onto a plate and top with half an avocado, sliced. Sprinkle with sea salt.

6. Serve alongside your warm protein bagel and enjoy this protein-packed feast!

## FAQ

**Can I make these bagels without an air fryer?**
Yes! Bake in a regular oven at 350°F for approximately 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through. Keep an eye on them and bake until golden brown.

**How long can I store the bagels?**
Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days, or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat in the toaster or air fryer when ready to eat.

**Can I prep this breakfast ahead?**
Absolutely! Make the bagels in advance and just cook the omelet fresh in the morning. The entire meal prep takes less than 5 minutes if your bagels are already made.

**Can I substitute the ingredients in the bagel recipe?**
You can experiment, but the combination of bread flour and Greek yogurt is key to the texture. The protein powder can be any flavor, and you can omit or swap the chocolate chips for other mix-ins like blueberries.

# Chocolate Chip Pumpkin Muffins

**Created:** February 9, 2026
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Delicious fall-inspired muffins made with pure pumpkin puree, wholesome flours, and chocolate chips - all mixed in one bowl for easy cleanup.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

When fall rolls around, there's something magical about baking with pumpkin. These chocolate chip pumpkin muffins from Holistic Bravo capture all the cozy vibes of autumn in the most delicious, wholesome way possible. What makes them extra special is how incredibly easy they are to make - you only need one large bowl, which means minimal cleanup and maximum enjoyment.

These muffins are made with a combination of all-purpose flour and oat flour, creating a texture that's both tender and hearty. Pure pumpkin puree adds moisture, fiber, and that classic fall flavor we all crave, while pumpkin pie spice fills your kitchen with the most amazing aroma. The mini chocolate chips scattered throughout make every bite a little celebration.

Holistic Bravo has a great tip for making these even easier: invest in a food scale. She swears by it, noting that you can get one for as little as $10, and it will save you from cleaning up all those measuring cups and tablespoons. Trust me, she says, it makes cooking more enjoyable. These muffins bake at a high temperature of 425°F, which creates a beautiful domed top while keeping the inside perfectly moist.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- One-bowl recipe means easy cleanup and less time in the kitchen
- Perfect balance of whole grain nutrition and indulgent chocolate
- Uses real pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling
- Optional egg whites add extra protein without changing the flavor
- High baking temperature creates a beautiful bakery-style dome
- Naturally lower in sugar with only 2 tablespoons maple syrup for the entire batch
- Perfect for meal prep - they keep well and freeze beautifully
- Combines the best of fall flavors with chocolate chip goodness

## Tips

- Invest in a food scale ($10) to make measuring easier and reduce cleanup
- If you don't have oat flour, simply blend rolled oats in a blender until fine
- Make sure you're using pure pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling (which has added sugar and spices)
- The high oven temperature (425°F) is intentional - it creates a nice dome on top
- Add chocolate chips "until it looks right to you" - trust your instincts
- Store in an airtight container at room temperature for 3 days or refrigerate for up to a week
- These freeze beautifully for up to 3 months - thaw at room temperature or warm in the microwave
- Use a non-stick muffin pan if you have one to skip the liners

## Ingredients

The beauty of these muffins is in the combination of wholesome ingredients that work together to create something truly special. You'll use both all-purpose flour (150g) and oat flour (70g) to create the perfect texture - the all-purpose flour provides structure while the oat flour adds whole grain nutrition and a subtle nutty flavor. If you don't have oat flour, just pulse rolled oats in a blender until they're finely ground.

Pure pumpkin puree is the star here, providing moisture, fiber, and that quintessential fall flavor while keeping these muffins naturally sweet. Make sure you're using pure pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling, which has added sugar and spices you don't need. One can of pure pumpkin is exactly what you need.

The leavening comes from baking soda (just 1g, or about 1/4 teaspoon), while salt and pumpkin pie spice (a full tablespoon!) bring all the warm, cozy flavors of cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves. Mini chocolate chips get folded in "until you know it's enough" - Holistic Bravo never measures them, and neither should you.

For the wet ingredients, one whole egg provides structure and richness, while a tablespoon of olive oil adds moisture. Just 2 tablespoons of maple syrup sweeten the entire batch - the pumpkin and chocolate chips provide plenty of natural sweetness on their own. Optional egg whites (50g) add extra protein without changing the flavor, but you can substitute with another whole egg if you prefer. A teaspoon of vanilla extract rounds everything out with warm, sweet notes.

## Instructions

1. Start by preheating your oven to 425°F - yes, that's hot, but it's the secret to those beautiful bakery-style domed tops.

2. In your large mixing bowl, combine all the dry ingredients first. Add 150g all-purpose flour, 70g oat flour, 1g (about 1/4 teaspoon) baking soda, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice. Whisk these together to distribute the leavening and spices evenly.

3. Add your mini chocolate chips to the dry mixture. Don't measure - just add them until it looks right to you. This is one of those recipes where you can trust your instincts. Mix the chocolate chips into the flour mixture so they get coated, which will help prevent them from sinking to the bottom of the muffins.

4. Now add all your wet ingredients directly to the same bowl. Add 1 whole egg, one can of pure pumpkin puree, 1 tablespoon olive oil, 2 tablespoons maple syrup, 50g egg whites (or another whole egg), and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract.

5. Using a wooden spoon or rubber spatula, mix everything together until just combined. The batter will be thick but pourable. Don't overmix - a few lumps are totally fine and actually preferred. Overmixing can make the muffins tough.

6. Prepare your muffin pan. If you have a non-stick muffin pan, you don't need to do anything. Otherwise, use paper liners or spray with non-stick cooking spray.

7. Divide the batter evenly among 12 muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 to 3/4 full. An ice cream scoop works great for this and ensures even portioning.

8. Bake at 425°F for 20-25 minutes, until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs. The tops should be beautifully domed and golden brown.

9. Let the muffins cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy warm or at room temperature!

## FAQ

**Can I make these without oat flour?**
Yes, you can use all all-purpose flour instead (220g total). You'll lose some of the whole grain nutrition and the subtle nutty flavor, but they'll still be delicious.

**Why bake at such a high temperature?**
The high heat (425°F) creates a beautiful dome on top and sets the exterior quickly while keeping the inside moist and tender. It's a professional bakery trick that really works!

**Can I reduce the sugar?**
These are already lightly sweetened with just 2 tablespoons of maple syrup for the entire batch of 12 muffins. The pumpkin and chocolate chips provide natural sweetness. You could reduce it slightly, but they may not be quite as sweet.

**What's the difference between pumpkin puree and pumpkin pie filling?**
Pumpkin puree is just cooked, pureed pumpkin with nothing added. Pumpkin pie filling has sugar, spices, and other ingredients already mixed in. You want the plain puree for this recipe.

The base is non-fat Greek yogurt, which is incredibly high in protein and creates a creamy, mousse-like texture that's absolutely heavenly. Mix in a scoop of chocolate protein powder and some mini chocolate chips, and you have yourself a dessert that packs 35-40 grams of protein. It's the kind of treat you can eat guilt-free, knowing you're actually fueling your body with quality protein.

What makes this even better is the versatility. You can eat it immediately as a mousse, or pop it in the freezer for a firmer, ice cream-like consistency. ## Ingredients

This recipe is beautifully simple with just three main ingredients. The base is 170 grams of non-fat Greek yogurt, which is a protein powerhouse. Greek yogurt is strained to remove most of the whey, leaving behind a thick, creamy yogurt that's packed with protein - typically around 15-20 grams per 170-gram serving. Non-fat means you get all that protein without the added fat and calories.

One scoop of chocolate protein powder adds both flavor and boosts the protein content even further. Holistic Bravo uses Momentous chocolate protein powder, which is plant-based, but you can use any protein powder you love. Whey protein, casein, or plant-based all work beautifully. Different brands and types will slightly affect the taste and texture, so experiment to find your favorite. Vanilla, peanut butter, and cookies and cream flavors also work wonderfully in this recipe.

Two tablespoons of mini chocolate chips provide texture and satisfy that chocolate craving with just a small amount. Mini chips distribute more evenly throughout the mixture than regular-sized chips, giving you chocolate in every bite. You can use regular chocolate chips if that's what you have, or swap for sugar-free chocolate chips to keep the sugar content even lower.

## Instructions

1. Grab a bowl or mug - any container works, though a mug makes for easy eating. Add 170 grams of non-fat Greek yogurt. If you have a kitchen scale, weigh it out. If not, it's about 3/4 cup.

2. Add one scoop of chocolate protein powder directly to the yogurt. The amount will vary depending on your protein powder brand, but it's typically around 25-30 grams.

3. Mix the yogurt and protein powder together thoroughly. Use a spoon or fork and really work it until the mixture is completely smooth and the protein powder is fully incorporated. This usually takes about a minute of good stirring. You don't want any protein powder clumps - it should look like chocolate mousse.

4. Fold in 2 tablespoons of mini chocolate chips, distributing them throughout the mixture. These add little pockets of chocolate heaven and make it feel like a real dessert.

5. At this point, you can either eat it immediately as a mousse-like treat, or place it in the freezer for 30-60 minutes for a firmer, ice cream-like texture. The choice is yours!

6. If you froze it, let it sit at room temperature for a minute or two before eating if it's too hard. Enjoy your high-protein chocolate treat that tastes indulgent but supports your goals!

## FAQ

**Can I use regular yogurt instead of Greek yogurt?**
Greek yogurt is much higher in protein (about double) and has a thicker, creamier consistency that works perfectly for this recipe. Regular yogurt will be too thin and watery, and you'll get significantly less protein. Stick with Greek yogurt for best results.

**How long does this keep in the fridge?**
Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The texture and flavor are best when fresh, but it will keep for a few days. The chocolate chips may lose some of their crispness over time as they absorb moisture.

**Is this actually filling?**
Yes! The high protein content from both the Greek yogurt and protein powder is very satisfying and helps curb sweet cravings. Many people find that this completely satisfies their dessert craving while keeping them full.

**Can I make this with flavored Greek yogurt?**
You can, but plain non-fat Greek yogurt is recommended because the protein powder and chocolate chips provide plenty of flavor. Flavored yogurts often have added sugar that you don't need.

## Tips

- Add water to the peanut butter powder slowly - it's easier to add more than to fix it if it's too wet
- The mixture should be pliable like cookie dough, not too sticky or too dry
- Use quality dark chocolate (70% cacao or higher) for best flavor and health benefits
- Coconut oil helps the chocolate melt smoothly and creates a nice shiny coating
- Sprinkle sea salt on top before the chocolate hardens for the best flavor
- Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks or freeze for up to 3 months
- Make mini balls for portion control or larger ones for more substantial snacks
- Try different nut butter powders like almond or cashew for variety

## Ingredients

The star of these truffles is peanut butter powder (also sold as PB powder or powdered peanut butter). You'll need 1 cup of the powder, which gets mixed with water to form a dough. Peanut butter powder is made by pressing most of the oil out of roasted peanuts, then grinding what's left into a fine powder. This process removes about 85% of the fat while keeping all the protein and peanut flavor, making it perfect for healthy treats. Popular brands include PB2, PBfit, and others available in most grocery stores.

You'll add 1/4 to 1/3 cup of water to the powder, but do this gradually. Start with 1/4 cup and add more as needed until you get a pliable, dough-like consistency. Different brands of peanut butter powder absorb water differently, so it's important to add it slowly.

For the chocolate coating, use 1 cup of dark chocolate mini morsels. Mini chips melt more evenly than large chunks. Dark chocolate (at least 70% cacao) provides a rich, sophisticated flavor and contains beneficial antioxidants. One teaspoon of coconut oil gets mixed with the chocolate to help it melt smoothly and creates a thinner coating that's easier to work with. The coconut oil also adds a subtle sheen to the finished truffles.

Optional sea salt for sprinkling on top is highly recommended - it cuts through the sweetness and makes the chocolate and peanut butter flavors pop.

## Instructions

1. In a medium mixing bowl, add 1 cup of peanut butter powder. Start adding water gradually, about a tablespoon at a time. Mix with a fork or spoon after each addition. You're looking for a consistency that's similar to cookie dough - pliable and easy to roll, but not too sticky. This usually takes between 1/4 and 1/3 cup of water, but the exact amount depends on your brand of peanut butter powder.

2. Once your peanut butter mixture has the right consistency, use your hands to roll it into balls. The size is up to you - make them about 1 inch in diameter for bite-sized truffles. Place the balls on a plate or baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

3. Put the plate of peanut butter balls in the refrigerator to firm up while you prepare the chocolate coating. This takes about 10-15 minutes and makes them easier to coat.

4. While the peanut butter balls are chilling, make the chocolate coating. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine 1 cup of dark chocolate mini morsels with 1 teaspoon of coconut oil. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring thoroughly between each interval. This usually takes 2-3 intervals (1.5-2 minutes total). Stir until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth. The coconut oil will help thin the chocolate slightly, making it perfect for coating.

5. Remove the peanut butter balls from the refrigerator. Working with one ball at a time, drop it into the melted chocolate. Use a fork to roll it around until it's completely coated, then lift it out with the fork, letting excess chocolate drip back into the bowl.

6. Place the chocolate-coated ball back on the parchment-lined plate. If you're adding sea salt (highly recommended!), sprinkle a tiny pinch on top of each ball immediately after coating, before the chocolate sets.

7. Once all balls are coated, return the plate to the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the chocolate to set completely. Store in the refrigerator in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks, or freeze for up to 3 months.

Coconut oil helps the chocolate melt smoothly and creates a thinner coating that's easier to work with. It also gives the chocolate a beautiful sheen once it hardens. Without it, the chocolate may be too thick to coat the balls evenly.

**Do I have to add sea salt?**
No, it's completely optional, but the salt really enhances the chocolate and peanut butter flavors. It creates that sweet-salty combination that's so addictive. Just a tiny pinch on each truffle makes a big difference.

**Can I make these with other nut butter powders?**
Absolutely! Almond butter powder, cashew butter powder, or any other nut butter powder will work. The process and ratios stay the same.

# Bang Bang Brussels Sprouts

**Updated:** February 9, 2026

## Introduction

If you think you don't like Brussels sprouts, you haven't tried these bang bang Brussels sprouts yet. Holistic Bravo has created a recipe that packs a punch in the best way possible, transforming these often-maligned vegetables into something truly crave-worthy. The combination of crispy, caramelized Brussels sprouts with a creamy, sweet, and spicy bang bang sauce is absolutely addictive.

The magic happens when you roast or air fry Brussels sprouts until they're golden brown and crispy on the outside. This high-heat cooking method brings out their natural sweetness and eliminates any bitterness. Then they get tossed in a homemade bang bang sauce that's a perfect balance of creamy vegan mayo, sweet chili sauce, sriracha heat, and a touch of agave nectar.

What makes this recipe even better is how inclusive it is - it works for vegan, vegetarian, and paleo diets (just use compliant ingredients). Whether you're serving these at a dinner party, bringing them to a potluck, or just making an impressive side dish for dinner, these Brussels sprouts will convert even the biggest skeptics. They're easy to throw together and guaranteed to disappear fast.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Transforms Brussels sprouts into something truly crave-worthy
- Crispy exterior with tender inside creates the perfect texture

## Tips

- Cut Brussels sprouts evenly for consistent cooking
- Don't overcrowd the pan - Brussels sprouts need space to get crispy
- Air fryer method creates extra crispy results with less oil

- Toss with sauce right before serving to maintain maximum crispiness
- Add sesame seeds and green onions for garnish and extra flavor
- For paleo version, use paleo-friendly mayo and sweetener
- Double the sauce recipe if you love really saucy vegetables

## Ingredients

You'll need 1 pound of Brussels sprouts, which is typically one large bag or container. Choose Brussels sprouts that are firm, bright green, and feel heavy for their size. You'll trim the ends and cut them in half (or quarter them if they're very large). Cutting them in half exposes more surface area to heat, creating more crispy, caramelized edges.

For roasting, use 2 tablespoons of olive oil or avocado oil. Avocado oil has a higher smoke point, making it ideal for high-heat cooking, but olive oil works beautifully too. Season with 1/2 teaspoon of salt and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper.

The bang bang sauce is where the magic happens. It starts with 1/3 cup of vegan mayonnaise (or regular mayo if you're not vegan). This creates the creamy base that coats the Brussels sprouts beautifully. Add 2 tablespoons of sweet chili sauce for that sweet-spicy kick - you can find this in the Asian foods aisle of most grocery stores.

For heat, use 1-2 teaspoons of sriracha, adjusting to your spice tolerance. One tablespoon of agave nectar or honey balances the heat with sweetness. One teaspoon of rice vinegar adds tanginess and helps thin the sauce slightly. Optional garnishes of sesame seeds and sliced green onions take these from homemade to restaurant-quality.

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C), or if using an air fryer, set it to 375°F. The high heat is essential for getting those crispy, caramelized Brussels sprouts.

2. Prepare your Brussels sprouts by trimming off the stem ends and removing any yellow or damaged outer leaves. Cut each sprout in half from top to bottom. If you have any particularly large sprouts, quarter those so everything cooks evenly.

3. In a large bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with 2 tablespoons of oil, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper. Make sure every sprout is coated with oil and seasoning.

4. **For oven roasting:** Spread the Brussels sprouts in a single layer on a baking sheet, cut side down. This is important - you want them to make direct contact with the hot pan to get crispy. Don't overcrowd them or they'll steam instead of roast. Roast for 20-25 minutes, flipping them halfway through, until they're crispy, golden brown, and caramelized in spots.

   **For air fryer:** Place Brussels sprouts in the air fryer basket in a single layer (you may need to work in batches). Air fry at 375°F for 15-18 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through to ensure even cooking.

5. While the Brussels sprouts are cooking, make your bang bang sauce. In a medium bowl, whisk together 1/3 cup vegan mayo, 2 tablespoons sweet chili sauce, 1-2 teaspoons sriracha (adjust to your spice preference), 1 tablespoon agave nectar, and 1 teaspoon rice vinegar. Whisk until smooth and well combined. Taste and adjust seasonings if needed - add more sriracha for heat or more agave for sweetness.

6. When the Brussels sprouts are done - they should be crispy on the outside and tender inside with lots of caramelized, golden-brown edges - transfer them to a large bowl.

7. Add the bang bang sauce and toss until all the Brussels sprouts are evenly coated. Work quickly so the Brussels sprouts stay as crispy as possible.

8. Transfer to a serving dish and garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions if using. Serve immediately while still crispy and hot!

## FAQ

**Can I make these ahead?**
You can prep and roast the Brussels sprouts ahead, but add the sauce just before serving. If you sauce them too early, they'll lose their crispy texture as they sit. Reheat the roasted Brussels sprouts in a hot oven or air fryer before saucing.

**What exactly is bang bang sauce?**
Bang bang sauce is typically a combination of mayonnaise, sweet chili sauce, sriracha or hot sauce, and a sweetener. It originated as a sauce for fried shrimp but works amazingly well on vegetables. The name comes from the flavor "bang" or punch it delivers.

**Are these actually spicy?**
The spice level is completely adjustable based on how much sriracha or hot sauce you add to the bang bang sauce. Start with 1 teaspoon and add more if you want it spicier. The sweet chili sauce and agave help balance the heat.

**Can I use frozen Brussels sprouts?**
Fresh Brussels sprouts work best for this recipe because they get crispier. Frozen Brussels sprouts contain more moisture and are harder to get crispy. If you must use frozen, thaw and pat them very dry before roasting.

## Ingredients

The base of these balls is 1-2 sweet potatoes, depending on their size. You want enough to make a good amount of mashed sweet potato - probably around 1 to 1.5 cups once mashed. Sweet potatoes provide natural sweetness, fiber, and a beautiful orange color, plus they're loaded with vitamin A.

The key ingredient is 30 grams of tapioca flour, also called tapioca starch. This comes from the cassava root, which is called yuca in Puerto Rico and other Latin American countries. Tapioca flour is naturally gluten-free and creates that distinctive chewy, mochi-like texture when cooked. You can find it in the gluten-free or international foods section of most grocery stores.

You'll also need 10 grams of corn starch, which helps bind the mixture and contributes to the crispy exterior. For sweetness, use 20 grams of sugar - Holistic Bravo uses monk fruit zero-calorie sweetener to keep these as low-calorie as possible, but regular sugar, coconut sugar, or any sweetener you prefer will work. One teaspoon of salt balances the sweetness and enhances all the flavors. Finally, you'll need cooking spray for making the exterior crispy in the air fryer.

## Instructions

1. Start by steaming your sweet potatoes. You can do this in a steamer basket, in the microwave, or by boiling them. Cook until they're completely soft and tender - a fork should slide through easily with no resistance. This usually takes 15-20 minutes depending on the size of your sweet potatoes.

2. Once the sweet potatoes are cooked, let them cool slightly, then peel and mash them thoroughly. You want a smooth, lump-free consistency. Holistic Bravo does this in the video and it comes together beautifully.

3. To your mashed sweet potato, add 30g tapioca flour, 10g corn starch, 20g sugar (or monk fruit sweetener), and 1 teaspoon of salt.

4. Now here's where it gets fun - use your hands to incorporate all the ingredients together. The mixture comes together very quickly, like within a minute or two. You'll feel it transform from separate ingredients into a cohesive dough that's pliable and smooth.

5. Form the mixture into ping pong-sized balls. Try to make them all roughly the same size so they cook evenly. You should get about 15-20 balls depending on how large you make them.

6. Spray each ball with cooking spray on all sides. This is important for getting that crispy exterior.

7. Place the balls in your air fryer basket, making sure they're not touching. You may need to work in batches depending on the size of your air fryer.

8. Air fry for 10 minutes on one side. After 10 minutes, carefully flip each ball over and spray the other side with cooking spray.

9. Cook for another 10 minutes on the second side, for a total cooking time of 20 minutes.

10. Remove from the air fryer and let cool for a few minutes. The outside should be crispy and golden, while the inside is soft and mochi-like. Enjoy the amazing texture!

You absolutely could! Holistic Bravo admits to eating the entire plate. Since each ball is only about 20 calories, eating 15-20 balls would only be 300-400 calories total. That's pretty reasonable for a treat!

**What's the difference between cassava and yuca?**
They're the same thing! Cassava is the English name, and yuca is the Spanish name. It's a starchy root vegetable commonly used in Latin American, African, and Asian cuisines. Tapioca flour is made from this root.

# Chickpea Blondies with Aquafaba Whipped Topping

It whips up into the most fluffy, luxurious cream that's perfect for topping these blondies, adding to pancakes, or stirring into hot chocolate. Holistic Bravo is obsessed with how creamy and delicious it is. This is one of those recipes that proves healthy desserts don't have to be a compromise - they can be just as good, if not better, than traditional treats.

## Ingredients

The star of the blondie base is one 15-ounce can of chickpeas. Make sure to drain them using a strainer, but save that chickpea water (aquafaba) - you'll need it for the whipped topping! The chickpeas provide all the structure, moisture, fiber, and protein in these blondies.

Half a cup of maple syrup sweetens the blondies naturally without refined sugar. It also adds moisture and that characteristic maple flavor that works so well in desserts. For the fat and additional protein, use 1/3 cup of nut butter - peanut, almond, or cashew butter all work beautifully. Holistic Bravo uses peanut butter powder for a lower-calorie option; if you're using PB powder, use less than 1/3 cup and add water to reconstitute it.

One tablespoon of vanilla extract adds warmth and helps mask any bean flavor (though you won't taste it anyway). The leavening comes from 1 teaspoon each of baking powder and baking soda, which help these blondies rise slightly and develop a nice texture. Half a teaspoon of salt balances the sweetness. Finally, 1/3 cup of almond flour helps bind everything together and adds a subtle nutty flavor. Optional mini chocolate chips on top make these extra special.

For the aquafaba whipped topping, you'll use the liquid from the can of chickpeas plus 1 teaspoon of lemon juice or vinegar (which helps form stronger peaks) and 4 teaspoons of powdered sugar (adjust to your taste preference).

## Instructions

**Making the Chickpea Blondies:**

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F and prepare a baking dish by greasing it or lining it with parchment paper.

2. Open your can of chickpeas over a strainer set over a bowl. This way you catch the chickpea water (aquafaba) for later. Rinse the chickpeas well under running water.

3. Add the drained, rinsed chickpeas to your food processor or high-powered blender.

4. Add 1/2 cup maple syrup, 1/3 cup nut butter (or less than 1/3 cup PB powder mixed with water), 1 tablespoon vanilla extract, 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon baking powder, and 1 teaspoon baking soda to the food processor.

5. Blend everything together until completely smooth. Holistic Bravo promises you will not taste the chickpeas once everything is blended. Scrape down the sides as needed and keep blending until there are no chunks.

6. Add 1/3 cup almond flour and pulse or blend until just combined with all the other ingredients. The batter will be thick but pourable.

7. Pour the batter into your prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.

8. If using mini chocolate chips, sprinkle them over the top of the batter now.

9. Bake at 350°F for 20-25 minutes, until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out mostly clean with just a few moist crumbs. Don't overbake - these are best when slightly fudgy.

**Making the Aquafaba Whipped Topping:**

1. While the blondies are baking, make your whipped topping. Add the reserved aquafaba (chickpea water) to a clean, dry mixing bowl. Make sure there's no grease or oil in the bowl.

2. Add 1 teaspoon of lemon juice or vinegar to the aquafaba. This is important - it helps form stronger, more stable peaks.

3. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, whip the aquafaba on high speed for about 5 minutes. At first it will seem like nothing is happening, but keep going. It will start to turn foamy, then eventually fluffy and white like whipped cream.

4. Once the aquafaba is fluffy and has soft peaks, start adding the powdered sugar gradually. Add about 1 teaspoon at a time, whipping gently after each addition. Holistic Bravo used 4 teaspoons total, but you can adjust to your preferred sweetness.

5. Continue whipping until you get stiff peaks - the topping should stand up when you lift the beaters and not droop over.

6. Transfer the whipped topping to a container and store in the freezer for best results. It will keep for several days to a week.

**To Serve:**

1. Let the blondies cool slightly in the pan, then cut into squares.

2. Top each square with a generous dollop of the aquafaba whipped cream.

3. Enjoy this chickpea-based dessert that's packed with fiber and protein!

**How long does the aquafaba whipped topping last?**

**Can I use a different type of flour?**
The recipe specifically calls for almond flour, but you could experiment with other nut flours or even oat flour. Each will slightly change the texture and flavor, so results may vary.

**What else can I use aquafaba whipped topping for?**
It's amazing on pancakes, stirred into hot chocolate for creaminess, as a topping for fruit, or anywhere you'd use regular whipped cream. It's a fantastic vegan alternative.

# Guava and Cheese Empanadas

**Created:** February 9, 2026
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Sweet and salty empanadas with flaky homemade dough, perfect for parties and gatherings.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

These guava and cheese empanadas are Holistic Bravo's go-to for parties because they're always a crowd-pleaser and surprisingly easy to put together. The combination of sweet guava paste and salty parmesan cheese wrapped in crunchy, flaky deliciousness is absolutely irresistible. Holistic Bravo could just eat guava by itself - she loves it that much - but when you pair it with cheese inside buttery, flaky pastry, it becomes something truly special.

Empanadas are a staple in Latin American cuisine, and the guava and cheese combination is a classic favorite. The homemade dough comes together with just a few simple ingredients - flour, butter, egg, salt, and water - and is surprisingly easy to work with. The key is keeping the butter cold and letting the dough rest in the refrigerator, which makes it easier to roll out and creates that perfect flaky texture when baked.

These empanadas are perfect for any gathering because they can be made ahead and baked when you're ready to serve. They're sweet enough to be dessert but also work beautifully as an appetizer or snack. The sweet-salty combination is universally loved, making them a safe bet when you're cooking for a crowd and don't know everyone's preferences.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Perfect sweet and salty combination that everyone loves
- Homemade dough is easier to make than you think
- Great for parties, gatherings, and potlucks
- Can be made ahead and frozen before baking
- Guava paste is widely available in most grocery stores
- Flaky, buttery pastry wrapped around delicious filling
- Sweet enough for dessert, savory enough for appetizer
- Cultural celebration food that connects to Latin American traditions

## Tips

- Keep the butter cold throughout the process for the flakiest crust
- Don't skip the chilling time - it makes the dough much easier to work with
- You can prepare the dough ahead of time and keep it refrigerated for a day or two
- Seal the edges well to prevent filling from leaking during baking
- Brush with egg wash before baking for a golden, shiny finish
- These can be frozen unbaked and cooked from frozen (add 5-10 minutes baking time)
- Try cream cheese instead of parmesan for a different flavor profile
- Store baked empanadas in an airtight container for 2-3 days
- Reheat in the oven to maintain crispiness (not the microwave)

## Ingredients

The dough is simple and traditional. You'll need 2½ cups of all-purpose flour, which provides structure for the empanada dough. One stick of butter (cold and cut into cubes) is crucial - the cold butter creates steam pockets when baking, resulting in those beautiful flaky layers. One egg at room temperature helps bind the dough and adds richness. One teaspoon of salt seasons the dough, and ½ cup of cold water brings everything together. The cold temperature of both the butter and water is important for creating the flakiest pastry.

For the filling, you'll need guava paste (also called pasta de guayaba), which is sold in blocks or tins in the Latin foods section of most grocery stores. It's a thick, sweet paste made from guava fruit that's been cooked down with sugar. Cut the guava paste into slices that will fit nicely in your empanadas - about 1½ to 2 inches long and ¼ inch thick.

The cheese component is parmesan, cut into thick slices similar in size to your guava paste slices. The salty, nutty flavor of parmesan is the perfect counterpoint to the sweet guava. You could also use cream cheese, which is another popular option that creates a creamier filling.

## Instructions

**Making the Dough:**

1. In a large bowl, combine 2½ cups of all-purpose flour and 1 teaspoon of salt. Whisk together to distribute the salt evenly throughout the flour.

2. Add the cold butter cubes to the flour mixture. Using your hands, work the butter into the flour by rubbing the butter and flour between your fingertips. You want to break up the butter into smaller and smaller pieces until the mixture looks like coarse crumbs with some pea-sized butter pieces remaining. This process takes a few minutes but is worth it for that flaky texture.

3. In a small bowl, lightly beat the egg. Add the beaten egg to the flour-butter mixture.

4. Start adding the cold water slowly, a little bit at a time. After each addition, use your hands to work the water into the dough. You might not need all the water - stop adding it when the dough starts to come together into a cohesive ball. The dough should be slightly tacky but not sticky.

5. Once the dough comes together, use your hands to form it into a smooth ball. Don't overwork it - just bring it together.

6. Cover the dough with plastic wrap or place it in a covered container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, up to 1 hour. This resting time allows the gluten to relax, making the dough easier to roll out, and keeps the butter cold.

**Assembly:**

1. Preheat your oven to 375°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

2. Remove the dough from the refrigerator and cut it into 10 equal pieces. Roll each piece between your palms into a smooth ball.

3. On a lightly floured surface, use a rolling pin to flatten each ball into a circle about 4-5 inches in diameter. The thickness should be about 1/8 inch - thin enough to be flaky but thick enough to hold the filling.

4. Place one slice of guava paste in the center of each circle, then place one slice of parmesan cheese on top of the guava.

5. Fold the dough circle in half, bringing one edge over to meet the other, creating a half-moon shape. Press the edges together firmly to seal.

6. For extra security and a pretty presentation, crimp the edges with a fork, pressing down along the sealed edge. This also ensures no filling will leak out during baking.

7. Place the formed empanadas on your prepared baking sheet, spacing them about an inch apart.

**Baking:**

1. Optional but recommended: Brush the tops of the empanadas with a little beaten egg or milk. This creates a beautiful golden-brown, shiny finish.

2. Bake at 375°F for 20-25 minutes, until the empanadas are golden brown and crispy.

3. Remove from the oven and let cool for about 5 minutes before serving. The filling will be very hot, so let them cool just enough to handle safely.

4. Enjoy warm for the best experience - the contrast between the flaky pastry and the warm, melty guava and cheese filling is incredible!

## FAQ

**Where can I find guava paste?**
Look in the Latin foods section of most grocery stores, or visit a Latin market. It's usually sold in blocks or tins. Popular brands include Goya and other Latin American brands. You might see it labeled as "pasta de guayaba."

**Can I make these ahead of time?**
Yes! You can assemble the empanadas completely and freeze them unbaked. Place them on a baking sheet in the freezer until frozen solid, then transfer to a freezer bag. Bake from frozen when ready, adding 5-10 minutes to the baking time.

**What other cheeses work well?**
Cream cheese is the most popular alternative and creates a creamier, milder filling. Queso fresco would also work beautifully. Some people even use mozzarella for a super melty interior, though it's less traditional.

**Can I use store-bought dough?**
You can use refrigerated pie crust or empanada dough discs if you want to save time. However, homemade dough really is superior in flavor and texture, and it's easier to make than you might think!

## Ingredients

The genius of these bagels lies in the Greek yogurt. This isn't just adding protein—the Greek yogurt actually replaces both eggs and traditional leavening agents while creating a tender, slightly tangy base. You'll want to use non-fat Greek yogurt for the best protein-to-calorie ratio, though full-fat works too if that's what you have on hand.

All-purpose flour creates the structure of the bagels. You'll need equal parts Greek yogurt and flour by weight (100g each for the base recipe), which makes it super easy to remember. The combination of yogurt and flour creates what's known as a "2-ingredient dough" that's become popular for good reason—it works!

Baking powder and salt round out the base with just a teaspoon of each. The baking powder helps create a bit of lift and chewiness, while the salt enhances flavor. From there, the variations get fun. Matcha powder adds a beautiful green color and earthy flavor without overwhelming the bagel. Dried cranberries add pops of tartness in the matcha version. For the double chocolate bagels, chocolate protein powder amps up the protein even more while creating a rich chocolate flavor, and mini chocolate chips make them legitimately feel like dessert. And for the everything bagel, well, everything bagel seasoning is all you need to sprinkle on top before air frying.

## Instructions

1. Start by making your base dough. In a large bowl, combine 100g all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon baking powder, and 1 teaspoon salt. Give it a quick whisk to distribute everything evenly.

2. Add 100g of non-fat Greek yogurt to the dry ingredients. Use a spoon to mix it together until it starts to come together, then use your hands to fully incorporate everything into a dough. It should be slightly sticky but workable.

3. Now choose your adventure! For matcha cranberry bagels, add 5g of matcha powder directly to the dough and knead it in until evenly distributed and the dough turns a beautiful green color. Then fold in 30g of dried cranberries throughout the dough.

4. For double chocolate bagels, add one scoop of chocolate protein powder to the base dough and mix well. The protein powder will make the dough significantly drier, so add extra Greek yogurt one spoonful at a time, mixing after each addition, until the mixture forms a solid ball that's no longer crumbly. 5. For everything bagels, simply proceed with the plain base dough—you'll add the seasoning on top before cooking.

6. Divide your dough into 2 equal pieces. This recipe makes 2 substantial bagels.

7. Roll each piece into a ball first, then use your finger to poke a hole through the center. Gently stretch and shape it into a bagel shape with a decent-sized hole in the middle (it will close up a bit during cooking).

8. If making everything bagels, now is the time to generously sprinkle everything bagel seasoning on top of each bagel, pressing it gently so it adheres.

9. Preheat your air fryer to 350°F. Place the shaped bagels in the air fryer basket, leaving space between them.

10. Air fry for 8 minutes on one side. Don't peek too early—let them get nice and golden!

11. Carefully flip the bagels using tongs or a spatula, then air fry for another 8 minutes on the other side until both sides are golden brown and the bagels are cooked through.

# Black Bean Patties with High Protein Buns

**Created:** 2024
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Vegetarian burgers with flavorful black bean patties and homemade Greek yogurt buns.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

Say goodbye to store-bought veggie patties forever. Once you make these homemade black bean patties, you'll never go back—they're just that good. And when you pair them with these high-protein buns made from Greek yogurt and bread flour, you've got a complete vegetarian burger experience that rivals anything you could buy at a restaurant.

Holistic Bravo realized while making this that there are actually more than four ingredients in the patties (despite initially thinking it was a four-ingredient recipe!), but it's still incredibly simple and straightforward. The patties come together in a food processor with black beans, red onion, egg whites, bread crumbs, and soy sauce. That's it. The result is a flavorful, protein-packed patty that holds together beautifully and has an actual meaty texture.

The high-protein buns are equally genius, using that magic Greek yogurt and flour combination that creates a tender, slightly chewy bun that's substantially higher in protein than regular burger buns. Together with the patties, toppings like pesto, shaved parmesan, arugula, and sliced onion, this is the kind of meal that makes you feel satisfied on every level—taste, nutrition, and the pride of having made it yourself from scratch.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Homemade black bean patties are worlds better than anything store-bought
- Each burger provides approximately 30g of protein from multiple sources before toppings
- High-protein buns use Greek yogurt instead of eggs and oil for a healthier alternative
- These pass the "upside down test" meaning the patties hold together perfectly without falling apart
- Perfect for meal prep—make the patties and buns ahead and assemble throughout the week

## Tips

- Don't over-process the black beans—you want texture, not a smooth paste
- The mixture should be thick enough to hold together when you turn the bowl upside down
- Use parchment paper when forming patties to prevent sticking to your hands and surfaces
- Cook patties covered on low to medium heat to ensure they cook through without burning
- Buns can be made ahead and frozen for up to a month for quick burger assembly
- Bread flour has more protein than all-purpose and creates better structure, but AP flour works too
- Make a batch of roasted sweet potatoes at the beginning of the week as an easy side

## Ingredients

Let's start with those high-protein buns, which are surprisingly simple. You'll use 350g of bread flour—bread flour is ideal here because it has higher protein content than all-purpose flour, which creates a chewier, more substantial bun that won't fall apart under burger toppings. Combined with 350g of Greek yogurt (notice the equal ratio—easy to remember!), plus baking powder and salt, you've got a dough that replaces traditional eggs and oil while pumping up the protein content significantly. Black sesame seeds on top are optional but add a beautiful finish and subtle nutty flavor.

For the black bean patties, start with one can of thoroughly drained and rinsed black beans. These are your protein and fiber powerhouse. You'll also need a quarter of a red onion for flavor and moisture, egg whites (about 1/3 cup) to bind everything together without adding the fat from yolks, bread crumbs (panko works great) to absorb moisture and create the right texture, and a tablespoon of low-sodium soy sauce for that savory umami depth.

For assembly, pesto is the perfect spread—it's rich and herby without being heavy. Shaved parmesan adds salty, nutty notes, fresh arugula brings a peppery bite, and sliced onion adds crunch. And don't forget to prep some sweet potatoes for the side—they're the perfect accompaniment.

## Instructions

1. First, let's tackle the high-protein buns. Preheat your oven to 350°F.

2. In a large bowl, combine 350g of bread flour with 1g of baking powder and a pinch of salt. Whisk together to distribute evenly.

3. Add 350g of Greek yogurt to the flour mixture. Use a spoon to start mixing, then switch to using your hands once it gets too thick. Bring the dough together and knead it in the bowl until it forms a smooth, cohesive dough. This should take about 3-5 minutes of kneading.

4. Divide the dough into 4 equal pieces. Roll each piece in your hands to form a ball, then gently flatten and shape it into a bun shape.

5. If using black sesame seeds, sprinkle them generously on top of each bun now, pressing gently so they stick.

6. Place the buns on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake at 350°F for 20 minutes.

7. After 20 minutes, increase the heat to 425°F and bake for another 10 minutes until the tops are slightly browned and the buns are cooked through. Remove and let cool while you make the patties.

8. For the black bean patties, make sure your black beans are thoroughly drained and rinsed. Add them to a food processor along with the red onion (cut into a few large chunks).

9. Pulse the food processor, stopping to scrape down the sides as needed. You want the black beans and onion to break down into small chunks—not a smooth paste. You should still see texture.

10. Add the egg whites (1/3 cup), bread crumbs (start with about a cup), and soy sauce (1 tablespoon). Pulse again to combine. The mixture should be thick enough to hold together.

11. Here's the test: if you can turn the food processor bowl upside down and the mixture doesn't fall out, it's perfect. If it's too wet, add more bread crumbs a little at a time until it passes the "upside down test."

12. Lay out a sheet of parchment paper on a cutting board or counter. This prevents the mixture from sticking everywhere.

13. Divide the mixture into 4 portions and use your hands to form each one into a patty shape. They should be substantial—about the size of your buns.

14. Heat a pan over low to medium heat and spray with cooking oil to prevent sticking.

15. Place the patties in the pan and cover with a lid. Cook for 3-4 minutes on the first side without disturbing them—this helps them develop a nice crust.

16. Carefully flip the patties and cook covered for another 3-4 minutes on the other side until they're crispy on the outside and heated through.

17. Now it's assembly time! Slice your buns in half horizontally.

18. Spread a generous amount of pesto on the bottom bun.

19. Place a black bean patty on top of the pesto.

20. Add shaved parmesan cheese, fresh arugula, and sliced onion on top of the patty.

21. Cap it with the top half of the bun.

22. Serve with a side of roasted sweet potatoes or your favorite side dish. Enjoy immediately while the patties are warm and the buns are fresh!

## FAQ

**Why use bread flour instead of all-purpose flour for the buns?**
Bread flour contains more protein than all-purpose flour, which creates a chewier, more substantial bun with better structure. These buns need to hold up to burger toppings and sauces, and the extra protein in bread flour helps them do exactly that. That said, all-purpose flour will work in a pinch—the buns just won't be quite as sturdy.

**Can I make the patties ahead of time?**
Absolutely! Form the patties and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Cover and refrigerate for up to 2 days before cooking. You can also freeze them for up to a month—just place parchment paper between each patty so they don't stick together, then store in a freezer bag. Cook from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

**What's the "upside down test"?**
This is Holistic Bravo's genius way to check if your patty mixture is the right consistency. If you can turn the bowl upside down and the mixture stays put without sliding out, it's thick enough to form into patties that will hold together during cooking. If it starts to slide out, add more bread crumbs until it passes the test!

# Homemade Dog Treats

Simple 3-ingredient all-natural treats for your furry best friend (safe for humans too!).

## Introduction

Today we're making something special for the best friends in our lives—our dogs! Rex is about to be one very happy pup with these super simple, all-natural dog treats that come together with just three wholesome ingredients: oats, peanut butter, and pumpkin. The best part? They're ready in under 40 minutes from start to finish, and your dog will absolutely love them.

Holistic Bravo created this recipe for her pup Rex, and watching him try them for the first time is pure joy. These treats have a Play-Doh-like consistency before baking, which makes them perfect for pressing into cute molds—think paw prints, bones, or any fun shape you can find. ## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Only 3 natural ingredients with no preservatives or mystery additives like store-bought treats
- Ready in about 40 minutes total including baking time
- Rex approved which means your pup will probably love them too
- Makes approximately 20-30 treats depending on mold size for training rewards or special occasions

## Ingredients

Let's start with rolled oats, which are the base of these treats. One cup is all you need. Oats are gentle on dog digestion, provide fiber, and are completely safe for dogs to eat. They'll get processed into smaller pieces when you blend everything together, creating that perfect moldable texture.

Next up is pumpkin puree—you'll use a quarter cup. Make sure you're using 100% pure pumpkin puree, NOT pumpkin pie filling which contains added sugar and spices that aren't necessary for dogs. Plain pumpkin is great for dog digestion, adds natural moisture and sweetness, and dogs generally love the flavor.

And finally, peanut butter—this is where you need to be careful. Use one-third cup of CREAMY peanut butter, and this is absolutely critical: check the ingredients label and make sure the ONLY ingredient is peanuts. No salt, no sugar, and especially NO XYLITOL. Xylitol is a common sweetener found in some peanut butters, and it's extremely toxic to dogs. Brands like Jif, Skippy, and most natural peanut butters that list only peanuts are safe. When in doubt, read that label!

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 300°F. This lower temperature ensures the treats bake through without burning.

2. Add 1 cup of rolled oats, 1/4 cup of pumpkin puree, and 1/3 cup of creamy peanut butter (ONLY peanuts in the ingredients—seriously, check this!) to a food processor or blender.

3. Pulse everything together until well combined. The oats will break down and the mixture should come together into a cohesive, slightly sticky mixture.

4. Check the consistency—it should be like Play-Doh. Moldable and holds its shape, but not too dry or crumbly, and not too wet. If it's too dry, add a tiny bit of water. If too wet, add a few more oats.

5. Grab your silicone molds—paw prints and bones are classic choices, but any shape works. Press the mixture firmly into each mold cavity, making sure to pack it in well so there are no air pockets.

6. Once all your molds are filled, place them on a baking sheet for easy handling.

7. Bake at 300°F for 25-30 minutes. The treats should be firm to the touch when done.

8. Remove from the oven and let them cool completely before giving them to your dog. This is important—you don't want Rex burning his mouth on a hot treat!

9. Once cooled, pop the treats out of the molds. They should come out easily if you used silicone molds.

10. Now comes the best part—let your dog try them! Rex sat so nicely for his first one, and you could tell he loved it immediately.

11. Store the treats in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week, or refrigerate them for up to 2 weeks. You can also freeze them for even longer storage—just thaw before giving to your pup.

12. Remember, these are treats, not a meal replacement. Give them in moderation as rewards for being such a good boy or girl!

**How long do these treats last?**
They'll keep at room temperature in an airtight container for about a week, in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks, or you can freeze them for a month or longer. Frozen treats can actually be a nice cool snack on hot days—just make sure they're not too hard for your dog's teeth.

## Ingredients

The pancake "buns" are where the magic happens. You'll start with one cup of all-purpose flour, one teaspoon of baking powder for a bit of lift, and a pinch of salt. Then you add one whole egg (not just egg whites—the whole egg helps with structure and richness), one tablespoon of olive oil for moisture, and one tablespoon of monk fruit sweetener. Monk fruit is a zero-calorie natural sweetener that makes these taste sweet without adding sugar, but you can absolutely use regular sugar if you prefer.

For the liquid, you'll add water or milk of choice one tablespoon at a time. The key here is to make the batter THICKER than regular pancake batter. You want it thick enough to hold together as a bun structure, not thin and runny like typical pancakes. This is crucial for them to work as sandwich buns.

For the filling, you'll need eggs—one or two per sandwich depending on how protein-packed you want it—and cheese slices. American cheese is classic for that authentic fast-food vibe, but use whatever cheese you love.

And you'll need round silicone molds or egg rings to create those perfect circular shapes. This is what makes them look like actual McGriddles!

## Instructions

1. Start by making your pancake batter. In a bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of baking powder, 1 tablespoon of monk fruit sweetener (or regular sugar), and a pinch of salt. Whisk these dry ingredients together.

2. Add 1 whole egg and 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the dry ingredients. Mix it together.

3. Now add your liquid—water or milk of choice—but do it gradually, just 1 tablespoon at a time. Mix after each addition. You want to reach a thick batter consistency, thicker than regular pancake batter. It should hold its shape when poured.

4. Heat a large pan over medium to low heat. You want the temperature controlled so nothing burns.

5. Place your silicone molds or egg rings in the heated pan. You'll need at least 4 molds for one sandwich (2 pancake buns + 1-2 egg molds depending on how many eggs you're using).

6. Pour the pancake batter into the molds to create your "buns." Fill them about 2/3 full so they have room to expand slightly.

7. In the separate molds, crack your eggs. One or two eggs per sandwich, depending on your preference.

8. Cover the pan with a lid. This is important—it helps everything cook evenly and helps the eggs set properly.

9. Here's a pro tip: add a splash of water to the pan (not in the molds, but around them) and re-cover. This creates steam that helps the eggs set quicker and makes flipping everything easier. The steam also helps the pancakes cook through without burning the bottoms.

10. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on the first side. Check the pancakes—they should be golden brown on the bottom and mostly set on top.

11. Carefully flip both the pancake "buns" and the egg molds. Use a thin spatula to get under them. Cook for another 3-4 minutes on the other side.

12. Once everything is cooked through, remove the molds from the pan and carefully pop everything out of the molds.

13. Time to assemble! Place one pancake "bun" on a plate.

14. Add a slice of cheese on top of the bottom bun.

15. Place the cooked egg(s) on top of the cheese.

16. Add another slice of cheese if you want extra cheesy goodness (always recommended).

17. Top with the second pancake "bun."

18. Enjoy immediately while everything is warm and the cheese is melty! The combination of sweet pancakes and savory egg and cheese is absolutely incredible.

## FAQ

**How do I get the pancakes to actually hold together like buns?**
The secret is making the batter thicker than normal pancake batter. Add your liquid gradually and stop when you reach a thick, spoonable consistency. The batter should hold its shape when poured, not spread out thin. Using molds also helps create uniform, sturdy shapes that work perfectly as buns.

**What if I don't have silicone molds?**
No problem! Metal egg rings work great and are super affordable. You can even use clean, empty tuna cans with both ends removed as makeshift molds in a pinch. Mason jar lids (just the metal ring part) also work. Just make sure whatever you use can handle the heat of the stovetop.

**Can I make these ahead of time?**
The pancake "buns" can definitely be made ahead and stored in the fridge in an airtight container. They'll keep for 3-4 days. When you're ready to eat, just reheat the buns for 20-30 seconds in the microwave, cook fresh eggs, and assemble. This makes busy weekday mornings so much easier!

# Mushroom Ravioli with Ricotta Filling

**Created:** 2024

A therapeutic and grounding homemade pasta dish featuring handmade ravioli stuffed with savory ricotta and mushroom filling.

## Introduction

I make pasta at least once a week because it grounds me. There's something incredibly meditative about kneading dough—I can drown out the world and just focus on the rhythm of my hands working the pasta. It's therapeutic in a way that few other cooking activities are.

This week's creation is mushroom ravioli, which is Holistic Bravo's favorite type of pasta to make. The process of forming each individual ravioli is hands-on and mindful, requiring just enough attention that your brain can't wander to stress or worries. You're fully present with the dough, the filling, the sealing of each pocket. It's pasta meditation.

The filling is a simple but flavorful combination of ricotta cheese mixed with sautéed mushrooms and fresh parsley. For the sauce, simplicity reigns again—just olive oil, more mushrooms cooked down with spices, and pasta water to bring everything together with fresh grated parmesan at the end. This is the kind of recipe you make when you need to take care of yourself, when you need something that nourishes both body and soul through the act of creating it.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- The hands-on process of making pasta dough and forming ravioli is genuinely therapeutic and grounding
- Fresh homemade ravioli has a tender texture that dried pasta simply cannot match
- Ricotta and mushroom filling is rich and flavorful without being heavy or complicated
- Simple mushroom sauce with pasta water creates restaurant-quality results at home

## Ingredients

For the pasta dough, you only need two ingredients: 200g of all-purpose flour and 2 whole eggs. That's it. This classic pasta dough ratio creates tender, elastic noodles that are perfect for ravioli. The act of kneading this simple dough for 8-10 minutes is where the therapeutic magic happens.

The filling starts with half a cup of ricotta cheese, which provides a creamy, mild base. To that, you'll add sautéed Bella mushrooms (also called cremini)—about 2 mushrooms chopped small. These get cooked down with a whole clove of minced garlic, onion powder, garlic powder, and sea salt until they're fully cooked and all the moisture has evaporated. Fresh parsley adds brightness and color—just a few sprigs chopped up will do.

For the sauce, you'll use olive oil as the base, more sliced Bella mushrooms (about 4), and seasonings: garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, sea salt, and black pepper. The magic ingredient is pasta water—that starchy, salty water the ravioli cooks in. You'll reserve 3/4 cup to create a light but flavorful sauce. And finally, fresh grated parmesan cheese to finish—half a cup of the good stuff.

## Instructions

1. Let's start with the therapeutic part: making pasta dough. Combine 200g of all-purpose flour with 2 whole eggs in a large bowl or directly on a clean counter.

2. Use your hands to bring the mixture together, then begin kneading. Fold the dough over itself, push it away with the heels of your hands, rotate, and repeat. Knead for 8-10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. This is the meditative part—just you, the dough, and the rhythm.

3. Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes. This allows the gluten to relax and makes rolling easier.

4. While the dough rests, prepare your filling. Heat a pan and add your chopped Bella mushrooms (about 2 mushrooms) and one whole clove of minced garlic.

5. Sauté the mushrooms and garlic together, seasoning with onion powder, garlic powder, and sea salt. Cook until the mushrooms are fully softened and all their liquid has evaporated. This is crucial—you don't want watery filling.

6. Once the mushrooms are cooked down, transfer them to a bowl with half a cup of ricotta cheese and a handful of chopped fresh parsley. Mix everything together well.

7. Transfer the filling mixture to a sandwich bag (a gallon-size freezer bag works great) and cut off one corner. This creates an easy piping bag for filling your ravioli.

8. After the dough has rested, roll it out on a lightly floured surface. You want it thin enough that you can almost see through it, but not so thin it tears. A pasta roller makes this easier, but a rolling pin works too.

9. Once rolled out, pipe small mounds of filling onto the pasta sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.

10. Fold the pasta over the filling or place another sheet on top. Press around each mound of filling to seal, making sure to push out any air pockets.

11. Use a fork to press and seal the edges really well—this prevents them from opening during cooking.

12. Cut the ravioli into individual squares using a knife or pasta cutter.

13. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. The water should taste like the sea.

14. Gently add the ravioli to the boiling water. They'll sink at first, then float to the surface when they're done—this takes about 3-4 minutes.

15. Before draining, use a measuring cup to scoop out 3/4 cup of pasta water. Set this aside.

16. While the ravioli cooks, make your sauce. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.

17. Add your sliced Bella mushrooms (about 4 mushrooms) and cook until they're softened and starting to brown.

18. Season the mushrooms with garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, sea salt, and black pepper to taste.

19. Add the reserved pasta water to the pan with the mushrooms. This creates a light sauce that will coat the ravioli.

20. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked ravioli directly from the pot to the pan with the mushroom sauce. Toss gently to coat.

21. Plate the ravioli and mushroom sauce, then top generously with fresh grated parmesan cheese.

22. Serve immediately while hot. Enjoy the fruits of your therapeutic pasta-making session!

## FAQ

**Can I make the pasta dough ahead of time?**
Absolutely! Wrap the kneaded dough tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. This actually makes it easier to work with. Just let it sit at room temperature for about 20-30 minutes before rolling so it's not too stiff.

**How do I know when the ravioli are fully cooked?**
Fresh ravioli will float to the surface of the boiling water when they're done, which typically takes 3-4 minutes. They should be tender but still have a slight bite—al dente. Don't overcook them or they'll become mushy.

**Can I freeze uncooked ravioli?**
Yes, and this is a great meal prep strategy! Lay the formed ravioli in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, making sure they don't touch. Freeze until solid (about 2 hours), then transfer to a freezer bag. They'll keep for up to a month. Cook from frozen, just adding 1-2 minutes to the cooking time.

For the orange dough, add 1-2 teaspoons of turmeric powder, which creates a beautiful golden-orange hue and brings warm, slightly peppery notes.

The filling is wonderfully simple but flavorful. Start with fresh spinach—about 2 cups—which you'll blanch briefly to wilt it down and make it easier to work with. You'll need to really squeeze out the excess water from the blanched spinach; this is crucial for preventing watery filling. Raw cashews (about half a cup) create a creamy, rich base similar to traditional pesto but without pine nuts. ## Instructions

1. Let's start by making three separate pasta doughs. For each dough, you'll use the same process but different additions.

2. For the natural dough: Combine 200g all-purpose flour with 2 eggs. Knead until smooth and elastic, about 8-10 minutes. Wrap and set aside.

3. For the green matcha dough: Mix 200g all-purpose flour with 1-2 teaspoons of matcha powder first, whisking to distribute the color evenly. Add 2 eggs and knead until smooth and uniformly green. The matcha will create a beautiful emerald color. Wrap and set aside.

4. For the orange turmeric dough: Mix 200g all-purpose flour with 1-2 teaspoons of turmeric powder, whisking to distribute. Add 2 eggs and knead until smooth and uniformly orange-gold. Wrap and set aside.

6. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch your fresh spinach for just 30-60 seconds—it should wilt down but still be bright green.

8. Add the squeezed spinach to a food processor along with the raw cashews, parmesan cheese, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

9. Blend until the mixture reaches a pesto-like consistency—it should be relatively smooth with some texture remaining.

10. Now for the fun part—rolling out your colored doughs! Take each dough one at a time and roll it out thin using a pasta roller or rolling pin. You want it thin enough to see your hand through it but not so thin it tears.

11. Get creative with your color combinations! You can use one color for the bottom layer of ravioli and a different color for the top. You can alternate colors randomly. You can create patterns. This is your art project.

13. Brush the edges and areas around the filling with a bit of water—this acts as glue.

14. Place your second sheet of pasta (same color or different color—your choice!) on top.

15. Press firmly around each mound of filling, sealing the ravioli and pushing out any air pockets. Air pockets can cause the ravioli to burst during cooking.

17. Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil.

18. Gently add your tri-color ravioli to the boiling water. They'll cook in 3-4 minutes and will float to the surface when done.

19. While they cook, prepare your sauce. For this pasta, simple is best—toss with olive oil and parmesan, or use a light butter sauce, or even just more of that pesto filling thinned with pasta water.

20. Drain the ravioli (or use a slotted spoon to transfer them) and toss with your chosen sauce.

21. Plate and admire your colorful creation before diving in! The colors make this dish absolutely Instagram-worthy.

Work with one color of dough at a time and keep them separated until you're ready to assemble the ravioli. When cutting, use a sharp cutter to get clean edges. The colors are in the dough itself, so they won't bleed during cooking as long as you maintain distinct layers.

# Lazy Girl Pumpkin Gnocchi

**Created:** February 9, 2026
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Quick and easy homemade gnocchi made with leftover pumpkin and flour in a food processor - no kneading required!

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

This is the recipe that proves homemade gnocchi doesn't have to be intimidating or time-consuming. Holistic Bravo came home from work at 3:15 and challenged herself to make dinner in under 20 minutes - she was shooting for 15. And she nailed it with this genius lazy girl method that requires no kneading, no rolling out, and barely any cleanup.

The secret is using leftover pumpkin puree (or really any vegetable "binder") mixed with flour in a food processor. No fancy pasta-making skills required. You literally just cut pieces of dough directly into boiling water and watch them float to the surface when they're done. It's the kind of cooking hack that feels almost too easy to be real, but the results are absolutely delicious.

What makes this recipe even better is how versatile it is. Have leftover sweet potato? Use that. Found some spinach in the back of the fridge that needs to be used? Perfect for gnocchi. It's the ultimate leftover-rescue recipe that turns yesterday's vegetables into tonight's impressive dinner.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- Ready in just 15 minutes from start to finish, perfect when you don't want to spend hours cooking
- No kneading or rolling required - the food processor does all the work
- Uses up leftover vegetables that might otherwise go to waste
- You literally just cut pieces of dough into the water, no shaping needed
- The simple garlic and olive oil sauce with pasta water creates a silky coating
- Can substitute any vegetable puree you have on hand for endless variations
- Makes you feel like a pasta-making genius without requiring any actual pasta-making skills

## Tips

- Don't rush the texture check - dough should be slightly springy to the touch, not sticky or dry
- Weigh your ingredients if you can for more consistent results, but cups work fine too
- For cup measurements, use 1 cup binder to slightly more than 1 cup flour
- Incorporate flour slowly until you reach the right consistency
- Reserve plenty of pasta water - it's essential for creating the silky sauce
- Add 1 tablespoon corn starch or tapioca starch to the pasta water if you want a thicker sauce
- Try other vegetable purees like sweet potato, regular potato, or butternut squash
- Make a double batch and freeze the uncooked gnocchi on a baking sheet before transferring to freezer bags

## Ingredients

The genius of this recipe is how minimal the ingredient list is. You need leftover pumpkin puree (the recipe uses 162g, which is about a cup), all-purpose flour (175g or slightly more than a cup), and a teaspoon of salt. That's literally it for the gnocchi itself.

The key is the ratio between your "binder" (the pumpkin or other vegetable puree) and your flour. Generally, you want slightly more flour than binder in grams. Don't stress about exact measurements - the creator emphasizes that you don't need precision here. Just add flour slowly until you get a dough that's springy to the touch.

For the sauce, simplicity reigns again. Olive oil, minced garlic, salt, pepper, and reserved pasta water create a classic Italian coating. The starchy pasta water emulsifies with the olive oil to make everything silky and cohesive. Top with fresh parmesan and parsley for the finishing touch.

## Instructions

1. Add your pumpkin puree (162g or about 1 cup), flour (175g or slightly more than 1 cup), and a teaspoon of salt to your food processor. Pulse until everything comes together into a cohesive ball of dough. It should be slightly springy when you touch it.

2. While the food processor is working, bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil.

3. Here's where it gets fun - take your dough ball directly to the pot. Using a knife or bench scraper, cut small pieces of dough and drop them directly into the boiling water. No rolling, no shaping, just cut and drop. This is as easy as it gets.

4. Watch as the gnocchi sink at first, then float to the surface when they're cooked, usually about 2-3 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to scoop them out as they finish. Make sure to reserve about a cup of the pasta water before draining.

5. In a large pan over medium heat, add a tablespoon of olive oil and your minced garlic. Season with salt and pepper. Sauté briefly until the garlic becomes fragrant but doesn't brown.

6. Pour some of the reserved pasta water into the pan with the garlic oil. This is what creates your sauce. Add the cooked gnocchi and toss everything gently to coat. If you want a thicker sauce, add a tablespoon of corn starch or tapioca starch.

7. Plate your gnocchi and top with freshly grated parmesan and chopped parsley. That's it - 15-minute homemade gnocchi done.

## FAQ

**Do I really not need to knead or roll out the dough?**
Correct! The food processor does all the work of bringing the dough together. You literally just cut pieces directly into the water. It's the lazy girl method for a reason.

**What if my dough is too sticky?**
Add a bit more flour, one tablespoon at a time, and pulse again until you reach the right consistency. The dough should be slightly springy to the touch.

**Can I use canned pumpkin?**
Yes! Just make sure it's pure pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling with added sugars and spices. Pure pumpkin puree works perfectly.

# Simple Two-Ingredient Pasta

**Created:** February 9, 2026
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Learn to make fresh pasta at home with just flour and water - no eggs needed!

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

Have you ever wanted to make pasta at home but thought it was too complicated, required too many ingredients, or needed expensive equipment? Holistic Bravo is here to hold your hand and prove that in the next 60 seconds, you can become a pasta expert. The philosophy behind this recipe is simple: don't over complicate your life. It takes two ingredients to make pasta - flour and water. That's it. No eggs, no fancy machinery, no complicated techniques. Just mix, knead for 2-3 minutes, roll out, cut, and boil. You can use store-bought sauce or make your own simple version with pasta water and parmesan.

What makes this water-based pasta special is how accessible it is. It's completely vegan, beginner-friendly, and proves that homemade pasta doesn't have to be intimidating. Plus, it's a great way to teach kids about cooking or build your own confidence before tackling more complex pasta recipes.

- Don't rush the mixing process - use a fork first, then the bench scraper helps incorporate everything

- Cut the dough into 4 equal pieces for easier handling

## Ingredients

The entire pasta requires just 100g of all-purpose flour and 50g of water. That's a 2:1 ratio that's easy to remember and scale up or down. The creator jokes that there's one extra ingredient required: love. But really, that's optional - the flour and water will get you there regardless.

For the flour, all-purpose works perfectly. Nothing fancy needed. For the water, regular tap water is fine. Some recipes call for warm water, but room temperature works just as well.

When it comes to serving, you have options. You can use any store-bought sauce you love, which is perfectly acceptable and encouraged. Or you can make a simple pasta water sauce by combining the starchy cooking liquid with parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. The pasta water contains starch that helps create a silky coating when mixed with cheese or oil.

## Instructions

1. Start by combining 100g flour and 50g water in a bowl. Use a simple fork to mix everything together until it starts to come together into a shaggy dough.

2. Switch to a bench scraper (or a knife if you don't have one) to help incorporate the mixture more thoroughly. Scrape and fold the dough together, getting all those dry bits mixed in.

3. Once it's mostly together, use your hands to knead everything for about 2-3 minutes. You're not trying to develop tons of gluten here, just getting everything nicely incorporated. Don't over-complicate it.

4. Let the dough rest while you set up your work area and get your water boiling. Even just 5-10 minutes of resting time helps the texture.

5. Cut your rested dough ball into 4 equal pieces for easier handling. Take one piece and roll it out with your hands into a long rope or log shape, about the thickness of your finger.

6. Using your bench scraper, cut the rope into small pieces about 1-2 inches each. You should end up with bite-sized pasta pieces.

7. Now for the fun part - shaping. If you have a gnocchi board, place a piece of dough on it and use your thumb to roll it down, creating grooves. Alternatively, use the edge of your bench scraper to roll pieces up quickly - this method is actually faster.

8. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add your shaped pasta and cook for 2-3 minutes until they float to the surface.

9. Toss with store-bought sauce or make your own by combining starchy pasta water with parmesan, salt, and pepper. That's it - homemade pasta accomplished.

## FAQ

Correct! This is a traditional water-based pasta that's completely vegan and works beautifully. No eggs required.

**What if I don't have a gnocchi board?**

**Can I make this ahead of time?**
Yes! Let the shaped pasta dry on a floured surface for 30 minutes, then store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. You can also freeze it.

## Ingredients

Spaghetti squash is the star here, and it's honestly a miracle vegetable when it comes to calorie density. An entire squash is only about 300 calories, meaning you could eat the whole thing if you wanted to. When roasted face-down at 425°F for 45-50 minutes, it becomes tender and the flesh separates into spaghetti-like strands with a fork. A drizzle of olive oil and some salt and pepper before roasting helps develop flavor.

The béchamel sauce is one of the classic French "mother sauces," made with equal parts butter and flour cooked together, then milk whisked in to create a creamy coating. The recipe uses 2 tablespoons each of butter and flour, plus 1 cup of milk. Using unsweetened almond milk reduces calories further while keeping the sauce creamy and delicious.

The key to a smooth béchamel is warming the milk first before adding it to the butter-flour mixture. This 30-45 second step in the microwave prevents lumps and helps the sauce come together quickly. Season with salt and pepper, and finish with freshly grated parmesan and chopped parsley for a restaurant-worthy presentation.

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 425°F. Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out all the seeds and stringy center bits with a spoon.

2. Drizzle the cut sides of the squash with olive oil and sprinkle generously with salt and black pepper. Place the halves face-down on a lined baking sheet.

3. Roast for 45-50 minutes until tender. If you prefer a more al dente texture, check it around the 40-minute mark. The squash is done when you can easily pierce it with a fork and the flesh starts to separate into strands.

4. During the last 5 minutes of roasting, make your béchamel. In a pan over low heat, melt 2 tablespoons of butter. Low heat is important - you don't want to burn the butter.

5. Once the butter is melted, add 2 tablespoons of flour. Stir constantly with a whisk or wooden spoon until the flour is fully dissolved and incorporated with the butter. This should take about 1-2 minutes.

6. While the butter-flour mixture cooks, warm 1 cup of milk in the microwave for 30-45 seconds. This step makes the sauce come together smoothly without lumps.

7. Pour the warmed milk into the butter-flour mixture while stirring constantly. The sauce will thicken quickly - this is what you want. Keep stirring until it reaches a creamy, pourable consistency.

8. Season the béchamel with 1 teaspoon salt and 1 teaspoon black pepper. Taste and adjust if needed. The entire sauce should take just 4-5 minutes total.

9. Remove the roasted squash from the oven and use a fork to scrape the flesh into noodle-like strands. You can leave it in the shell for a pretty presentation or transfer to a bowl.

10. Pour half of the béchamel sauce over the squash and gently toss to coat. Grate fresh parmesan over the top and sprinkle with chopped parsley. Serve immediately while hot.

Store the squash and sauce separately in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat gently in the microwave or on the stovetop.

# Three-Ingredient Shortbread

**Created:** February 9, 2026
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Simple, buttery shortbread cookies made with just butter, powdered sugar, and flour.

## Introduction

Sometimes life is about doing what makes you happy, even when everyone else says it's too early for the holiday season. - CRITICAL: Don't microwave the butter to soften it - let it sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes instead
- The proper butter texture is softened but still cool - should indent when pressed but not be greasy
- IMPORTANT: Chill the dough for a full 30 minutes (or more) for cookies that hold their shape
- If the dough is too soft to work with after chilling, pop it back in the fridge for another 10 minutes
- Cut into classic shapes like circles, squares, or holiday themes with cookie cutters
- Add 1 teaspoon vanilla extract for extra flavor if desired
- Dip cooled cookies in melted chocolate for extra indulgence
- Sprinkle with coarse sugar before baking for sparkle and crunch

## Ingredients

The beauty of shortbread is in its simplicity. Real butter is the star - use 1.5 sticks (12 tablespoons) of good quality butter, and never substitute margarine. The butter must be softened properly at room temperature, not microwaved, for best texture and shape retention. This takes patience - about 30-60 minutes sitting on the counter - but it makes all the difference.

Powdered sugar (1/2 cup) creates a more tender, delicate texture than granulated sugar. It's what gives shortbread that signature melt-in-your-mouth quality. The fine texture of powdered sugar incorporates more easily into the butter and creates cookies that are less dense.

All-purpose flour (1.5 cups) provides the structure. The key is not overmixing once you add the flour, or the cookies will be tough instead of tender. Mix just until the dough comes together, then stop.

## Instructions

1. Take your butter out of the fridge well in advance - at least 30-60 minutes before you plan to bake. Let it soften at room temperature until it's soft enough to indent with your finger but not greasy or melted. Don't rush this step by microwaving it.

2. In a bowl, beat together the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. This usually takes 2-3 minutes with an electric mixer, or a bit longer by hand.

3. Gradually add the flour to the butter-sugar mixture. Mix until just combined and a dough forms. Don't overmix or your cookies will be tough.

4. Here's the step many people skip but shouldn't: wrap the dough and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. The creator only did 10 minutes and regretted it - don't make the same mistake. The chilling helps the cookies hold their shape.

5. After the dough is properly chilled, roll it out on a floured surface to about 1/2 inch thickness. Use cookie cutters to cut into desired shapes.

6. Place cookies on a lined baking sheet with a bit of space between them. They don't spread much, but give them some room.

7. Bake at 350°F for 12-15 minutes until the edges just start to turn golden. Don't overbake - shortbread should still be quite pale when done.

8. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes - they're fragile when hot. Then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

9. Serve with your favorite coffee or tea and celebrate the season your way, regardless of what others think about timing.

## FAQ

**Why didn't my cookies keep their shape?**
Two common reasons: the butter wasn't properly softened at room temperature (microwaving makes it too soft), and/or the dough wasn't chilled long enough before baking. Both steps are crucial.

**Can I freeze the dough?**
Yes! Shortbread dough freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and freeze. Thaw in the fridge overnight before rolling and cutting.

**What's the proper butter texture for shortbread?**
Softened but still cool - it should indent when you press it with your finger, but shouldn't be greasy, melted, or sliding around. This takes 30-60 minutes at room temperature, not 30 seconds in the microwave.

3. Crack one whole egg into the center well you created.

4. Pour egg whites directly on top of the cabbage around the whole egg. This holds everything together and adds extra protein.

5. Season with sea salt and black pepper to taste. Keep it simple or add other seasonings you like.

6. Cover the pan and cook until the egg is set to your preference - runny yolk, medium, or fully cooked.

7. Serve on top of toast or with rice or any grain you like. It's like a deconstructed breakfast with built-in vegetables.

Just nuts or seeds or rice, water, and whatever sweetener or flavorings you choose.

## Tips

- A high-speed blender like a Vitamix is essential for breaking down nuts and seeds thoroughly
- A nut milk bag makes straining much easier and more thorough than cheesecloth
- Always use raw, unsalted nuts and seeds for milk-making - roasted varieties create off-flavors
- Add dates for natural sweetness instead of processed sugar
- Add vanilla extract for classic milk flavor
- Add a pinch of salt to any milk to enhance the natural flavors

## Ingredients

For hemp milk, you need raw hemp seeds or hemp hearts plus water. Hemp doesn't require soaking and blends quickly. It's the highest in omega-3 fatty acids and contains complete protein, making it nutritionally impressive.

Rice milk requires cooked white rice that's been cooled, plus water. This is the most naturally sweet option because of the rice's carbohydrate content. Add cinnamon and vanilla for authentic horchata flavor.

Almond milk is made from raw almonds and water. Almonds benefit from soaking for a few hours or overnight, which makes them easier to blend and creates creamier milk. Optional additions include dates for sweetness and vanilla for flavor.

Cashew milk uses raw cashews and water. Cashews can skip the soaking step because they're softer than almonds, though soaking does create even creamier results. This milk has the thickest consistency of all four.

The general ratio for most nut and seed milks is about 1 cup of the base ingredient to 3-4 cups of water, though you can adjust for thicker or thinner milk. Always use a high-speed blender for best results.

## Instructions

### General Process for All Milks

1. Prepare your base ingredient. For nuts and seeds, soak if needed - almonds benefit from soaking for a few hours or overnight, cashews can skip it but soaking helps, hemp doesn't need soaking. For rice milk, cook rice first and let it cool completely.

2. Add your base ingredient and water to a high-speed blender. The typical ratio is about 1 cup base to 3-4 cups water, but this varies. Hemp and cashew use less water for creamier results, rice and almond use more.

3. Blend on high speed for 1-2 minutes until completely smooth. A high-speed blender like a Vitamix makes a huge difference in the final texture.

4. Strain the mixture through a nut milk bag or fine-mesh strainer into a bowl or pitcher. Squeeze the bag thoroughly to extract all the liquid. The pulp that remains can be saved for other recipes.

5. Add any sweeteners or flavorings now if desired - dates, vanilla extract, cinnamon, maple syrup, or a pinch of salt.

6. Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate. Homemade milk lasts 3-5 days. Shake well before each use as natural separation is normal.

Hemp milk is the most flavorful and was the creator's biggest surprise. It doesn't require soaking and has a distinct hemp taste that's actually delicious. Ranked #1.

Almond milk is the most versatile and never overpowers other ingredients in recipes. It's the creator's go-to for using in cooking and baking because of this neutrality. Ranked #3.

Cashew milk is the thickest and most similar to heavy cream in consistency. Perfect when you need that creamy texture for soups or sauces, but a little thick for everyday drinking. Ranked #4 not because it's bad, but because the thickness isn't ideal for all uses.

Start with cashew or almond - they're the easiest and most forgiving for beginners. They blend smoothly and taste familiar to most people.

3-5 days in the refrigerator. Without preservatives, the shelf life is much shorter than store-bought, but the flavor is fresher and better.

**Do I need to strain all of them?**
Cashew milk can skip straining if you like it thick and creamy. The others benefit from straining for a smooth, drinkable texture.

**What do I do with the leftover pulp?**
Use it in smoothies for extra fiber, add it to muffin or cookie batter, make energy balls, or use as a thickener in soups. Don't waste it - it still has nutrients and fiber.

# High-Protein Greek Yogurt Crescent Rolls

**Created:** December 2024
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Homemade crescent rolls made with Greek yogurt for added protein and a delicious flaky texture - a healthier take on the classic Pillsbury rolls.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

If you've ever craved those buttery, flaky Pillsbury crescent rolls but wished they packed more of a protein punch, this recipe is about to become your new favorite. Holistic Bravo has created a brilliant homemade version that uses Greek yogurt to boost the protein content while maintaining all that irresistible flakiness you love. These turned out so much better than expected - they have all the delicious flaky-ness you could want from a crescent roll.

What makes these special is the lamination technique borrowed from puff pastry making. By incorporating grated butter through a series of folds, you create those signature layers that make crescent rolls so addictive. The Greek yogurt not only adds protein but also keeps the rolls incredibly tender and moist. Whether you're making them for breakfast alongside scrambled eggs or as a side dish for soup, these rolls come together surprisingly quickly in just 30 minutes.

The best part? You can make these in an air fryer for convenience, or bake them traditionally in the oven. Either way, you'll end up with golden, flaky crescents that are actually good for you.

- **Higher protein content** - Greek yogurt adds 5-6g protein per roll compared to traditional crescent rolls
- **Simple ingredients** - Just five pantry staples create bakery-worthy results
- **Quick preparation** - Ready in 30 minutes from start to finish
- **Versatile cooking methods** - Works in both air fryer and conventional oven
- **Meal prep friendly** - Freeze unbaked crescents and cook fresh whenever you need them

## Tips

- Use full-fat Greek yogurt for the best texture and flakiness
- Keep the butter cold when grating - this is crucial for creating those flaky layers
- Don't overwork the dough or it will become tough instead of tender
- Brush with melted butter before cooking for extra richness and golden color
- Try adding herbs like rosemary or garlic powder to the dough for savory variations
- Store unbaked crescents in the freezer for quick future meals

## Ingredients

Greek yogurt is the star player here, bringing both protein and moisture to create tender, delicious rolls. You'll want to use a thick, full-fat Greek yogurt for the best results - the consistency matters for creating a workable dough. All-purpose flour provides structure, while baking powder gives these rolls their lift without requiring yeast or long rise times. A touch of salt enhances all the flavors. The grated butter is what creates those beautiful flaky layers through the folding technique - keep it cold from the fridge and grate it right before using. Unsalted butter is preferred so you can control the salt level in the dough.

## Instructions

1. **Make the dough base:** In a large bowl, mix together 100g Greek yogurt, 100g all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon baking powder, and 1 teaspoon salt. Stir until the mixture comes together and forms a cohesive ball of dough.

2. **Shape for lamination:** Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and flatten it into a rectangle shape using your hands or a rolling pin.

3. **Add the butter layers:** Grate 2 tablespoons of cold, unsalted butter directly onto the center of the dough rectangle. Fold the two long sides of the dough over the butter to enclose it completely.

4. **First fold and roll:** Use a rolling pin to gently seal the dough and press out the butter throughout. The goal is to distribute the butter in thin layers.

5. **Second fold:** Fold the dough in half and roll it out gently again with the rolling pin. You're creating layers with each fold, similar to making puff pastry.

6. **Final fold:** One more time - fold the dough in half and roll it out gently. This third fold creates multiple butter layers for maximum flakiness.

7. **Cut the crescents:** Using a knife or pizza cutter, cut the dough diagonally into triangles. You should get about 8 triangles.

8. **Shape into crescents:** Starting from the wide end of each triangle, roll the dough toward the point to create the classic crescent shape. Place each rolled crescent with the point tucked underneath.

9. **Cook in air fryer:** Place crescents in the air fryer basket, making sure they're not touching. Air fry at 350°F for 15 minutes until golden brown and cooked through.

10. **Serve warm:** Remove from the air fryer and let cool for just a minute or two before serving. These are best enjoyed warm when the layers are at their flakiest!

## FAQ

**Can I use a different type of yogurt?**
Greek yogurt works best due to its thick consistency and high protein content. Regular yogurt is too watery and will make the dough wet and difficult to work with. If you only have regular yogurt, strain it through cheesecloth for a few hours to thicken it first.

**Do I need an air fryer to make these?**
Not at all! You can bake these in a conventional oven at 375°F for 12-15 minutes until golden brown. The air fryer is just convenient, but the oven works beautifully too.

**Can I freeze these crescent rolls?**
Yes! Freeze the unbaked, shaped crescents on a baking sheet until solid, then transfer to a freezer bag. When you're ready to eat them, cook directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time. This makes them perfect for meal prep.

# Chinese-Style Steamed Eggs

**Created:** 2024
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Soft, decadent, and custard-like steamed eggs - a comforting Asian breakfast dish that's incredibly smooth and delicate.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

If you've never tried Chinese-style steamed eggs, you're in for a treat. This is Holistic Bravo's go-to breakfast for slow mornings when self-care is the priority. These eggs have a silky, custard-like texture that literally melts in your mouth - nothing like scrambled or fried eggs. The texture is closer to a savory flan or silken tofu, impossibly smooth and delicate.

This traditional Asian preparation transforms simple eggs into something truly special. The secret is in the gentle steaming method and the precise ratio of eggs to water. While the ingredient list is minimal - just eggs, water, and some flavorful toppings - the technique requires a bit of attention to detail. You'll want to remove all those air bubbles for a perfectly smooth surface, and the steaming needs to happen gently over low heat so the eggs set slowly without becoming rubbery.

First-time making these can be a revelation. They're perfect for those mornings when you want something comforting and protein-rich but also light and delicate. Served with soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame seeds, with optional additions like kimchi for crunch or chillies for heat, this is a breakfast that feels both nourishing and indulgent.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- **Incredibly silky texture** - Unlike any other egg preparation, truly custard-like
- **Simple ingredients** - Just eggs and water create something extraordinary
- **High protein** - 12-14g protein per serving from the eggs
- **Gentle and comforting** - Perfect for slow mornings or when you need comfort food

## Tips

- Removing ALL air bubbles is the key to a smooth, glossy surface
- Don't overcook - eggs should still jiggle slightly in the center when done
- Use low heat for gentle cooking; high heat will create holes and bubbles
- Cover the bowl with foil or plastic wrap with holes poked in it to prevent water from dripping onto the eggs
- Adjust water amount for different textures: less water makes firmer eggs, more water makes softer eggs
- Try adding a splash of dashi or chicken broth to the water for extra umami flavor
- Serve over rice for a complete meal
- Top with crispy fried shallots or garlic for textural contrast

## Ingredients

This recipe is deceptively simple with just two main ingredients. You'll need 2 fresh eggs and 1 cup of water - that 1:1 ratio of eggs to water is crucial for achieving the signature silky, custard-like texture. Using warm water (not cold) helps the eggs cook more evenly throughout. The magic happens in the steaming technique rather than in complicated ingredients.

For serving, soy sauce and seasoned rice vinegar are essential. The soy sauce provides that savory, umami depth, while the rice vinegar adds a subtle tang that brightens the rich eggs. A sprinkle of sesame seeds adds a touch of nuttiness and visual appeal. Beyond that, you can customize with optional additions. Kimchi adds crunch and fermented tang. Fresh or crushed chillies bring heat. Green scallions contribute freshness, texture, and a mild onion flavor that complements the eggs beautifully.

## Instructions

1. **Prepare your steaming setup:** Fill a pot or pan with water and bring it to a boil. Set up a steamer rack or basket inside the pot.

2. **Mix the egg base:** In a bowl, crack 2 eggs and beat them gently with a fork or whisk. Add 1 cup of warm water to the eggs and whisk together until well combined.

3. **Remove air bubbles (critical step!):** Look at the surface of your egg mixture - you'll see lots of little bubbles. Use a spoon to carefully skim and remove ALL of these air bubbles. This step is essential for achieving that perfectly smooth, silky surface. Take your time here; it makes a huge difference.

4. **Transfer to steaming bowl:** Pour the egg mixture into a heatproof bowl that fits comfortably in your steamer with the lid closed.

5. **Steam gently:** Place the bowl of eggs into the steamer. For very hot stoves, steam on low heat for 2 minutes, then turn OFF the heat completely and let the eggs continue steaming with the residual heat, covered, for an additional 7-8 minutes. For regular stoves, steam on low heat for 8-10 minutes total until the eggs are just set but still jiggly in the center.

6. **Check for doneness:** The eggs should be set but still have a gentle jiggle in the center, similar to panna cotta or silken tofu. They'll firm up slightly as they cool, so don't overcook them.

7. **Add toppings and serve:** Carefully remove the bowl from the steamer. Drizzle with soy sauce and seasoned rice vinegar. Sprinkle with sesame seeds. Add your choice of optional toppings like kimchi, chillies, or scallions.

8. **Enjoy immediately:** Serve while hot for the best texture and flavor. The eggs will be incredibly soft, smooth, and comforting.

## FAQ

**Why are my steamed eggs not smooth and have lots of holes?**
This happens when air bubbles aren't completely removed before steaming, or if the heat is too high. Make sure to carefully remove ALL bubbles from the surface before steaming, and keep the heat gentle and low throughout the cooking process.

**Can I use a microwave instead of steaming?**
You can, but the results won't be nearly as silky and smooth. If you must use a microwave, cook on 50% power in 30-second intervals, checking frequently, until just set. The texture won't be quite the same, but it will still be softer than regular scrambled eggs.

**How do I know when the steamed eggs are done?**
The eggs should be just set but still jiggly in the center - think of the texture of silken tofu or panna cotta. If you gently shake the bowl, the surface should have a slight wobble. They'll continue to firm up a bit as they cool.

# Spinach Ravioli

## Introduction

This is like eating a salad but so much better! Homemade ravioli might sound intimidating, but this dish actually comes together in 30 minutes or less - faster than you'd think.

The brilliance of this recipe is threefold. First, the spinach gets used in two ways: blended into the pasta dough for vibrant green noodles, and wilted in the sauce for serving. Second, the filling is made using a clever double-boiler technique that creates a rich, custard-like egg-Parmesan mixture that stays creamy inside the ravioli. Third, you're getting protein from both the eggs and the spinach, all packaged in gorgeous homemade pasta pillows.

Using up greens that are starting to wilt is the perfect application here - the blending and cooking process revives them completely. You'd never know these weren't made with the freshest greens from the farmers market. Whether you have a ravioli mold or just use a glass to cut circles, this recipe proves that homemade pasta is absolutely achievable on a weeknight.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- **Uses up wilting greens** - Perfect way to rescue spinach, arugula, or mixed greens before they go bad
- **Ready in 30 minutes** - Faster than most people think homemade pasta takes
- **High protein** - 22-24g protein per serving from eggs and Parmesan
- **Beautiful presentation** - Vibrant green pasta looks restaurant-quality
- **Clever filling technique** - Double-boiler method creates custard-like eggs without scrambling

## Tips

- Any leafy greens work: spinach, arugula, kale, or mixed greens
- If you don't have a ravioli mold, cut circles with a glass and fold into half-moons
- Keep extra flour handy while rolling to prevent sticking
- Don't let the egg mixture scramble - maintain gentle heat and whisk constantly
- The pasta dough should be elastic and smooth; if too sticky, add more flour
- Can substitute ricotta cheese mixed with the eggs for a more traditional filling
- Add fresh herbs like basil to the sauce for extra flavor
- Reserve extra pasta water to adjust sauce consistency if needed

## Ingredients

The pasta dough starts with about half a container of baby spinach and arugula mix (or any leafy greens you have) blended with 1 cup of water until bright green and smooth. You'll strain this to remove chunks, then mix it with approximately 2.5 cups of all-purpose flour - you might need a bit more or less depending on how much liquid you have. A pinch of salt enhances the flavor. The dough comes together quickly and rests while you make the filling.

The egg-Parmesan filling is genius in its simplicity. You'll crack 4 eggs into a bowl set over simmering water (double-boiler style) and whisk continuously as they gently heat. Adding ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese while whisking creates this incredible custardy paste that's rich and creamy, perfect for stuffing ravioli. The gentle indirect heat prevents scrambling - it's all about patience here.

For the garlic-spinach sauce, you'll use the remaining half of the greens along with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Adding pasta water to the sauce creates a silky coating that clings to the ravioli. Extra Parmesan, sea salt, and black pepper finish the dish beautifully.

## Instructions

1. **Make the green pasta liquid:** Add half of your spinach and greens to a blender along with 1 cup of water. Blend on high until completely smooth and bright green. Run this through a strainer to catch any chunks - you want just the smooth green liquid.

2. **Form the pasta dough:** Pour the green liquid into a large bowl. Gradually add flour, starting with 1 cup and continuing to add more (you'll use about 2.5 cups total) while mixing with a spoon or your hands. When it becomes too thick to stir, turn it out onto a floured surface and knead until you have a smooth, elastic dough ball.

3. **Rest the dough:** Cover the dough with a bowl and let it rest while you make the filling. This relaxes the gluten and makes rolling easier.

4. **Prepare the double boiler:** Bring a pot of water to a boil. Place a large heatproof bowl over the pot, making sure the bottom of the bowl doesn't touch the water.

5. **Make the egg-Parmesan filling:** Crack 4 eggs into the bowl set over the simmering water. Whisk continuously as the steam gently heats the eggs. Add a pinch of sea salt. As the eggs begin to slightly thicken, add ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese and continue whisking. Keep whisking until the mixture becomes thick and custardy like a paste. If it's cooking too quickly or starting to scramble, adjust the heat down or briefly remove from heat while continuing to whisk. Set aside when done.

6. **Roll and cut the pasta:** Divide your dough into 4 pieces. On a well-floured surface, roll each piece out very thinly into long sheets. If using a ravioli mold, make the sheets long enough to cover it.

7. **Fill the ravioli:** Place the first pasta sheet over your ravioli mold, pressing gently to create indentations. Spoon the egg-Parmesan custard into each indentation. Place a second pasta sheet over the top and press firmly to seal. Use your ravioli cutter to cut out individual pieces. If you don't have a mold, cut circles, place filling in the center, fold in half, and seal edges with a fork.

8. **Repeat:** Continue with remaining dough and filling until all ravioli are formed.

9. **Start the sauce:** Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add 1 heaping tablespoon minced garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add the remaining half-container of greens, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 teaspoon black pepper. Let the greens wilt and cook down.

10. **Cook the ravioli:** Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Gently add the ravioli and cook until they float to the surface, about 3-4 minutes.

11. **Combine:** Add 1 cup of pasta water to the sauce with the wilted greens. Use a slotted spoon or spider to transfer the cooked ravioli directly to the sauce pan. Toss gently until the ravioli are coated in the sauce.

12. **Serve:** Plate the ravioli with plenty of sauce. Top with extra grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of sea salt and black pepper. Enjoy immediately!

## FAQ

**Can I make the pasta dough ahead of time?**
Yes! Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When you're ready to use it, let it come to room temperature for about 30 minutes before rolling - this makes it much easier to work with.

**What if I don't have a ravioli mold?**
No problem! Use a glass or round cookie cutter to cut circles from your rolled pasta sheets. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle, fold it in half to make a half-moon shape, and press the edges together firmly. Use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative seal.

**How do I prevent the egg filling from scrambling?**
The key is gentle, indirect heat from the double boiler. Keep whisking constantly, and if you notice the eggs cooking too quickly or starting to get grainy, immediately remove the bowl from the heat while continuing to whisk. The residual heat will continue cooking without scrambling.

3. **Bake the rice:** Place the baking dish in the oven and bake at 400°F for 15 minutes.

Oyster mushrooms have a delicate, slightly sweet flavor and become wonderfully crispy when fried.

You'll need minced garlic - as much as you like, since garlic makes everything better. Leftover pesto sauce is ideal (waste not!), but jarred pesto or freshly made pesto all work beautifully. ## Instructions

1. **Boil the pasta water:** Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Now here's the important part - salt it generously. We're talking really salt it, until your arm hurts from shaking the salt container. Seriously salted pasta water is the secret to flavorful pasta. This is where all the seasoning happens.

2. **Fry the mushrooms:** While the water heats, add a good glug of olive oil to a large pan over medium-high heat. Add your oyster mushrooms (or mushrooms of choice) and let them fry without moving them too much. You want them to get nice and crispy with golden-brown edges.

3. **Add the garlic:** Once the mushrooms are beautifully crispy, add your minced garlic to the pan. Sauté just until fragrant, about 30 seconds to 1 minute. Don't let it burn.

The starch released into the pasta cooking water acts as an emulsifier, helping the sauce cling to the pasta and creating that restaurant-quality glossy coating. It also helps thin out thick sauces like pesto without adding more oil. Never skip reserving that pasta water!

# Classic Shortbread Cookies

**Created:** 2024
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Simple, melt-in-your-mouth shortbread cookies made with just three ingredients.

[IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]

## Introduction

Holistic Bravo is a self-proclaimed sugar addict, and honestly, who can blame her? Sometimes you just need something sweet at the end of the day - not anything big or elaborate, just a little something to signal to your body that you're done eating. These shortbread cookies are perfect for exactly that moment.

She made these gingerbread-man-shaped shortbread cookies again, and this time they turned out so much better. The secret? Patience. By taking the time to let the dough rest in the fridge for a whole hour, the butter firms up and the gluten relaxes, creating that perfect tender, crumbly, melt-in-your-mouth texture that makes shortbread so addictive.

True shortbread is magnificently simple - just three ingredients create these buttery, delicate cookies that are somehow both crispy and tender at the same time. They're perfect for holiday baking, gift-giving, or just keeping a batch around for those moments when you need a sweet treat. The simplicity is the point - no fancy add-ins needed, just pure buttery perfection.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- **Only three ingredients** - Butter, sugar, and flour create perfection
- **Melt-in-your-mouth texture** - That signature crumbly, tender shortbread texture
- **Perfect for any occasion** - Holidays, gifts, tea time, or everyday treats
- **Easy to customize** - Add vanilla, lemon, or spices for variations
- **Great for gift-giving** - Beautiful when decorated with icing

## Tips

- Don't skip the chilling time - it makes a huge difference in texture and prevents spreading
- Room temperature butter is essential - leave it out for 30-60 minutes before baking
- Don't overbake - cookies should be pale with just barely golden edges
- Roll dough between two sheets of parchment paper to prevent sticking
- Add 1 teaspoon vanilla extract for classic vanilla shortbread
- Mix in lemon zest for bright citrus shortbread
- Dip cooled cookies in melted chocolate for an elegant touch
- Add spices like cinnamon or ginger for spiced shortbread variation
- Press dough into a pan and cut into squares after baking for easier preparation

## Ingredients

Classic shortbread requires only three ingredients, and the quality really matters since there's nowhere to hide. You'll need 1½ sticks of butter - it must be at room temperature, not cold from the fridge and not melted. Leave it out on the counter for 30-60 minutes before you start baking. Room temperature butter creams properly with the sugar and creates the right texture. Using unsalted butter is best so you can control the salt level, though a pinch of salt can be added if desired.

Powdered sugar (also called confectioners' sugar or icing sugar) is crucial here - don't substitute granulated sugar. The powdered sugar dissolves completely into the butter and creates that fine, delicate crumb that makes shortbread so tender. You'll use ½ cup. Finally, 1½ cups of all-purpose flour provides structure. The ratio of these three ingredients is what creates that signature shortbread texture - high butter content for richness and tenderness, just enough sugar for sweetness without being overpowering, and flour to hold it all together.

## Instructions

1. **Cream butter and sugar:** In a large bowl, combine 1½ sticks of room temperature butter with ½ cup powdered sugar. Mix together using a hand mixer, stand mixer, or wooden spoon until well incorporated and fluffy. The mixture should be smooth and lightened in color.

2. **Add flour:** Add 1½ cups all-purpose flour to the butter-sugar mixture. Continue mixing until a soft dough forms. The dough will start crumbly but will come together as you mix. Don't overmix once it forms a cohesive dough or the cookies will be tough.

3. **Shape and chill:** Shape the dough into a ball or disk. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or cover the bowl. Place in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour. This step is crucial - don't skip it! The chilling firms up the butter and relaxes the gluten for the best texture.

4. **Preheat oven:** About 15 minutes before you're ready to bake, preheat your oven to 325°F. This relatively low temperature ensures gentle baking for tender cookies.

5. **Roll out dough:** Remove the chilled dough from the refrigerator. On a generously floured surface (or between two sheets of parchment paper), roll the dough out to about ¼ inch thickness. If the dough is too hard to roll, let it sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to soften slightly.

6. **Cut shapes:** Use cookie cutters to cut the dough into desired shapes. Gingerbread men work great, but stars, circles, or any shape you like will work beautifully.

7. **Transfer to baking sheet:** Place the cut cookies on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart. They won't spread much, so they can be fairly close together.

8. **Bake:** Bake at 325°F for 12-15 minutes. The cookies should be barely golden at the edges but still pale in the center. Don't overbake - pale cookies are tender cookies!

9. **Cool on sheet:** Remove from the oven and let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. They'll be very soft when hot but will firm up as they cool.

10. **Cool completely:** Transfer cookies to a wire rack to cool completely before decorating or serving. Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks.

## FAQ

**Why do I need to chill the dough for so long?**
Chilling serves two important purposes. First, it firms up the butter, which prevents the cookies from spreading too much during baking and helps them hold their shape. Second, it allows the gluten in the flour to relax, which results in more tender, less tough cookies. It really does make a huge difference!

**Can I freeze the dough?**
Yes! Shortbread dough freezes beautifully. Shape it into a log, wrap tightly in plastic wrap, and freeze for up to 3 months. When you're ready to bake, you can either thaw it in the fridge and roll it out, or slice frozen dough into rounds and bake, adding 1-2 extra minutes to the baking time.

**Why are my cookies too crumbly and falling apart?**
This usually means they were overbaked. Shortbread should be removed from the oven when barely golden at the edges. If they weren't overbaked, the dough might not have been mixed enough to properly bring the butter and flour together - it needs to form a cohesive dough.

2. **Prepare tomatoes:** Halve your 2-3 tomatoes. You should have enough halves to cover most of the bottom of your pan.

3. **Cook tomatoes:** Place the tomato halves cut-side down in the hot pan. Let them cook for 2-3 minutes without moving them. This allows them to soften and release their delicious juices into the pan.

4. **Add eggs:** Crack 3 fresh eggs directly on top of the tomatoes in the pan, spacing them out evenly. The tomatoes will hold the eggs in place.

5. **Season:** Sprinkle the eggs with salt and pepper to taste.

6. **Add microgreens:** Scatter bok choy microgreens (or your choice of microgreens or greens) over everything in the pan.

7. **Cover and cook:** Place a lid on the pan to trap the steam. This helps cook the tops of the eggs evenly. Cook for 5-7 minutes, depending on how you like your eggs. For runny yolks, aim for 5 minutes; for fully set yolks, go for 7 minutes or more.

8. **Check doneness:** The egg whites should be completely set and opaque, while the yolks can be cooked to your preference.

9. **Serve:** Carefully transfer everything to a plate using a spatula, keeping the eggs and tomatoes together as much as possible.

10. **Add pickled vegetables:** Serve with pickled cabbage (or sauerkraut/kimchi) on the side for gut health benefits and a tangy contrast to the rich eggs.

11. **Enjoy:** Eat immediately while everything is hot. Happy holidays!

## FAQ

**Can I use regular greens instead of microgreens?**
Absolutely! Baby spinach, arugula, or chopped kale all work great. Microgreens are lovely because they're tender and packed with nutrients, but regular greens are just as delicious and nutritious.

**What if I don't have pickled vegetables?**
The main dish is still complete and delicious without them. You can serve with fresh vegetables, avocado, or just skip the side entirely. The fermented vegetables are a nice addition for gut health but certainly not required.

**Do I need to cover the pan?**
Covering the pan creates steam that helps cook the tops of the eggs evenly, especially the whites. If you prefer, you can leave it uncovered and baste the eggs with the tomato juices using a spoon, though this requires more attention.

# Chinese Souffle Steamed Eggs

**Created:** 2024
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

Silky smooth Chinese-style steamed eggs with a flan-like texture - a decadent breakfast that takes patience but is worth every minute.

## Introduction

If you're bored of traditional scrambled eggs, it's time to try something new and completely different. These Chinese soufflé eggs are actually very easy to make - the hardest part is simply having the patience to let the eggs cook at a slow rate. But trust Holistic Bravo when she says: you can do this too. Just trust the process.

The texture of these eggs is similar to a flan or panna cotta - very smooth, silky, and absolutely delicious on the palate. It took her probably 5 to 10 times of trying this dish before she got it right, before she achieved this perfect flan-like consistency. She kept overcooking them at first, and they'd end up turning into an omelette with a bunch of bubbles on the top instead of that beautifully smooth surface.

But she finally cracked the code (pun intended), and now you can benefit from all that trial and error. The recipe itself is deceptively simple - just eggs, boiling water, and love. The technique is where the magic happens. This is for those mornings when you have 20 minutes to kill and want a truly decadent breakfast. The result is impossibly tender eggs with that perfect little jiggle when you cut into them.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- **Unique texture** - Like flan or panna cotta, completely different from any other egg dish
- **Simple ingredients** - Just eggs, water, and your choice of toppings
- **Impressive results** - Restaurant-quality technique you can master at home
- **High protein** - 12-14g protein per serving from eggs
- **Worth the learning curve** - Once you nail the technique, you'll make these again and again

## Tips

- Removing ALL air bubbles is absolutely critical for that smooth, silky surface
- Turn OFF the heat before adding the egg bowl - this is the key to avoiding overcooking
- Patience is essential - resist the urge to increase the heat or check too early
- The eggs should jiggle like panna cotta when done, not be completely firm
- If you don't have a steamer rack, use a heat-safe bowl elevated on something in a pot with less water
- Add toppings like green onions, sesame oil, or crispy fried shallots for extra flavor
- For savory variation, mix in a bit of chicken broth instead of plain water
- Serve with congee (rice porridge) for a traditional Chinese breakfast
- Can be served warm or at room temperature

## Ingredients

This is minimalist cooking at its finest. You need just 2 eggs and ⅓ cup of boiling water. The ratio of eggs to water is crucial - this 2:⅓ proportion creates that signature custard-like texture. The water must be boiling when you add it to the eggs to start the gentle cooking process. And then there's the most important ingredient: LOVE. Holistic Bravo insists on this, and honestly, the patience and care you put into this dish really do show in the results.

For serving, you'll drizzle the finished eggs with soy sauce for that essential savory, umami flavor and rice vinegar for a subtle tang that brightens the rich eggs. These two simple seasonings are traditional and perfect - they don't overwhelm the delicate egg flavor but enhance it beautifully. If you want to get fancy, you can add green onions, a drizzle of sesame oil, or crispy fried shallots, but the eggs are stunning with just soy sauce and vinegar.

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the steamer:** Fill a pan with water and bring it to a rolling boil. Have your steamer rack ready to go.

2. **Mix eggs and water:** Crack 2 eggs into a heatproof bowl. Measure ⅓ cup of the boiling water and whisk it into the eggs until very well combined. The boiling water will slightly cook the eggs as you whisk - this is normal and good!

3. **Remove air bubbles (critical!):** This is the most important step for achieving that perfectly smooth surface. Look at your egg mixture - you'll see lots of little bubbles floating on top and throughout. Take a spoon and meticulously remove ALL of these bubbles. Scoop them off, pop them, do whatever it takes to get rid of every single one. This is what gives you that gorgeous, smooth, silky top without holes or pockmarks. Don't rush this step!

4. **Set up for steaming:** Place your steamer rack over the pot of boiling water. Now here's the critical part: Turn OFF the heat completely and move the pan away from the burner. This is essential! If the heat is too high, you'll end up with an omelette instead of silky steamed eggs.

5. **Add the eggs:** Carefully place your bowl with the egg mixture onto the steamer rack.

6. **Cover tightly:** Put the lid on the pot securely. Make sure it's tightly sealed.

7. **Be patient:** Now comes the hard part - waiting. The residual heat and steam will cook the eggs slowly and gently. This is where patience is absolutely key. Let it steam undisturbed for about 15-20 minutes. Don't peek! Resist the temptation.

8. **Check for doneness:** After 15-20 minutes, carefully remove the lid. The eggs should be just set but still have a beautiful jiggle in the center, similar to panna cotta or silken tofu. If you gently shake the bowl, the surface should wobble slightly. That perfect jiggle means they're done!

9. **Serve:** Drizzle the steamed eggs with soy sauce and rice vinegar.

10. **Cut and enjoy:** Use a spoon to cut into the eggs and reveal that perfect, silky, smooth texture. Eat while warm and marvel at what you just accomplished!

## FAQ

**Why did my eggs turn into an omelette instead of the smooth texture?**
The heat was too high or the cooking was too fast. This is the most common mistake! Remember to turn OFF the heat completely before adding the bowl of eggs, and let the residual heat and steam do the cooking slowly. Patience is absolutely essential here.

**Why does my steamed egg have holes and bubbles throughout?**
You didn't remove enough air bubbles before steaming, or the heat was too high causing the eggs to bubble during cooking. Take your time removing every single bubble from the surface before steaming, and make sure the heat is off.

**How do I know when they're perfectly done?**
The eggs should be just set but still jiggle significantly in the center when you gently shake the bowl. Think of the texture of silken tofu or panna cotta - there should be that beautiful wobble. They'll firm up very slightly as they cool, so don't overcook them trying to make them completely firm.
