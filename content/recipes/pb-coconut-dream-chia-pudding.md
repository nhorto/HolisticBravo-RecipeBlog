When I created this chia pudding, my goal was simple: a high-protein, make-ahead breakfast that feels indulgent but fuels your body the right way. This PB Coconut Dream Chia Pudding delivers exactly that. Rich coconut milk, warming cinnamon, and a thick layer of peanut butter drizzle come together into something that tastes like a tropical dessert but packs a serious nutritional punch. At over 26 grams of protein and nearly 18 grams of fiber per serving, this is the kind of breakfast that keeps me full for hours.

The magic of this recipe is that it requires absolutely no cooking. I blend a few simple ingredients, layer on the peanut butter topping, and let it set overnight in the fridge. By morning, I've got a thick, creamy pudding waiting for me - no stovetop, no oven, no effort. That's the whole point. I designed this for busy mornings, meal prep Sundays, or anytime you want something nourishing without spending time over a hot stove.

What makes this stand out from other chia puddings is the combination of coconut milk and PB powder. The coconut milk brings richness and healthy fats that help stabilize your energy throughout the morning, while the PB powder adds plant-based protein without the heaviness of full-fat peanut butter. Topped with your favorite nuts, seeds, or fresh fruit, this pudding becomes a customizable bowl of goodness that works as breakfast, dessert, or an afternoon snack.

FIELD: tips
========================================
- I always use full-fat coconut milk for the creamiest, most satisfying texture - light coconut milk will work but the pudding will be thinner
- I like to blend the coconut milk mixture with a hand blender or regular blender to break up the chia seeds slightly, which creates a smoother base rather than a gritty texture
- When I mix the PB powder, I add water one tablespoon at a time until I reach a thick but pourable consistency - you want it to drizzle, not clump
- I've found that letting the pudding set for at least 6 hours gives great results, but overnight is best for the thickest, most pudding-like texture
- I store mine in individual glass jars or containers so I can grab one each morning for the ultimate grab-and-go breakfast
- Don't skimp on the cinnamon - I love how it adds warmth and pairs beautifully with the coconut and peanut butter
- I top mine with sliced banana, berries, shredded coconut, or crushed peanuts right before serving for extra texture and flavor

FIELD: ingredientsOverview
========================================
The base of this pudding starts with half a cup of coconut milk, which brings all the creamy, tropical richness I need. Full-fat canned coconut milk is my go-to here because the fat content helps the chia seeds gel into a thick, luxurious pudding rather than a watery mess. If you only have light coconut milk, it'll still work - just expect a slightly thinner consistency.

Chia seeds are the real workhorse of this recipe. I use 1 tablespoon of chia seeds, and these tiny seeds do something incredible - they absorb liquid and expand into a gel-like texture that gives the pudding its signature thickness. Beyond texture, chia seeds bring healthy omega-3 fats, fiber, and antioxidants that support digestion and reduce inflammation. One teaspoon of ground cinnamon rounds out the base with warm, cozy flavor and even more anti-inflammatory benefits, while 1 teaspoon of monk fruit sweetener adds just a touch of natural sweetness without spiking your blood sugar.

The peanut butter layer is where the protein really shines. I use a quarter cup of PB powder mixed with about 4 tablespoons of water to create a thick, drizzly peanut butter sauce that's packed with plant-based protein but without the heavy fat content of regular peanut butter. PB powder is seriously my go-to for hitting protein goals. You can find it at most grocery stores - PB2 and PBfit are popular brands that I've had great results with.

========================================
FIELD: instructionsOverview
========================================
1. I start by adding half a cup of coconut milk to a medium mixing bowl. Then I add 1 teaspoon of monk fruit sweetener, 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, and 1 tablespoon of chia seeds to the bowl. I give everything a quick stir to combine before blending.

2. Using a hand blender (or transferring to a regular blender), I blend all the ingredients together until the mixture is smooth and the chia seeds are partially broken down. I find this step is important because it creates a creamier, more uniform base. You should see the mixture become slightly thicker and more cohesive after blending - that's exactly what you want.

3. I pour the blended mixture into a glass jar or container. Something with a lid works best since this is going straight into the fridge. A mason jar, small Tupperware, or glass meal prep container all work perfectly here.

4. In a separate small bowl, I add a quarter cup of PB powder. I add water one tablespoon at a time, stirring between each addition, until I reach my desired consistency. I usually use about 4 tablespoons total, but the exact amount depends on the brand of PB powder. I'm looking for a thick, smooth, pourable texture - think drizzle, not paste.

5. I carefully pour the peanut butter mixture over the top of the chia pudding base. You can swirl it in slightly for a marbled look, or leave it as a distinct layer on top - both ways are delicious. The PB layer will slowly sink and meld with the chia base as it sets, creating beautiful ribbons of peanut butter throughout.

6. I cover the container and place it in the refrigerator to set overnight, or for a minimum of 6 hours. The chia seeds need time to absorb the liquid and expand into that thick, pudding-like consistency. When I pull it out in the morning, it should be thick, creamy, and ready to eat. I top mine with my favorite nuts, seeds, shredded coconut, or fresh fruit and enjoy.

FIELD: faq
========================================
**Can I use regular peanut butter instead of PB powder?**
Absolutely, but keep in mind it will change the nutrition profile significantly. Regular peanut butter adds more fat and calories, while PB powder gives you that peanut butter flavor with a fraction of the fat and a higher protein-to-calorie ratio. If I'm out of PB powder, I use about 2 tablespoons of regular PB thinned with a little water or coconut milk so it's still drizzleable.

**How long does this keep in the fridge?**
I find this pudding stores beautifully for up to 5 days in the refrigerator, making it perfect for my weekly meal prep. Just keep it in a sealed container. The texture actually gets better over the first day or two as the chia seeds fully absorb the liquid. I always add any fresh toppings right before eating so they stay fresh and crunchy.

**Can I use a different milk instead of coconut milk?**
You can swap in any milk you like - almond milk, oat milk, soy milk, or cashew milk all work. However, coconut milk gives this recipe its signature richness and tropical flavor, which is why I always come back to it. If you use a thinner milk, I'd recommend adding an extra teaspoon of chia seeds to make sure the pudding sets properly.

**Is monk fruit sweetener necessary?**
Not at all. I sometimes substitute maple syrup, agave, or stevia, or just leave it out entirely if I'm not in the mood. The coconut milk and peanut butter provide natural sweetness on their own. I use monk fruit because it's a zero-calorie natural sweetener that doesn't affect blood sugar, but any sweetener you enjoy will work.

**Can I make this without a blender?**
Yes! I've made it by simply whisking everything together vigorously by hand. The pudding will have more visible chia seeds and a slightly different texture, but it'll still be delicious. Just make sure to whisk well so there are no clumps of cinnamon or sweetener.

FIELD: recipeCardIngredients
========================================
- 1/2 cup coconut milk
- 1 tsp monk fruit sweetener
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1/4 cup PB powder
- 4 tbsp water
- Optional: nuts, seeds, or fresh fruit for topping

========================================
FIELD: recipeCardInstructions
========================================
1. Combine coconut milk, monk fruit sweetener, cinnamon, and chia seeds in a bowl.
2. Blend with a hand blender until smooth.
3. Pour mixture into a glass container.
4. In a separate bowl, mix PB powder with water one tablespoon at a time until thick and smooth.
5. Pour PB mixture over the chia pudding base.
6. Cover and refrigerate overnight (minimum 6 hours).
7. Top with nuts, seeds, or fruit before serving.
