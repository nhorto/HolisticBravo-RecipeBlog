FIELD: tips
========================================
- I always give the mixture a really good froth or stir right after combining - chia seeds love to clump, and I want them evenly distributed for a smooth pudding
- I let it sit for at least 6-8 hours for the best texture, but overnight is my go-to
- If my pudding seems too thick in the morning, I stir in a splash of water or milk to loosen it up to my preferred consistency
- If it seems too thin, I add a few more chia seeds and let it sit for another 30 minutes
- Don't skimp out on the mini chocolate chips on top - they add a satisfying crunch and that little indulgence that makes this feel like dessert
- I make this three times a week and prep multiple jars at once for a grab-and-go breakfast situation
- Mason jars or small containers with lids work perfectly for storing individual servings - I keep a stack of them ready to go
- For an extra creamy, richer version, I swap the water for oat milk, almond milk, or any milk of my choice

FIELD: ingredientsOverview
========================================
The foundation of this pudding starts with one scoop (32 grams) of chocolate protein powder. This is where the bulk of my protein comes from, and it doubles as the flavoring agent - so I always recommend choosing a brand you genuinely enjoy the taste of. I use a plant-based chocolate protein, but whey, casein, or any protein powder you love will work beautifully here. The chocolate flavor is what gives this pudding its rich, dessert-like quality without needing added sugar or cocoa.

Next up are the chia seeds - you'll need just 1 tablespoon. These tiny seeds are seriously powerful. When they absorb liquid, they expand and create a gel-like pudding texture that's thick, creamy, and satisfying. Beyond the texture magic, chia seeds bring omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and antioxidants to the table. They're also what gives this pudding its slow-digesting energy, keeping you fueled steadily instead of crashing an hour later.

My liquid base is 1 cup of water, which keeps the calorie count low and lets the chocolate protein flavor shine. That said, if you want extra protein and creaminess, swapping the water for a milk or milk substitute is an excellent move - I love using oat milk, almond milk, or coconut milk because they add their own layer of richness. Finally, I top it off with 2 tablespoons of mini chocolate chips once the pudding has set. They add a sweet crunch and make every bite feel like a real treat. You can also get creative with additional toppings like fresh berries, sliced banana, nuts, or a drizzle of nut butter.

========================================
FIELD: instructionsOverview
========================================
1. I start by adding one scoop (32 grams) of my favorite chocolate protein powder and 1 tablespoon of chia seeds to a jar, bowl, or container. I give them a quick stir to combine the dry ingredients before adding the liquid - this helps prevent the protein powder from clumping when it hits the water.

2. Next, I pour in 1 cup of water (or my preferred milk or milk substitute for a creamier result). Using a small frother or whisk, I blend everything together until the mixture is smooth and the protein powder is fully dissolved. I find that a milk frother works especially well here because it breaks up any clumps and ensures the chia seeds are evenly distributed throughout the liquid rather than settling in one spot.

3. I give the mixture one final stir, then cover the container and place it in the refrigerator. I let it sit overnight, or for at least 6-8 hours. During this time, the chia seeds absorb the liquid and expand, transforming the mixture from a thin chocolate drink into a thick, creamy pudding. You'll know it's ready when the texture is gel-like and holds its shape on a spoon.

4. When I'm ready to enjoy, I pull the pudding from the fridge and give it a good stir to break up any clumps and even out the texture. I top it with 2 tablespoons of mini chocolate chips and whatever other toppings I'm in the mood for - fresh fruit, crushed nuts, seeds, coconut flakes, or a spoonful of nut butter all work wonderfully. Serve it right in the jar or transfer to a bowl, and enjoy your high-protein chocolate treat.

FIELD: faq
========================================
**Can I make this vegan?**
Absolutely. I just use a plant-based protein powder and check that my chocolate chips are dairy-free. Many brands offer vegan mini chocolate chips that taste just as good. With those two swaps, this recipe is fully vegan with no compromise on flavor or texture.

**How long does this keep in the fridge?**
I find that chia pudding stores beautifully in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, making it one of my best meal prep recipes. I just keep it covered and add my toppings fresh each morning for the best texture and crunch.

**Can I use something other than water?**
Definitely - and I'd actually encourage it if you want an even richer pudding. Oat milk, almond milk, coconut milk, or regular dairy milk all work great and will add extra creaminess and protein. I just keep in mind that swapping to milk will increase the calorie count slightly, but it's absolutely worth it for the added flavor and nutrition.

**Why is my pudding lumpy or not setting properly?**
This usually comes down to one of two things: either the chia seeds weren't stirred well enough at the start and clumped together, or it hasn't sat long enough. I always make sure to froth or whisk thoroughly when combining, and I give it a full 6-8 hours minimum. If it still seems thin, I add a few more chia seeds and let it sit for another 30 minutes.

**Can I heat this up?**
You can warm it gently on the stovetop or in the microwave for a cozy warm pudding option. I just heat it slowly and stir frequently - high heat can affect the protein powder's texture. I usually prefer it cold, but warm chia pudding with chocolate chips is seriously comforting on a chilly morning.
