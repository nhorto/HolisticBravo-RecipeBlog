FIELD: tips
========================================
- I always use full-fat Greek yogurt for the richest, most cheesecake-like flavor and texture - though any percentage works
- I actually prefer frozen blueberries here because they release more juice as they thaw, creating that gorgeous jammy layer
- I let the pudding sit for at least 6 hours, but overnight (8-10 hours) gives me the best chia seed texture
- I like to layer the blueberries on the bottom and the chia mixture on top for the prettiest presentation when you flip your spoon through it
- Mason jars or small glass containers are my go-to for meal prepping multiple servings
- If you like a thicker pudding, I recommend adding an extra teaspoon of chia seeds and letting them absorb more liquid
- Don't skimp out on the overnight rest - I've learned that chia seeds need time to fully bloom and develop that signature pudding texture
- I love topping mine with granola, sliced almonds, or a drizzle of nut butter right before eating for added crunch and nutrition

FIELD: ingredientsOverview
========================================
The base of this pudding is 1 cup of Greek yogurt, which is where all that incredible protein comes from. Greek yogurt brings a thick, tangy creaminess that genuinely mimics the richness of cheesecake filling. I use whatever brand I have on hand - just make sure it's a good quality Greek yogurt with a high protein count. If you want to keep this fully plant-based, I'd suggest swapping in a non-dairy yogurt like coconut or cashew-based, though the protein content will be lower.

Next up are the chia seeds - my go-to is 1 tablespoon. These tiny seeds are nutritional powerhouses, packed with omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and plant-based protein. When they sit in moisture overnight, they absorb liquid and develop a gel-like coating that transforms the whole mixture into a thick, spoonable pudding. That's the whole point of the overnight rest - giving those chia seeds time to work their magic.

For the fruit layer, I use 1 cup of frozen blueberries. Blueberries are loaded with antioxidants, vitamin C, and fiber, and they bring a beautiful natural sweetness to the pudding as they thaw. Finally, 1 tablespoon of agave nectar ties everything together with a gentle, clean sweetness that doesn't overpower the fruit. If you prefer a sweeter pudding, I say go ahead and add an additional tablespoon of agave on top before serving - life is all about balance.

FIELD: instructionsOverview
========================================
1. I start by adding 1 cup of Greek yogurt to a medium mixing bowl. I spoon in 1 tablespoon of agave nectar and 1 tablespoon of chia seeds. The agave blends most easily when the yogurt is at a slightly cool room temperature, but straight from the fridge works just fine too.

2. Using a spoon or small whisk, I mix everything together until the chia seeds and agave are evenly distributed throughout the yogurt. I want a smooth, consistent mixture with no clumps of chia seeds hiding at the bottom. I take my time here - about 30 seconds of good stirring should do it.

3. Now it's time to layer. I grab my serving jar or bowl and add a generous layer of frozen blueberries to the bottom - roughly half of the cup. I spoon about half of the chia pudding mixture on top, then add the remaining blueberries, and finish with the rest of the chia mixture. You can also do a simple two-layer version with all the blueberries on the bottom and all the pudding on top - either way looks and tastes amazing.

4. I cover the jar or bowl and place it in the refrigerator overnight, or for at least 6 to 8 hours. As the pudding rests, the chia seeds will absorb moisture and swell, thickening the yogurt into a rich, creamy pudding consistency. The frozen blueberries will slowly thaw and release their beautiful purple juices, creating a natural fruit compote layer that swirls into the pudding when you dig in.

5. When I'm ready to enjoy, I give it a gentle stir or eat it layered - completely up to you. I top mine with an extra drizzle of agave if I'm feeling something sweeter, or add a handful of nuts, seeds, or granola for some crunch. This pudding is best consumed within 5 days, stored in the fridge.

FIELD: faq
========================================
**Can I use fresh blueberries instead of frozen?**
Absolutely! Fresh blueberries work well, but I actually prefer frozen for this recipe. Frozen berries release more juice as they thaw overnight, creating that beautiful jammy, compote-like layer that makes this taste like real cheesecake. If you use fresh, I'd recommend lightly mashing a few with a fork to release some of their juices.

**Can I make this vegan?**
Yes! I'd recommend swapping the Greek yogurt for a plant-based yogurt like coconut, cashew, or soy-based yogurt. Just keep in mind that the protein content will be lower with most non-dairy yogurts. I sometimes add a scoop of plant-based protein powder to the mixture to boost the protein back up. Agave and chia seeds are already naturally vegan.

**How long does this keep in the fridge?**
This pudding stores beautifully in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, making it an excellent meal prep option. I've noticed the texture actually improves over the first day or two as the chia seeds continue to absorb and thicken. Just keep it sealed in an airtight container. I don't recommend freezing it, so keep it in the fridge.

**Can I use a different sweetener instead of agave?**
Of course. Maple syrup, honey (if you're not strictly vegan), or even a few drops of stevia all work as substitutes. I use agave because it has a mild, neutral sweetness that doesn't compete with the blueberry flavor, which is why it's my go-to here, but use whatever sweetener you have on hand.

**What other fruits can I use instead of blueberries?**
Mixed berries, strawberries, raspberries, or mango all work wonderfully. I love blueberries because they give you that classic cheesecake vibe and are particularly high in antioxidants, but this recipe is really flexible. Just keep the measurement at about 1 cup of fruit for the right ratio.
