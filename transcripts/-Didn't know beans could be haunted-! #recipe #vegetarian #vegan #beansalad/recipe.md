### Description
This bean salad is a favorite that not only tastes delicious but packs a serious nutrient punch! High in protein, iron, magnesium, and zinc, with plenty of fiber to keep you satiated. It's especially great for those with PCOS or high cortisol. The longer it sits with the dressing in the fridge, the more the flavors develop.

### Ingredients

**Dressing:**
- 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp stone ground mustard
- 2 tbsp hot honey
- 1 tsp sea salt
- Juice of 1 lemon

**Beans (mix as many or as few as you like):**
- 1 can (15 oz) garbanzo beans (chickpeas), drained and rinsed
- 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans (white beans), drained and rinsed
- 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup edamame (fresh or frozen and thawed)

**Vegetables (add as many or as few as you like):**
- 1 cucumber, diced
- 1/2 red onion, diced
- 1 cup tri-colored bell peppers, diced
- Optional additions: tomatoes, zucchini, carrots, radishes, jalapeños

**Optional Topping:**
- Feta cheese, crumbled (omit for vegan version)

### Instructions
1. In a large bowl, prepare the dressing by whisking together apple cider vinegar, olive oil, stone ground mustard, hot honey, sea salt, and lemon juice.
2. Drain and rinse your chosen beans (garbanzo, cannellini, black beans, edamame).
3. Add all beans to the bowl with dressing.
4. Dice cucumber, red onion, and tri-colored bell peppers.
5. Add all diced vegetables to the bean mixture.
6. Toss everything together until well coated with dressing.
7. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to let flavors meld (longer is better).
8. Before serving, add crumbled feta cheese on top if desired.
9. Enjoy throughout the week - flavors improve with time!

### About the Ingredients
Beans are powerhouses of nutrition, providing plant-based protein, fiber, iron, magnesium, and zinc. The variety of beans creates different textures and flavors. Apple cider vinegar and lemon juice provide acidity and may help with blood sugar regulation. Stone ground mustard adds tang and emulsifies the dressing. Hot honey provides a sweet-spicy kick that balances the acidity. The colorful vegetables add vitamins, antioxidants, and crunch.

### Tips & Variations
- Use canned beans for convenience or cook dried beans from scratch
- The longer it marinates, the better it tastes - make it the night before
- Customize bean combination based on preference or what you have on hand
- Add jalapeños or hot sauce if you want it spicier
- Try different mustards (Dijon, whole grain, spicy brown)
- Swap hot honey for regular honey + red pepper flakes
- Add fresh herbs like cilantro, parsley, or basil
- Include cherry tomatoes for extra color and flavor
- Add avocado just before serving for healthy fats
- Keep feta separate for vegan friends

### FAQ
1. **Can I use canned beans?** Absolutely! Canned beans are convenient and work perfectly. Just drain and rinse them well.
2. **How long does this keep in the fridge?** Properly stored in an airtight container, this salad keeps for 5-7 days. The flavors actually improve over time.
3. **Why is this good for PCOS or high cortisol?** The high fiber content helps regulate blood sugar, beans provide plant-based protein, and the nutrient density supports hormonal balance.
