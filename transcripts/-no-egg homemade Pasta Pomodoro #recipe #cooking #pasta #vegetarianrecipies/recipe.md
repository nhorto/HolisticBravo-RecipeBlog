# No-Egg Homemade Pasta Pomodoro
Simple vegan pasta made with just water and flour, paired with a rustic pomodoro sauce featuring both air-fried and stovetop tomatoes for complex sweet and acidic flavors.

## Recipe Card

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**Total Time:** 35 minutes
**Servings:** 2 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 400-450 calories per serving

### Description

This dish showcases how simple, quality ingredients can create restaurant-worthy meals. The pasta is made with just water and flour - no eggs needed, making it completely vegan (before cheese is added). The creator actually prefers this water-based pasta over egg pasta because it absorbs sauce better and has a superior texture. The pomodoro sauce is the star, using fresh tomatoes cooked two ways: air-fried for sweet, jammy notes and stovetop for bright acidity. With only 4 ingredients or less in the sauce, it's simplicity perfected.

### Ingredients

**For the Pasta:**
- All-purpose flour (approximately 100g per serving)
- Water (approximately 50g per serving)

**For the Pomodoro Sauce:**
- 1 pack fresh tomatoes (divided for two cooking methods)
- Olive oil
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- Pasta water
- Block of parmesan cheese (optional, for finishing)

### Instructions

1. **Make the Pasta Dough:** Combine flour and water in 2:1 ratio (e.g., 100g flour to 50g water). Mix and knead until smooth. Let rest while you prepare sauce.

2. **Prepare Tomatoes Two Ways:**
   - **Air Fryer Batch:** Place half the tomatoes in air fryer. Cook until they develop a sweet, jammy quality.
   - **Stovetop Batch:** Heat olive oil in a pan. Add remaining tomatoes with salt and pepper. Cook down until softened.

3. **Roll and Cut Pasta:** Roll out pasta dough to desired thickness. Cut into your preferred shape (the video shows wider noodles).

4. **Cook Pasta:** Boil pasta in salted water for 2-3 minutes until al dente. Reserve some pasta water before draining.

5. **Finish the Sauce:** Add reserved pasta water to the stovetop tomatoes to thin the sauce slightly. The starch helps bring everything together.

6. **Combine:** Add cooked pasta to the tomato pan. Add the air-fried tomatoes as well. Toss everything together.

7. **Add Cheese (Optional):** If using, grate fresh parmesan cheese over the pasta. The cheese "helps the sauce come together in the best way possible."

8. **Serve:** Plate and enjoy immediately.

### About the Ingredients

**Water-Based Pasta:** Creates a pasta that absorbs more sauce and has better texture than egg pasta, according to the creator. Still slightly al dente but softer and more pliable.

**Two-Method Tomatoes:** The air-fried tomatoes provide sweet, jammy, concentrated flavors while the stovetop tomatoes maintain bright, acidic notes. This dual preparation creates a complex sauce that tastes like it took hours.

**Minimal Sauce Ingredients:** Keeping it to 4 ingredients or less (tomatoes, olive oil, salt, pepper) plus pasta water is usually all you need. The tomatoes are truly the star.

**Parmesan:** While optional, the cheese helps emulsify the sauce and adds umami depth that brings everything together beautifully.

### Use Cases

- Vegan pasta dinner (omit the parmesan)
- Using up fresh tomatoes that need to be eaten
- When you want homemade pasta without eggs
- Quick weeknight dinner that feels special
- Showcasing quality, simple ingredients
- Teaching pasta-making basics

### Tips & Variations

- Water-based pasta has better texture and absorbs more sauce than egg pasta
- The air-fried tomatoes add a sweet, almost caramelized depth
- You could leave the sauce with just 4 ingredients, but parmesan really elevates it
- Save that pasta water - it's essential for bringing the sauce together
- Can use cherry tomatoes, roma tomatoes, or any fresh tomatoes
- If no air fryer, roast half the tomatoes in oven at 425°F for 15-20 minutes
- Keep the sauce simple - don't over-complicate with too many additions
- Fresh basil would be a nice addition but isn't necessary

### FAQ

1. **Is water-based pasta as good as egg pasta?** The creator prefers it! It absorbs sauce better and has a better texture - still al dente but softer and more sauce-friendly.

2. **Do I need an air fryer?** No, you can roast half the tomatoes in the oven instead for similar results.

3. **Can I make this completely vegan?** Yes! Just omit the parmesan at the end, or use nutritional yeast or vegan parmesan instead.
