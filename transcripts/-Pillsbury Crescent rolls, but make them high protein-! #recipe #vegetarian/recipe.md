## Recipe Card

**Title:** High-Protein Greek Yogurt Crescent Rolls
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**Total Time:** 30 minutes
**Servings:** 8 rolls
**Calories:** Approximately 120 calories per roll

### Description
These homemade crescent rolls use Greek yogurt to boost the protein content while maintaining the flaky, buttery texture you love. Perfect for a healthier breakfast or side dish that doesn't compromise on taste.

### Ingredients
- 100g Greek yogurt
- 100g all purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp grated butter (unsalted)

### Instructions
1. In a bowl, mix together Greek yogurt, flour, baking powder, and salt until a ball is formed.
2. Flatten the dough into a rectangle shape.
3. Add the grated butter in the center and fold the two sides over.
4. Use a rolling pin to seal it up.
5. Fold the dough in half and roll it out gently again.
6. Repeat: fold the dough in half one more time and roll it out to incorporate layers.
7. Cut the dough diagonally into triangles.
8. Roll each triangle into a crescent shape starting from the wide end.
9. Place in the air fryer at 350°F for 15 minutes.
10. Serve warm and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients
Greek yogurt is the star of this recipe, adding protein and moisture while keeping the rolls tender. The butter layers create that signature flaky texture through the folding technique, similar to making puff pastry. Baking powder helps the rolls rise and become light.

### Use Cases
Perfect for breakfast alongside eggs, as a side for soups and salads, or as a higher-protein alternative to regular crescent rolls. Great for meal prep as you can freeze the unbaked crescents and air fry them fresh when needed.

### Tips & Variations
- Use full-fat Greek yogurt for the best texture
- Keep butter cold for optimal flakiness
- Don't overwork the dough to maintain tenderness
- Brush with melted butter before cooking for extra richness
- Try adding herbs like rosemary to the dough for a savory twist
- Can also be baked in a conventional oven at 375°F for 12-15 minutes

### FAQ
1. **Can I use a different type of yogurt?** Greek yogurt works best due to its thick consistency and high protein content. Regular yogurt may make the dough too wet.
2. **Do I need an air fryer?** No, you can bake these in a conventional oven at 375°F for 12-15 minutes until golden brown.
3. **Can I freeze these?** Yes! Freeze unbaked crescents on a baking sheet, then store in a freezer bag. Cook from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

### Nutrition
Each roll contains approximately 120 calories and provides about 5-6g protein from the Greek yogurt and flour. Also provides 4g fat, 16g carbohydrates, and trace amounts of calcium and B vitamins from the yogurt.
