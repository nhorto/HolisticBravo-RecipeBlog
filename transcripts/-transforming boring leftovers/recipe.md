## Recipe Card

**Servings:** 1-2 servings

### Description
Taking time to transform the leftovers you already have makes them more appealing, especially if you're not a big fan of having the same meal-prepped foods every day. This quick and delicious recipe turns leftover rice and sweet potato into a completely different, exciting meal.

### Ingredients

**Crispy Rice:**
- Leftover rice (plain or with quinoa)

**Sweet Potato Tempura:**
- 1 cooked sweet potato
- Flour (for dredging)
- Liquid egg whites (or whole eggs)
- Panko bread crumbs

**Chimichurri Sauce:**
- 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 1 tsp garlic, minced
- ½ tsp sea salt
- ¼ tsp dried oregano
- ¼ tsp red pepper flakes
- ¼ tsp smoked paprika
- ½ cup fresh parsley (about 1 handful)

**For Assembly:**
- 1 fried egg (for protein)

### Instructions

**Make Crispy Rice:**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Spread leftover rice in a thin, single layer on a baking dish (as thin as possible).
3. Bake at 400°F for 15 minutes.
4. Switch to broil for the last 5 minutes to get a crispy top.
5. Remove from oven and set aside.

**Make Sweet Potato Tempura:**
1. Cut cooked sweet potato into thick slices or wedge shapes (doesn't have to be perfect).
2. Set up breading station: Place flour in one bowl, egg whites in another, and panko breadcrumbs in a third.
3. Dredge each sweet potato piece: first in flour, then egg white, then breadcrumbs.
4. Place breaded sweet potato pieces in air fryer.
5. Cook at 400°F for 12-15 minutes until golden and crispy.

**Make Chimichurri Sauce:**
1. Add all chimichurri ingredients to a food processor or blender.
2. Pulse 10-12 times until combined but still slightly chunky.
3. Set aside.

**Assemble the Bowl:**
1. Place a portion of crispy rice in a bowl.
2. Add sweet potato tempura pieces.
3. Top with a fried egg for protein.
4. Drizzle generously with chimichurri sauce.
5. Serve immediately and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients
This recipe is all about making leftovers exciting again. The thin layer of rice gets incredibly crispy when baked and broiled, creating texture similar to rice cakes. Sweet potatoes become indulgent when breaded and air-fried tempura-style. The fresh chimichurri adds brightness and herbaceous flavor that brings everything together.

### Use Cases
Perfect for using up leftover rice and sweet potatoes from meal prep. Great for when you're tired of eating the same thing but don't want to waste food. Ideal for a quick, satisfying dinner when you're really hungry and want something delicious fast.

### Tips & Variations
- Works with any type of leftover rice - white, brown, or rice/quinoa mix
- Spread rice as thin as possible for maximum crispiness
- Can pan-fry sweet potato instead of air frying if preferred
- Use whole eggs instead of just egg whites for richer flavor
- Make chimichurri ahead and store in fridge for up to 1 week
- Try with other leftover roasted vegetables like butternut squash or regular potatoes
- Add avocado, pickled onions, or hot sauce for extra toppings
- Substitute cilantro for parsley in chimichurri for different flavor

### FAQ
1. **Can I make this without an air fryer?** Yes! Bake the breaded sweet potato at 425°F for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.
2. **What if my rice isn't getting crispy?** Make sure it's spread in a very thin layer and give it the full broil time. Also, slightly drier leftover rice works better than freshly cooked.
3. **Can I use store-bought chimichurri?** Absolutely! This recipe is about convenience, so use what you have available.
