## Recipe Card

**Title:** Crispy Tofu Lettuce Wraps with Peanut Sauce

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes
**Total Time:** 40 minutes
**Servings:** 2-3 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 280 calories per serving

### Description
These plant-based lettuce wraps are full of flavor and filling without weighing you down. Crispy air-fried tofu cubes are coated in a delicious peanut powder sauce and paired with a tangy carrot salad for a complete, protein-rich meal.

### Ingredients

**Carrot Salad:**
- 5 carrots, shredded
- ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
- Handful of fresh parsley
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tbsp hot honey
- 1 lemon, juiced
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp pepper

**Tofu:**
- 1 block tofu, drained and cut into cubes
- ½ cup peanut butter powder (PB powder)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- ½ tbsp rice wine vinegar
- 1 tbsp black sesame seeds
- Water, as needed to thin sauce

**For Assembly:**
- Butter lettuce leaves (or romaine, iceberg)

### Instructions
1. **Prepare the carrot salad:** In a bowl, combine shredded carrots, sliced red onion, and parsley. Add red wine vinegar, hot honey, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Toss well and set aside.
2. **Cook the tofu:** Drain and cut tofu into cubes. Place in air fryer and cook at 400°F for 25 minutes until crispy.
3. **Make the peanut sauce:** In a bowl, whisk together PB powder, soy sauce, maple syrup, rice wine vinegar, and black sesame seeds. If the mixture is too thick, add water 1 tablespoon at a time until desired consistency.
4. **Coat the tofu:** Once tofu is crispy, toss it in the peanut sauce mixture until well coated.
5. **Assemble wraps:** Take a butter lettuce leaf, add a spoonful of carrot salad, top with coated tofu cubes, and wrap.
6. **Serve immediately and enjoy!**

### About the Ingredients
Peanut butter powder is a game-changer for high-protein, low-fat sauces. Combined with the tofu, this recipe packs a serious protein punch while staying plant-based. The carrot salad adds crunch, freshness, and a tangy-sweet contrast to the savory tofu. Butter lettuce provides the perfect delicate wrap vessel.

### Use Cases
Perfect for a light lunch or dinner that won't leave you feeling heavy. Great for meal prep - make the components ahead and assemble when ready to eat. Ideal for anyone looking for satisfying vegan protein options or wanting to incorporate more plant-based meals.

### Tips & Variations
- Press tofu for 15-20 minutes before cooking for extra crispiness
- Can pan-fry tofu instead of air frying if preferred
- Substitute almond butter powder for peanut butter powder
- Add sriracha or chili garlic sauce to the peanut sauce for heat
- Try different lettuces - romaine provides more crunch, while iceberg cups are sturdier
- Add sliced cucumber, avocado, or fresh herbs for extra freshness
- The carrot salad can be made a day ahead

### FAQ
1. **Can I use regular peanut butter instead of powder?** Yes, but use about 3-4 tablespoons and thin with more water or soy sauce. The texture will be richer and calories higher.
2. **How do I press tofu?** Wrap the tofu block in a clean kitchen towel, place on a plate, and set something heavy on top for 15-20 minutes to remove excess moisture.
3. **Can I bake the tofu instead?** Yes! Bake at 400°F for 30-35 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crispy.
