## Recipe Card

**Title:** Low-Calorie Spaghetti Squash with Béchamel Sauce
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 45-50 minutes
**Total Time:** 55-60 minutes
**Servings:** 2 servings (1/2 squash each)
**Calories:** Approximately 250-300 calories per serving (including sauce)

### Description
This game-changing recipe proves you can stay in a calorie deficit while still enjoying indulgent, creamy pasta dishes. Spaghetti squash naturally separates into noodle-like strands when roasted, providing an incredibly low-calorie base (only 100 calories for HALF of an entire squash!). Top it with a quick 4-5 minute béchamel sauce for indulgence without the guilt. The low calorie count of the "noodles" means you can add all the sauce and cheese you want.

### Ingredients

**For the Spaghetti Squash:**
- 1 medium spaghetti squash
- Olive oil
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

**For the Béchamel Sauce:**
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1 cup milk (almond milk or any milk of choice)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper

**For Topping:**
- Fresh parmesan cheese, grated
- Fresh parsley, chopped

### Instructions

1. **Prepare the Squash:** Cut spaghetti squash in half lengthwise. Scoop out the seeds and stringy center.

2. **Season:** Drizzle cut sides with olive oil. Sprinkle with salt and black pepper.

3. **Roast:** Place squash halves face-down on a lined baking sheet. Roast at 425°F for 45-50 minutes. For more al dente texture, check at 40 minutes.

4. **Make the Béchamel (during last 5 minutes of roasting):** In a pan over low heat, melt 2 tablespoons of butter.

5. **Add Flour:** Once butter is melted, add 2 tablespoons of flour. Stir constantly until flour is fully dissolved and incorporated with the butter.

6. **Warm the Milk:** While butter-flour mixture cooks, warm 1 cup of milk in microwave for 30-45 seconds (makes it incorporate better).

7. **Add Milk:** Pour warmed milk into the butter-flour mixture. Stir constantly as sauce thickens (this happens quickly!).

8. **Season the Sauce:** Add 1 teaspoon salt and 1 teaspoon black pepper. The sauce will be done in just 4-5 minutes total.

9. **Fluff the Squash:** Remove roasted squash from oven. Use a fork to scrape and fluff the flesh into noodle-like strands.

10. **Combine:** Add half of the béchamel sauce to the squash (still in its shell or transfer to a bowl).

11. **Finish:** Grate fresh parmesan over the top and sprinkle with chopped parsley.

### About the Ingredients

**Spaghetti Squash:** A miracle vegetable for calorie deficit - you can eat half an entire squash for only 100 calories! It naturally forms "noodles" when cooked, making it the perfect pasta substitute.

**Béchamel Sauce:** A classic French white sauce (one of the "mother sauces"). Made with butter, flour, and milk in equal ratios, it's creamy and indulgent but can be made lighter with plant-based milk.

**Almond Milk Option:** Using unsweetened almond milk reduces calories further while keeping the sauce creamy.

### Tips & Variations

- Prefer al dente? Take squash out around 40 minutes
- Warm the milk first - it incorporates much better and prevents lumps
- The entire béchamel process takes only 4-5 minutes - have everything ready!
- Keep heat on LOW for the sauce to prevent burning
- Because the squash is so low calorie, you can add extra cheese guilt-free
- Try marinara sauce instead of béchamel for even lower calories
- Add sautéed mushrooms or cherry tomatoes for extra flavor
- Season the squash well before roasting for more flavor

### FAQ

1. **Can I eat an entire squash?** Yes! An entire spaghetti squash is only about 300 calories, so you can definitely eat the whole thing if you're hungry.

2. **What if my béchamel gets lumpy?** Whisk vigorously or use an immersion blender to smooth it out. Warming the milk first prevents this.

3. **How do I store leftovers?** Store squash and sauce separately in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat gently.
