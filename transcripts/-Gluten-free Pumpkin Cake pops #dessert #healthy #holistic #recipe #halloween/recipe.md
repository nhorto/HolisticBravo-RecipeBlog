### Description
Perfect for fall and Halloween, these no-bake gluten-free cake pops are simple, gut-healthy, and delicious. Made with just 4 ingredients, they come together in minutes and can be stored in the fridge for up to 4 days or frozen for longer storage. The turmeric in the white chocolate coating adds a beautiful orange color and anti-inflammatory benefits.

### Ingredients

**Cake Pop Base:**
- 100g almond flour
- 50g pure pumpkin puree
- 2 tsp monk fruit zero calorie sweetener
- Mini chocolate chips (measure with your heart)
- Sea salt to taste

**White Chocolate Coating:**
- 2 tbsp white chocolate
- 2 tbsp turmeric powder (for orange color and anti-inflammatory benefits)

### Instructions

1. In a bowl, combine almond flour, pumpkin puree, monk fruit sweetener, and a pinch of sea salt.
2. Mix until well combined into a dough-like consistency.
3. Fold in mini chocolate chips.
4. Roll mixture into small balls (cake pop size).
5. Place on a tray and refrigerate or freeze to firm up.
6. Melt white chocolate and mix in turmeric powder until you achieve an orange color.
7. Dip or drizzle the cake pops with the orange white chocolate.
8. Return to fridge to set.
9. Store in fridge for up to 4 days or freeze for longer storage.

### About the Ingredients
**Almond Flour:** Gluten-free, grain-free flour that's high in protein and healthy fats. Easy on the digestive system.

**Pumpkin Puree:** Adds moisture, fiber, and that classic fall flavor. Rich in vitamins and minerals.

**Monk Fruit Sweetener:** Zero-calorie natural sweetener that doesn't spike blood sugar. A healthier alternative to refined sugar.

**Turmeric:** Natural orange food coloring with powerful anti-inflammatory properties and natural pain relief benefits - great for those with painful cramps.

### FAQ
1. **Do these taste like turmeric?** No! The turmeric is in the white chocolate coating and is primarily for color. The pumpkin and chocolate flavors dominate.
2. **Can I use regular sugar instead of monk fruit?** Yes, but monk fruit is recommended for a zero-calorie option that won't spike blood sugar.
3. **How long do these last?** Up to 4 days in the fridge, or several weeks in the freezer.
