### Description
Perfect for slow mornings and self-care, these decadent steamed eggs have a silky, custard-like texture that melts in your mouth. This traditional Asian preparation creates the softest, most delicate eggs you've ever tasted.

### Ingredients
- 2 eggs
- 1 cup water
- Soy sauce, to taste
- Seasoned rice vinegar, to taste
- Sesame seeds, for garnish

**Optional Additions:**
- Kimchi (for crunch)
- Fresh or crushed chillies (for spice)
- Green scallions (for texture and flavor)

### Instructions
1. In a bowl, scramble eggs with 1 cup of warm water until well combined.
2. Remove any air bubbles using a strainer or spoon - this is key for smooth texture.
3. Pour the egg mixture into a heatproof bowl suitable for steaming.
4. Bring water to a boil in a steamer or pot with a steaming rack.
5. Place the bowl of eggs in the steamer.
6. For very hot stoves: Steam on low heat for 2 minutes, then turn off heat and let steam covered for an additional 7-8 minutes.
7. For regular stoves: Steam on low heat for 8-10 minutes total until just set and jiggly.
8. Remove from steamer carefully.
9. Drizzle with soy sauce and seasoned rice vinegar.
10. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
11. Add optional toppings like kimchi, chillies, or scallions if desired.
12. Serve immediately while hot.

### About the Ingredients
The 1:1 ratio of eggs to water is crucial for achieving that signature silky, custard-like texture. Warm water helps the eggs cook more evenly. Removing air bubbles ensures a perfectly smooth surface without holes. Soy sauce and rice vinegar provide the traditional Asian savory-tangy flavor profile.

### Use Cases
Ideal for slow breakfast mornings, comfort food when you're feeling under the weather, or as a gentle protein option. Perfect for anyone who loves soft-cooked eggs or wants to explore Asian breakfast traditions. Also great for meal prep - can be made ahead and gently reheated.

### Tips & Variations
- The key to smooth eggs is removing ALL air bubbles before steaming
- Don't overcook - eggs should still jiggle slightly in the center when done
- Cover the bowl with foil or plastic wrap (with holes poked) to prevent water from dripping onto eggs
- Adjust water amount for different textures: less water = firmer, more water = softer
- Try adding a splash of dashi or chicken broth to the water for extra umami
- Top with crispy fried shallots or garlic for texture contrast
- Serve over rice for a complete meal

### FAQ
1. **Why are my steamed eggs not smooth?** Air bubbles are the culprit. Make sure to strain or carefully remove all bubbles before steaming. Also ensure gentle, low heat.
2. **Can I use a microwave instead?** Yes, but results won't be as silky. Microwave on 50% power in 30-second intervals, checking frequently.
3. **How do I know when they're done?** Eggs should be just set but still jiggly in the center, similar to silken tofu. They'll firm up slightly as they cool.
