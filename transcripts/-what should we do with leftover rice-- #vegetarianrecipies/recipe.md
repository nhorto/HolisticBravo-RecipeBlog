### Description
Rice is only boring if you make it boring! This recipe takes leftover rice and elevates it into something delicious in less than 10 minutes. By cooking it at home, you control the amount of oil and sodium, making it a healthier version of your favorite fried rice.

### Ingredients
- Leftover rice (or rice and quinoa mixture)
- 2 eggs
- 1 bag mixed vegetables (any variety)
- 2 tbsp low sodium soy sauce or coconut aminos
- 1 tbsp seasoned rice vinegar
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp umami mushroom spice
- 1 tbsp ginger paste or ginger powder
- Oil or cooking spray

### Instructions
1. Heat a decent-sized pan on medium heat. Lightly spray or oil the pan.
2. Crack 2 eggs into the pan and scramble until completely cooked.
3. Remove scrambled eggs from pan and set aside.
4. In the same pan, add mixed vegetables.
5. Cook vegetables until slightly tender.
6. Add leftover rice to the pan and incorporate with the veggies.
7. Once mixed well, add seasonings: soy sauce (or coconut aminos), rice vinegar, turmeric, umami mushroom spice, and ginger paste.
8. Mix well until all seasonings are combined and rice is heated through.
9. Toss the scrambled eggs back in.
10. Mix everything together one final time.
11. Serve immediately and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients
Any grain works for this recipe - rice, quinoa, or a mixture of both. Mixed vegetables add color, nutrition, and texture. The combination of soy sauce, rice vinegar, ginger, and umami mushroom seasoning creates authentic fried rice flavor. Turmeric adds color and anti-inflammatory benefits. Scrambling the eggs separately prevents them from getting overcooked or rubbery.

### Use Cases
Perfect for using up leftover rice or grains. Ideal for a quick weeknight dinner when you need something fast and satisfying. Great for meal prep - makes enough for 2 days. Excellent way to add more vegetables to your diet while creating a complete protein meal.

### Tips & Variations
- Leftover rice works better than fresh - it's drier and fries better
- Use any frozen or fresh mixed vegetables you have on hand
- Add protein: tofu, edamame, or cooked chicken
- Substitute tamari for gluten-free option
- Add sesame oil at the end for extra flavor
- Top with green onions, sesame seeds, or sriracha
- Can add garlic or chili flakes for more flavor
- Try with cauliflower rice for a low-carb version
- Day-old rice from the fridge works best - the grains separate better

### FAQ
1. **Can I use fresh rice instead of leftover?** Fresh rice works but tends to get mushy. If using fresh rice, spread it on a sheet pan and refrigerate for 30 minutes to dry it out first.
2. **What vegetables work best?** Any! Frozen mixed veggies are convenient, or use fresh carrots, peas, bell peppers, broccoli, or edamame.
3. **Can I make this vegan?** Yes! Substitute the eggs with crumbled firm tofu or just add extra vegetables.
