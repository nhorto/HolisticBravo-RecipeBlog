## Recipe Card

**Title:** Homemade Dog Treats (Oat, Peanut Butter & Pumpkin)
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes
**Total Time:** 40 minutes
**Servings:** Approximately 20-30 treats (depending on mold size)
**Calories:** Approximately 15-25 calories per treat (for dogs), 35-45 calories per treat (human version with maple syrup)

### Description
Super simple, all-natural dog treats made with just three wholesome ingredients: oats, peanut butter, and pumpkin. These treats come together quickly and can be made in cute shapes using paw print and bone molds. Rex approved! (Also safe for humans - add maple syrup for a human-friendly version.)

### Ingredients

**For Dogs:**
- 1 cup rolled oats
- ¼ cup pumpkin puree (100% pure pumpkin)
- ⅓ cup peanut butter (creamy, with ONLY peanuts as ingredients - no salt or additives)

**For Humans (optional variation):**
- Same as above, plus:
- 2 tbsp maple syrup

### Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 300°F.
2. Add rolled oats, pumpkin puree, and peanut butter to a food processor.
3. **IMPORTANT:** Make sure the peanut butter is creamy and contains ONLY peanuts - no salt, xylitol, or other additives that can be harmful to dogs.
4. Pulse in the food processor or blender until well combined.
5. The mixture should have a Play-Doh-like consistency - moldable and slightly sticky.
6. Press mixture into silicone molds (paw prints, bones, or any shape you like).
7. Place filled molds on a baking sheet.
8. Bake at 300°F for 25-30 minutes.
9. Remove from oven and let cool completely before giving to your dog.
10. Store in an airtight container.

### About the Ingredients
**Rolled Oats:** Provides fiber and is gentle on dog digestion. A wholesome grain that's safe for dogs.

**Pumpkin Puree:** Great for dog digestion, adds moisture and natural sweetness. Use 100% pure pumpkin, NOT pumpkin pie filling.

**Peanut Butter:** Dogs love it! Make sure it's creamy and contains ONLY peanuts. Check the label carefully - xylitol (a common sweetener) is toxic to dogs.

### Use Cases
- Homemade treats for your dog
- Training rewards

- DIY dog gifts for fellow pet parents

- Human snack (with maple syrup added)

### Tips & Variations
- **CRITICAL:** Always check peanut butter ingredients - it should only contain peanuts. Xylitol is toxic to dogs!
- Use silicone molds for easy removal and cute shapes
- The mixture should be like Play-Doh - moldable but not too wet
- These treats are also safe for humans to eat (though bland without added sweetener)
- For a human version, add 2 tbsp maple syrup to the mixture
- Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week, or refrigerate for up to 2 weeks
- Can be frozen for longer storage
- Everything in moderation - these are treats, not meals!
- Rex loved them, so your pup probably will too!

### FAQ
1. **Are these safe for my dog?** Yes, as long as you use peanut butter with ONLY peanuts (no xylitol or other additives). These three ingredients are all dog-safe.

2. **How long do these last?** Store at room temperature for up to a week, refrigerate for up to 2 weeks, or freeze for a month or more.

3. **Can humans eat these too?** Absolutely! They're safe for humans, though you might want to add maple syrup for better flavor.
