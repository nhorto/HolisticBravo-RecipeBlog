### Ingredients

**For Hemp Milk:**
- Hemp seeds/hearts
- Water
- Optional: sweetener, vanilla

**For Rice Milk:**
- Cooked white rice
- Water
- Optional: cinnamon, vanilla (for horchata-like flavor)

**For Almond Milk:**
- Raw almonds
- Water
- Optional: dates or sweetener, vanilla

**For Cashew Milk:**
- Raw cashews
- Water
- Optional: sweetener, vanilla

### Instructions

#### General Process for All Milks:

1. **Prepare Base Ingredient:**
   - For nuts/seeds: Soak if needed (almonds benefit from soaking, cashews can skip)
   - For rice: Cook rice first and let cool

2. **Blend:** Combine base ingredient with water in a high-speed blender (Vitamix recommended). Ratio varies by ingredient but typically 1 cup base to 3-4 cups water.

3. **Strain:** Pour through a nut milk bag or fine-mesh strainer to remove solids. Squeeze thoroughly to extract all liquid.

4. **Store:** Transfer to airtight container. Refrigerate for up to 3-5 days. Shake before each use as separation is natural.

#### Specific Notes:

**Hemp Milk:**
- Most flavorful of the bunch
- Doesn't require soaking
- Has a distinct hemp taste that's surprisingly delicious

**Rice Milk:**
- Gives nostalgia of drinking lower-calorie horchata
- Can add cinnamon for authentic horchata flavor
- Naturally sweet from the rice

**Almond Milk:**
- Most versatile - works in everything
- Never overpowers other ingredients in recipes
- Creator's go-to for recipe use

**Cashew Milk:**
- Thickest consistency of all
- Most similar to heavy cream
- Perfect when you need creamy texture
- Great for creamy soups or sauces

### About the Ingredients

**Vitamix or High-Speed Blender:** Essential for breaking down nuts and seeds thoroughly to extract maximum milk and creaminess.

**Nut Milk Bag:** Makes straining much easier and more thorough than cheesecloth or regular strainers.

**Raw vs Roasted:** Always use raw, unsalted nuts/seeds for milk-making. Roasted varieties will create off-flavors.

### FAQ

1. **Which milk should I start with?** Start with cashew or almond - they're the easiest and most forgiving for beginners.

2. **How long does homemade plant milk last?** 3-5 days in the refrigerator. No preservatives means shorter shelf life than store-bought.

3. **Do I need to strain all of them?** Cashew milk can skip straining if you like it thick. Others benefit from straining for smooth texture.

4. **What do I do with the leftover pulp?** Use in smoothies, baking (cookies, muffins), energy balls, or as a thickener in soups. Don't waste it!
