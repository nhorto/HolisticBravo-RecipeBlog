## Recipe Card

**Title:** High-Protein Peanut Butter Chocolate Truffles
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 5 minutes
**Cook Time:** 2 minutes (chocolate melting)
**Total Time:** 7 minutes + chilling time
**Servings:** Approximately 12-15 balls (depending on size)
**Calories:** Approximately 60-80 calories per ball

### Description
These high-protein PB balls are not only yummy but pack protein and come together in just 5 minutes. Made with peanut butter powder and coated in dark chocolate, they're the perfect healthy sweet treat that never misses.

### Ingredients
- 1 cup peanut butter powder (PB powder)
- 1/4 to 1/3 cup water (add gradually)
- 1 cup dark chocolate mini morsels
- 1 tsp coconut oil
- Sea salt (optional, for topping)

### Instructions
1. Mix peanut butter powder with water gradually until the mixture becomes pliable and dough-like.
2. Roll the PB mixture into balls and place in the refrigerator.
3. While PB balls are chilling, melt dark chocolate mini morsels in the microwave with 1 tsp coconut oil.
4. Heat in microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until fully melted and smooth.
5. Remove PB balls from refrigerator.
6. Roll each PB ball in the melted chocolate until fully coated.
7. Place chocolate-coated balls back in the refrigerator until chocolate sets.
8. Optional: Sprinkle sea salt on top before chocolate hardens to hit all the flavor notes.

### About the Ingredients
Peanut butter powder is dehydrated peanut butter with most of the fat removed, making it high in protein and low in calories compared to regular peanut butter. Dark chocolate provides antioxidants and satisfies chocolate cravings. Coconut oil helps the chocolate melt smoothly and creates a nice coating. Sea salt enhances the sweet and salty flavor profile.

### Tips & Variations
- Add the water to PB powder slowly - easier to add more than to fix if too wet
- Use quality dark chocolate (70% cacao or higher) for best flavor and health benefits
- Try adding vanilla extract to the PB mixture
- Roll in crushed peanuts instead of chocolate for variety
- Freeze for a firmer, truffle-like texture
- Make mini balls for portion control
- Store in refrigerator for up to 2 weeks or freeze for up to 3 months
- Experiment with different nut butter powders (almond, cashew)

### FAQ
1. **Can I use regular peanut butter instead of powder?** You can, but it won't have the same high-protein, low-fat profile. The balls will also be more difficult to form and coat.
2. **Why add coconut oil to the chocolate?** Coconut oil helps the chocolate melt smoothly and creates a thinner coating that's easier to work with. It also adds a slight sheen.
3. **Do I have to add sea salt?** No, it's completely optional, but the salt enhances the chocolate and peanut butter flavors significantly.
