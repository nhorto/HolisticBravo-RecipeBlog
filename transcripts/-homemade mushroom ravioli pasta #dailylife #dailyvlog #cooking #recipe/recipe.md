## Recipe Card

**Total Time:** 1 hour
**Servings:** 2-3 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 450-550 calories per serving

### Description
This is a meditative, hands-on pasta dish that grounds you in the moment. Fresh homemade ravioli is stuffed with a rich mixture of ricotta cheese, sautéed mushrooms, and parsley, then served with a simple but flavorful mushroom and olive oil sauce topped with fresh parmesan. Perfect for when you need to drown out the world and focus on something therapeutic.

### Ingredients

**For the Pasta Dough:**
- 200g all-purpose flour
- 2 whole eggs

**For the Filling:**
- 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
- 2 Bella mushrooms, chopped
- Few sprigs fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 whole clove garlic, minced
- Onion powder, to taste
- Garlic powder, to taste
- Sea salt, to taste

**For the Sauce:**
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 4 Bella mushrooms, sliced
- Garlic powder, to taste
- Onion powder, to taste
- Oregano, to taste
- Sea salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- 3/4 cup pasta water
- 1/2 cup parmesan cheese, grated

### Instructions

1. **Make the Pasta Dough:** Combine 200g all-purpose flour and 2 whole eggs. Knead until smooth and elastic, about 8-10 minutes. Wrap and let rest for 30 minutes.

2. **Prepare the Filling:** In a pan, sauté the chopped mushrooms with minced garlic clove. Season with onion powder, garlic powder, and sea salt. Cook until mushrooms are fully cooked down and liquid has evaporated.

3. **Mix the Filling:** Combine cooked mushrooms and garlic with ricotta cheese and chopped parsley. Mix well and transfer to a sandwich bag with the corner cut off for easy piping.

4. **Form the Ravioli:** Roll out pasta dough thin, add small amounts of filling, fold and seal. Cut into individual ravioli squares.

5. **Cook the Ravioli:** Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook ravioli for 3-4 minutes until they float to the surface. Reserve 3/4 cup pasta water.

6. **Make the Sauce:** In a large pan, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add sliced mushrooms and cook until softened. Season with garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, salt, and pepper.

7. **Finish the Dish:** Add pasta water to the mushroom pan to create a light sauce. Add cooked ravioli and toss gently. Top with fresh grated parmesan and serve immediately.

### About the Ingredients

**Bella Mushrooms:** Also known as cremini mushrooms, these provide a deep, earthy flavor that pairs beautifully with ricotta and enhances the overall dish.

**Fresh Pasta Dough:** Making pasta from scratch creates a tender texture that absorbs sauce beautifully and provides a therapeutic cooking experience.

**Ricotta Cheese:** A mild, creamy cheese that provides the perfect base for the filling without overpowering the mushroom flavor.

**Pasta Water:** The starchy water helps emulsify the sauce and bind everything together for a silky finish.

### Tips & Variations

- The dough kneading is meditative - take your time and enjoy the process
- Make sure mushrooms are fully cooked down before adding to ricotta to avoid watery filling
- Use a sandwich bag with corner cut off as an easy piping bag for filling
- Keep ravioli edges well-sealed with a fork press to prevent opening during cooking
- Fresh parmesan makes a huge difference - grate it right before serving
- Can substitute spinach or sun-dried tomatoes for mushrooms in the filling
- Make extra ravioli and freeze them on a baking sheet before storing in bags

### FAQ

1. **Can I make the pasta dough ahead of time?** Yes, wrap tightly and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Let it come to room temperature before rolling.

2. **How do I know when the ravioli are cooked?** They will float to the surface of the boiling water, typically after 3-4 minutes.

3. **Can I freeze uncooked ravioli?** Yes, freeze them on a baking sheet in a single layer, then transfer to a freezer bag. Cook from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes to cooking time.
