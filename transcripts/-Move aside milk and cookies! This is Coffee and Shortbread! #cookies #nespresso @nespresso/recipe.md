### Description
These classic shortbread cookies require only three ingredients and celebrate the simple pleasures of buttery, melt-in-your-mouth cookies paired with good coffee. While this particular batch didn't hold their shape perfectly (due to impatience with softening butter and chilling time), they still delivered on flavor - buttery and soft on the inside with crunchy edges. Sometimes doing what makes you happy is more important than perfection!

### Ingredients

- 1.5 sticks (12 tablespoons) butter, softened
- 1/2 cup powdered sugar
- 1.5 cups all-purpose flour

### Instructions

1. **Soften Butter Properly:** Take butter out of the fridge well in advance to let it soften at room temperature (this is important for cookie shape retention). Do NOT microwave to speed up the process.

2. **Cream Butter and Sugar:** In a bowl, beat together softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.

3. **Add Flour:** Gradually add flour to the butter mixture. Mix until just combined and dough forms.

4. **Chill the Dough:** This is crucial! Wrap dough and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. (The recipe notes that only 10 minutes was used, which affected the shape - don't make this mistake!)

5. **Shape Cookies:** On a floured surface, roll out dough to about 1/2 inch thickness. Cut into desired shapes with cookie cutters.

6. **Bake:** Place cookies on a lined baking sheet. Bake at 350°F for 12-15 minutes until edges just start to turn golden. Don't overbake - they should still be pale.

7. **Cool:** Let cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

8. **Serve:** Enjoy with Gingerbread Nespresso coffee or your favorite hot beverage.

### About the Ingredients

**Butter:** The star of shortbread. Use real butter (not margarine) for authentic flavor. It must be properly softened at room temperature for best texture and shape retention.

**Powdered Sugar:** Creates a more tender, delicate texture than granulated sugar and helps cookies melt in your mouth.

**All-Purpose Flour:** The structure of the cookies. Don't overmix once flour is added or cookies will be tough.

### Tips & Variations

- **IMPORTANT:** Don't rush the butter softening - let it sit at room temperature, don't microwave it!
- **CRITICAL:** Chill the dough for full 30 minutes (or more) for cookies that hold their shape
- If dough is too soft to work with, chill longer
- Cut into classic shapes like circles, squares, or holiday themes
- Add vanilla extract for extra flavor (1 teaspoon)
- Dip cooled cookies in melted chocolate for extra indulgence
- Sprinkle with coarse sugar before baking for sparkle
- Add lemon zest for lemon shortbread variation

### FAQ

1. **Why didn't my cookies keep their shape?** Two common reasons: butter wasn't properly softened at room temperature (microwaving makes it too soft), and/or dough wasn't chilled long enough before baking.

2. **Can I freeze the dough?** Yes! Shortbread dough freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Thaw in fridge before rolling and cutting.

3. **What's the proper butter texture for shortbread?** Softened but still cool - should indent when pressed but not be greasy or melted. This takes 30-60 minutes at room temperature.
