# Homemade Spinach Ravioli with Egg-Parmesan Filling

## Recipe Card

**Title:** Homemade Spinach Ravioli with Egg-Parmesan Filling
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 20 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15 minutes
**Total Time:** 35 minutes
**Servings:** 2-3 servings (approximately 16-20 ravioli)
**Calories:** Approximately 380 calories per serving

### Description
This is like eating a salad but better! Homemade pasta looks more time-consuming than it is, but this dish comes together in 30 minutes or less. Using wilting greens to make vibrant green pasta and a rich egg-Parmesan filling, you get protein from eggs and spinach in a delicious package.

### Ingredients

**For the Spinach Pasta:**
- Half a container of baby spinach and arugula mix (or leafy greens)
- 1 cup water
- 2.5 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for rolling
- Salt, to taste

**For the Egg-Parmesan Filling:**
- 4 eggs
- ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated

**For the Garlic-Spinach Sauce:**
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 heaping tbsp minced garlic
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- Remaining half of spinach/greens container
- 1 cup pasta water

**For Serving:**
- Extra Parmesan cheese
- Sea salt and black pepper

### Instructions

**Make the Pasta Dough:**
1. In a blender, add half of the spinach mixture and 1 cup of water. Blend until smooth and bright green.
2. Run the green liquid through a strainer to remove any chunks.
3. Pour into a large bowl and gradually mix in flour, starting with 1 cup and adding more until a dough forms (about 2.5 cups total).
4. Knead until you have a smooth dough ball.
5. Cover with a bowl and let rest while you make the filling.

**Make the Egg-Parmesan Filling:**
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
2. Place a large heatproof bowl over the pot (double-boiler method).
3. Crack 4 eggs into the bowl and whisk continuously.
4. Add a pinch of sea salt.
5. As the eggs begin to slightly thicken from the steam heat, add the Parmesan cheese.
6. Continue whisking until the mixture becomes nice and custardy (like a thick paste).
7. Adjust heat if eggs are cooking too quickly or starting to scramble.
8. Remove from heat and set aside.

**Assemble the Ravioli:**
1. Cut the pasta dough into 4 pieces.
2. Roll each piece out thinly on a floured surface into long sheets that fit your ravioli mold.
3. Place first pasta sheet over ravioli mold to create indentations.
4. Fill each indentation with the egg-Parmesan custard mixture.
5. Place second pasta sheet over the top.
6. Press down to seal and cut out individual raviolis.
7. Repeat with remaining dough and filling.

**Make the Sauce and Cook:**
1. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
2. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
3. Add remaining spinach mixture, salt, and pepper.
4. Let the greens cook down until wilted.
5. Meanwhile, bring a pot of salted water to a boil.
6. Add raviolis and cook until they float to the surface (about 3-4 minutes).
7. Add 1 cup of pasta water to the sauce.
8. Transfer cooked raviolis directly to the sauce pan.
9. Toss gently until raviolis are coated in sauce.

**To Serve:**
1. Plate the raviolis with sauce.
2. Top with extra Parmesan cheese.
3. Sprinkle with sea salt and black pepper.
4. Enjoy immediately!

### About the Ingredients
This recipe is brilliant for using up greens that are starting to wilt - the blending and cooking process revives them perfectly. The egg-Parmesan filling is made using a double-boiler technique that creates a rich, custard-like texture without scrambling. The combination of spinach in both the pasta and sauce means you're getting lots of nutrients alongside the protein from eggs.

### Use Cases
Perfect for using up leafy greens before they go bad. Great for impressing dinner guests with homemade pasta. Ideal for a special weeknight meal that feels restaurant-quality but comes together quickly. Excellent for anyone wanting to incorporate more vegetables into their pasta dishes.

### Tips & Variations
- Any leafy greens work: spinach, arugula, kale, or mixed greens
- If you don't have a ravioli mold, cut circles and fold them into half-moons
- Keep extra flour handy while rolling to prevent sticking
- Don't let the egg mixture scramble - maintain gentle heat and whisk constantly
- The pasta dough should be elastic and smooth - if too sticky, add more flour
- Can substitute ricotta cheese mixed with the eggs for a more traditional filling
- Add fresh herbs like basil to the sauce for extra flavor
- Reserve extra pasta water to adjust sauce consistency

### FAQ
1. **Can I make the pasta dough ahead?** Yes! Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. Let come to room temperature before rolling.
2. **What if I don't have a ravioli mold?** Use a glass or cookie cutter to cut circles, place filling in center, fold in half, and seal edges with a fork.
3. **How do I prevent the egg filling from scrambling?** Use gentle indirect heat (double boiler), whisk constantly, and if it's cooking too fast, remove from heat briefly while continuing to whisk.
