## Recipe Card

**Title:** Guava and Cheese Empanadas
**Author:** Holistic Bravo

**Cook Time:** 20-25 minutes
**Total Time:** 70 minutes
**Servings:** 10 empanadas
**Calories:** Approximately 200-250 calories per empanada

### Description
These guava and cheese empanadas are the perfect combination of sweet and salty wrapped in crunchy, flaky deliciousness. They're a crowd-pleaser at parties and surprisingly easy to put together. The homemade dough is simple to work with and creates the perfect vessel for the guava paste and parmesan filling.

### Ingredients

**Dough:**
- 2½ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 stick butter, cold and cut into cubes
- 1 egg, room temperature
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ cup cold water

**Filling:**
- Guava paste, cut into slices
- Parmesan cheese, cut into thick slices

### Instructions

**For the Dough:**
1. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.
2. Add cold butter cubes to the flour mixture.
3. Use your hands to incorporate the butter with the flour until the mixture becomes crumbly.
4. Add the scrambled egg to the mixture.
5. Slowly add cold water a little at a time until the dough comes together.
6. Use your hands to roll the dough into a ball.
7. Cover and let sit in the fridge for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

**Assembly:**
1. Remove dough from fridge and cut into 10 equal pieces.
2. Roll each piece into a ball.
3. Use a rolling pin to flatten each ball into a circle.
4. Place a slice of guava paste and a slice of parmesan cheese in the center of each circle.
5. Fold the dough over and seal the edges (use a fork to crimp if desired).
6. Place empanadas on a baking sheet.

**Baking:**
1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
2. Optional: Brush empanadas with egg wash for golden color.
3. Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown and crispy.

### About the Ingredients
**Guava Paste:** Sweet, tropical fruit paste that provides the signature flavor. Look for it in the Latin foods section of grocery stores. Known as "pasta de guayaba" in Spanish.

**Parmesan Cheese:** The salty, savory component that balances the sweetness of the guava. Creates that perfect sweet-and-salty combination that's so addictive.

**Cold Butter:** Essential for creating flaky, tender pastry. The cold temperature keeps the butter from melting into the flour too quickly, creating layers in the dough.

### Tips & Variations
- Keep the butter cold for the flakiest crust
- Don't skip the chilling time - it makes the dough easier to work with
- You can prepare the dough ahead of time and keep it refrigerated
- Seal the edges well to prevent filling from leaking during baking
- These can be frozen before baking and cooked from frozen (add extra baking time)
- Try cream cheese instead of parmesan for a different flavor profile
- Brush with egg wash before baking for a golden, shiny finish
- Store baked empanadas in airtight container for 2-3 days, reheat in oven

### FAQ
1. **Where can I find guava paste?** Look in the Latin foods section of most grocery stores, or at Latin markets. It's usually sold in blocks or tins.
2. **Can I make these ahead of time?** Yes! Assemble the empanadas and freeze them unbaked. Bake from frozen when ready to serve.
3. **What other cheeses work well?** Cream cheese is popular, but you could also try queso fresco or even mozzarella for a milder flavor.
