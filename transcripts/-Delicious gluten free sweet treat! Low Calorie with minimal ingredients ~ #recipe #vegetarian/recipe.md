### Description
If you like mochi, you will LOVE these! The consistency is amazing with a nice crunch on the outside and a soft mochi texture inside. They're gluten-free, gut healthy, and incredibly low in calories at only 20 calories per ball. Made with tapioca flour from cassava root (known as yuca in Puerto Rico).

### Ingredients
- 1-2 sweet potatoes (depending on size)
- 30g tapioca flour
- 10g corn starch
- 20g sugar (monk fruit zero-calorie sweetener used in video)
- 1 tsp salt
- Cooking spray

### Instructions
1. Steam 1-2 sweet potatoes until completely soft.
2. Mash the steamed sweet potatoes thoroughly.
3. Add 30g tapioca flour to the mashed sweet potato.
4. Add 10g corn starch.
5. Add 20g sugar (or monk fruit sweetener).
6. Add 1 tsp salt.
7. Use your hands to incorporate all ingredients together - the mixture comes together very quickly.
8. Form mixture into ping pong-sized balls.
9. Spray balls with cooking spray on all sides.
10. Place in air fryer.
11. Cook for 10 minutes on one side.
12. Flip and spray other side with cooking spray.
13. Cook for 10 minutes on the second side.
14. Enjoy the crispy exterior and soft mochi interior!

### About the Ingredients
Tapioca flour comes from cassava root (yuca), a staple in Puerto Rican and Latin American cuisine. It creates a unique chewy, mochi-like texture when cooked. Sweet potato provides natural sweetness, fiber, and vitamins while keeping these treats wholesome. Corn starch helps bind the mixture and contributes to the crispy exterior. Using monk fruit sweetener keeps calories even lower while maintaining sweetness.

### Tips & Variations
- Make sure sweet potatoes are fully steamed and very soft for easy mashing
- The mixture should come together quickly - don't overmix
- Uniform ball size ensures even cooking
- Can be made in oven if you don't have an air fryer (425°F for 20-25 min, flipping halfway)
- Try different sweeteners (regular sugar, coconut sugar, stevia)
- Add cinnamon or pumpkin spice for fall flavor
- Experiment with other root vegetables (kabocha squash, Japanese sweet potato)
- Roll in cinnamon sugar after cooking for extra flavor
- Store in airtight container for up to 3 days

### FAQ
1. **Can I bake these in a regular oven?** Yes! Bake at 425°F for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through. Results will be similar to air fryer.
2. **Where can I find tapioca flour?** Most grocery stores carry it in the gluten-free or international foods section. It's also called tapioca starch.
3. **Can I eat all of these in one sitting?** You could! The entire batch is relatively low in calories since each ball is only about 20 calories. The creator admits to eating the entire plate.
