### Description
This stunning tri-color ravioli showcases your pasta-making skills with naturally colored doughs in green (matcha), orange (turmeric), and classic white. The filling is a simple but flavorful blend of blanched spinach, cashews, parmesan, and olive oil that mimics a fresh pesto. Perfect for impressing guests or trying something new and creative in the kitchen.

### Ingredients

**For the Pasta Dough (3 batches):**
- For Natural Dough: 200g all-purpose flour + 2 eggs
- For Green Dough: 200g all-purpose flour + 2 eggs + 1-2 tsp matcha powder
- For Orange Dough: 200g all-purpose flour + 2 eggs + 1-2 tsp turmeric powder

**For the Filling:**
- Fresh spinach, blanched (approximately 2 cups)
- Raw cashews (1/2 cup)
- Parmesan cheese (1/2 cup)
- Olive oil (2-3 tbsp)
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

### Instructions

1. **Prepare Three Dough Colors:** Make three separate batches of pasta dough. For the first, make a standard pasta dough with flour and eggs. For the second, add matcha powder to create a green color. For the third, add turmeric powder for an orange hue. Knead each dough separately until smooth.

2. **Rest the Doughs:** Wrap each colored dough separately and let rest for at least 30 minutes.

3. **Make the Filling:** Blanch fresh spinach in boiling water for 30-60 seconds, then drain and squeeze out excess water. In a food processor, combine blanched spinach, cashews, parmesan cheese, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Blend until it reaches a pesto-like consistency.

4. **Roll Out the Dough:** Roll each colored dough separately to a thin, even thickness suitable for ravioli.

5. **Assemble the Ravioli:** Place small spoonfuls of filling on the pasta sheets. You can get creative with how you combine the three colors - use different colors for top and bottom, or create patterns.

6. **Seal the Ravioli:** Brush edges with water, place second sheet on top, and press around each mound of filling to seal. Cut into individual ravioli squares or circles.

7. **Cook the Ravioli:** Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook ravioli for 3-4 minutes until they float.

8. **Serve:** Toss with your favorite sauce or simply with olive oil and parmesan.

### About the Ingredients

**Matcha Powder:** A vibrant green tea powder that gives the pasta a beautiful emerald color and adds subtle earthy notes.

**Turmeric Powder:** Provides a golden-orange color and mild, warm flavor to the pasta dough.

**Cashews:** Create a creamy, rich texture in the filling similar to traditional pesto but without pine nuts.

**Blanched Spinach:** Adds vibrant green color and nutrients to the filling while keeping it smooth.

### Tips & Variations

- This was the creator's first attempt - the aesthetics can be improved with practice
- Make sure to thoroughly squeeze blanched spinach to prevent watery filling
- Use a pasta roller for more even, thinner sheets
- Natural food coloring powders won't significantly alter the taste
- Can substitute pine nuts for cashews for a more traditional pesto flavor
- Experiment with other natural colorings like beet powder (pink) or squid ink (black)
- Keep colored doughs separate until assembly to maintain distinct colors

### FAQ

1. **Will the matcha and turmeric affect the taste significantly?** They add subtle flavor but won't overpower the dish. The amounts used for coloring are small.

2. **Can I use food coloring instead of natural powders?** Yes, but natural powders add a subtle flavor dimension and are a more whole-food approach.

3. **How do I prevent the colors from bleeding together?** Work with one color at a time and keep doughs separate until final assembly.
