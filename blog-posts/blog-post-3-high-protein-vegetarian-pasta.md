## Introduction

Here's the thing about pasta - most people think of it as a carb bomb. Something delicious but not exactly a "healthy" meal. And if you're vegetarian, the protein situation gets even trickier. Traditional pasta with marinara? Maybe 8-10g of protein if you're lucky. That's not a meal, that's an appetizer.

But what if the pasta itself was the protein source? That's exactly what my tofu pasta does - and it changed how I think about pasta completely. By blending tofu directly into the dough, you get 15-18g of protein per serving before you even add a sauce. No protein powder, no weird tricks, just tofu and flour.

I've been making homemade pasta at least once a week for a while now, and I've developed a collection of high-protein vegetarian pasta recipes that actually keep you full. From quick 15-minute gnocchi to impressive handmade ravioli, these 5 recipes deliver 12-26g of protein per serving. Real protein from real food - cheese, eggs, tofu, nuts, and spinach doing the heavy lifting.

## 1. High Protein Tofu Pasta

[INSERT IMAGE: recipe-images/high-protein-tofu-pasta/]

This is the recipe that started it all - and it's an absolute game-changer. Two ingredients: flour and firm tofu. That's it. You blend the tofu, mix it into the flour, knead for a few minutes, and you have a pasta dough that's packed with protein and tastes like... pasta. Genuinely. You cannot taste the tofu.

I tried this for the first time while filming a video and it immediately went into my weekly rotation. The tofu replaces eggs as both the protein source and the binder, so the dough comes together the same way traditional pasta does. Roll it thin, cut it into whatever shape you want, and cook it for 3-5 minutes. The pasta water gets nice and starchy too, which is perfect for making a quick garlic and greens sauce.

**What makes it special:**
- **15-18g of protein per serving** from the tofu alone
- Only 2 ingredients with a 1:1 ratio (200g flour to 200g tofu) - easy to scale up or down
- Cooks in 3-5 minutes vs. 10-12 for dried pasta
- Dough stores in the fridge for 2-3 days or freezer for up to a month
- Works with any shape - fettuccine, pappardelle, even ravioli

**Nutrition per serving:** 320-380 calories, 15-18g protein, 55-65g carbs, 4-6g fat

The sauce I recommend starting with is dead simple: garlic, olive oil, baby greens (arugula or spinach), salt, pepper, and a splash of that starchy pasta water to bring it all together. It's ready in the time it takes the pasta to cook.

## 5. Lazy Girl Pumpkin Gnocchi

[INSERT IMAGE: recipe-images/lazy-girl-pumpkin-gnocchi/]

When I don't have an hour to make ravioli but still want homemade pasta, this is what I make. It's the easiest gnocchi recipe you'll ever find - dump leftover pumpkin and flour into a food processor, pulse, shape, and cook. No kneading. No rolling. Barely any cleanup. I timed myself once and had dinner on the table in 15 minutes.

The pumpkin gives the gnocchi a subtle sweetness and beautiful orange color, plus it adds fiber and vitamins. At 12-15g of protein per serving (less than the others on this list, but the speed makes up for it), it's the perfect weeknight option.

- **Total time:** 15 minutes

## Conclusion

Pasta doesn't have to be empty carbs. Every recipe on this list delivers real protein from real ingredients - tofu, ricotta, eggs, cashews, Parmesan - without relying on protein powder or supplements.

If you're just getting started with high-protein vegetarian pasta, begin with the Tofu Pasta. It's the quickest, uses the fewest ingredients, and the protein payoff is the best for the effort. Once you're comfortable with that, work your way up to the ravioli recipes for a more impressive (and even more protein-dense) meal.
