## Why This Analysis Matters

Keyword Difficulty (KD) from Ahrefs is a useful filter, but it doesn't tell the full story. A KD of 6 might mean "no strong competitors" or it might mean "10 established blogs already rank here but none have great backlinks to those specific pages." This SERP analysis answers: **Who are we actually competing against, and can we realistically rank?**

## Key Takeaways

### What the SERPs Tell Us

1. **KD scores are validated.** Low-KD keywords genuinely have smaller sites ranking. KD:1-2 keywords have personal blogs on page 1.

2. **The "high protein" niche is competitive but not locked up.** 10 sites rank for it, but most are general vegan blogs with a roundup post. None have high-protein as their core identity the way Holistic Bravo does (24% of recipes are explicitly high-protein). The differentiator is real but not as strong as I originally claimed.

3. **Mega-blogs (Love and Lemons, Food52) rank everywhere but can be beaten on specific queries.** They have DR 60-80+ but their content is spread across thousands of pages. A focused post with better content can eventually outrank them for specific long-tail keywords.

4. **Small sites ARE ranking right now.** Omventure, Sustainable Rookie, Creative Vegetable Gardener, The Banana Diaries - these prove that domain authority isn't the only factor. Content relevance and specificity matter.

5. **Visual content matters.** Pinterest ranking for "vegetable side dishes" indicates Google values visual/image-rich content for food queries. Good food photography will help.

### What We Should Do Differently Based on SERPs

1. **Start with KD:1-2 keywords**, not the highest-volume ones. Build domain authority with early wins.
2. **Study the small sites that rank** - what are they doing right? (Likely: focused content, good structure, specific niche)
3. **Don't try to out-quantity the mega-blogs.** "60 Easy Vegetarian Dinner Recipes" (Taste of Home) beats us on volume. Win on depth and quality instead - detailed descriptions, personal stories, nutritional info, beautiful photos.
4. **Homemade pasta is a genuine content differentiator** that most competitors don't have. Lean into this.
5. **The protein angle works but needs honest framing.** Don't claim "we're the only ones" - instead position it as "every recipe was designed with protein in mind" vs. competitors who have a protein category as an afterthought.
