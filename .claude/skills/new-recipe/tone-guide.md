# Holistic Bravo Voice & Tone Guide

## Brand Voice

Holistic Bravo (Crystal) is a vegetarian/plant-based cooking content creator. Her recipes are approachable, health-conscious, and focused on proving that healthy food can be delicious and satisfying.

## Point of View

- **ALL sections use FIRST PERSON:** "I created...", "When I first shared...", "my go-to", "I find that...", "I make this three times a week..."
- **NEVER use third person** like "Holistic Bravo shared..." or "When Holistic Bravo created..."
- **Why You'll Love This:** First person with bold leads. `- **Bold Title** - Description using I/my/me`
- **Tips:** First person. "I always...", "I find that...", "I usually..."
- **Introduction, Ingredients, Instructions, FAQ:** All first person throughout

## Tone Characteristics

- **Warm and encouraging** — like talking to a friend in the kitchen
- **Practical, not preachy** — focuses on how easy/fast/cheap something is, not lecturing about health
- **Enthusiastic but grounded** — genuinely excited about food, not over-the-top
- **Personal stories sprinkled in** — "I make this X times a week", "This is my go-to when..."
- **Body-positive** — mentions protein, calories, and nutrition as fuel/energy, never as restriction or guilt

## Signature Phrases & Patterns

Use these naturally (not all at once):
- "game-changer"
- "guilt-free"
- "that's the whole point"
- "the magic of this recipe is..."
- "seriously" (as emphasis)
- "Don't skimp out on..."
- "This is the kind of [meal] you [eat/make] when..."
- "Life is all about balance"
- References to meal prep, busy schedules, staying full for hours
- Protein content is always highlighted as a positive

## Ingredients Section Rules

The Ingredients section is NARRATIVE paragraphs, NOT a bulleted list. This is a key differentiator of the Holistic Bravo blog style.

**Do this:**
> The magic of this recipe is in its simplicity. Egg whites are pure protein with virtually no fat or carbohydrates, making them ideal for high-protein, low-calorie baking. You'll need 200 grams of egg whites, which you can buy pre-separated in cartons at most grocery stores for convenience.

**NOT this:**
> - 200g egg whites
> - 1 scoop protein powder
> - Dark chocolate M&Ms

The bulleted list format is reserved ONLY for the Recipe Card section at the bottom.

## Instructions Section Rules

Each numbered step is a full paragraph with context, not a terse command.

**Do this:**
> 1. Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and preparing your muffin tin with liners or non-stick spray.
> 2. Add 200 grams of egg whites to a large, clean mixing bowl. Make sure the bowl is completely free of any grease or oil, as this can prevent the whites from whipping properly. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, whip the egg whites on high speed until they form stiff peaks.

**NOT this:**
> 1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
> 2. Whip egg whites until stiff peaks form.

The concise format is reserved ONLY for the Recipe Card section at the bottom.

## Recipe Categories

Recipes typically fall into these themes:
- **High-protein** — protein content always highlighted, often 20g+ per serving
- **Quick & easy** — minimal ingredients, 30 minutes or less
- **Meal prep friendly** — stores well, reheats well, make-ahead
- **Comfort food made healthy** — familiar dishes reimagined with whole foods
- **Plant-based/vegetarian** — default orientation, meat is never used

## Common Substitutions to Mention

- Protein powder: any brand/flavor works, whey or plant-based
- Greek yogurt: can sub non-dairy yogurt for vegan version
- Eggs: mention vegan alternatives when relevant (aquafaba, flax eggs)
- Gluten: always mention if recipe is naturally GF or how to make it GF
