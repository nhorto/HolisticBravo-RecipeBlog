FIELD: tips
========================================
- I always make sure my egg whites are at room temperature for the best whipping results
- I whip the egg whites until they form stiff peaks - this is crucial for that fluffy texture
- I fold in the protein powder gently to preserve all those air bubbles I just created
- Don't overmix when adding the protein powder - I use gentle folds and that's the key
- I add the optional dark chocolate M&Ms halfway through baking so they don't sink to the bottom
- I store these in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days
- These taste great cold straight from the fridge or at room temperature - I honestly love them both ways

FIELD: ingredientsOverview
========================================
The magic of this recipe is in its simplicity. Egg whites are pure protein with virtually no fat or carbohydrates, making them ideal for high-protein, low-calorie baking. When I whip them to stiff peaks, they create an airy, cloud-like texture that's absolutely heavenly. I use 200 grams of egg whites, which I buy pre-separated in cartons at most grocery stores for convenience.

The protein powder adds both flavor and a serious protein boost while keeping the recipe vegetarian-friendly. I use Momentous plant-based chocolate protein powder, but any protein powder you love will work beautifully - whey, casein, or plant-based all create delicious results. I've tried vanilla, strawberry, and peanut butter flavored protein and they all come out great.

The dark chocolate M&Ms are completely optional but I highly recommend them. They provide a small indulgence and hit of chocolate that makes these feel like a real dessert rather than just a protein snack. If you want to keep them even healthier, I suggest swapping for sugar-free chocolate chips.

FIELD: instructionsOverview
========================================
The technique for these muffins is straightforward but requires a gentle touch. I start by preheating my oven to 350°F (175°C) and preparing my muffin tin with liners or non-stick spray.

I add 200 grams of egg whites to a large, clean mixing bowl. I make sure the bowl is completely free of any grease or oil, as this can prevent the whites from whipping properly. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, I whip the egg whites on high speed until they form stiff peaks. This usually takes me 3-5 minutes. I know they're ready when I can lift the beaters and the peaks stand straight up without drooping.

Once I have beautiful stiff peaks, it's time to add the protein powder. I add one scoop of my favorite chocolate protein powder to the whipped egg whites. Using a rubber spatula, I gently fold the protein powder into the egg whites. I use a gentle folding motion from the bottom of the bowl up and over - I want to preserve as much of the air I just whipped in as possible. The mixture should be light and fluffy, not deflated.

I divide the mixture evenly among 5 muffin cups. The batter will be very light and airy, almost like a mousse. I bake at 350°F for 5 minutes, then remove from the oven. If I'm using dark chocolate M&Ms or chocolate chips, I add them to the tops of the muffins now. This prevents them from sinking to the bottom and ensures they stay on top for that pretty presentation.

I return the muffins to the oven and bake for another 5 minutes, for a total baking time of 10 minutes. The muffins should be lightly golden and set to the touch. I let them cool slightly in the pan, then transfer to a wire rack. These are delicious warm or cold!

FIELD: faq
========================================
**Can I use whole eggs instead of just egg whites?**
You can, but it will significantly increase the calorie and fat content. I stick with egg whites because they keep these ultra-low calorie while still packing in the protein. Whole eggs would also make them denser and less cloud-like.

**What if I don't have a muffin pan?**
No problem! I've made this as one large "muffin" or cake in a small baking dish. Just adjust the cooking time as needed - I check for doneness around the 15-minute mark.

**Do I need to use plant-based protein powder?**
Absolutely not. Any protein powder works beautifully - whey, casein, or plant-based. I use whatever I have on hand. Just know that different protein powders may slightly affect the texture and taste.

**Why are mine deflating after baking?**
This can happen if the egg whites weren't whipped to stiff enough peaks or if you overmixed when folding in the protein powder. I make sure to whip until stiff peaks form and fold very gently.

- 200g egg whites
- 1 scoop plant-based chocolate protein powder (or any protein powder)
- Optional: Dark chocolate M&Ms for topping

========================================
FIELD: recipeCardInstructions
========================================
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
2. Whip 200g of egg whites until they form stiff peaks.
3. Gently fold in 1 scoop of chocolate protein powder.
4. Divide mixture into 5 muffin cups.
5. Bake at 350°F for 5 minutes.
6. Remove from oven and add dark chocolate M&Ms on top (optional).
7. Return to oven and bake for another 5 minutes.
