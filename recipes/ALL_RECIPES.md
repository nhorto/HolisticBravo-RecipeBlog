## Recipe Card

**Title:** Two-Ingredient Chocolate Protein Cosmic Muffins
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 5 minutes
**Cook Time:** 10 minutes
**Total Time:** 15 minutes
**Servings:** 5 muffins
**Calories:** 250 calories for all 5 muffins (50 calories each)

### Description
These amazing high-protein muffins are made with just two base ingredients - whipped egg whites and protein powder. They're incredibly light, fluffy, and satisfying while being extremely low in calories. Optional dark chocolate M&Ms add a fun touch of indulgence while keeping the recipe balanced.

### Ingredients
- 200g egg whites
- 1 scoop plant-based chocolate protein powder (Momentous brand used in video)
- Optional: Dark chocolate M&Ms for topping

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
2. Whip 200g of egg whites until they form stiff peaks.
3. Gently fold in 1 scoop of your favorite chocolate protein powder until just combined.
4. Divide mixture into 5 muffin cups.
5. Bake at 350°F for 5 minutes.
6. Remove from oven and add dark chocolate M&Ms on top (optional).
7. Return to oven and bake for another 5 minutes.
8. Total baking time: 10 minutes.

### About the Ingredients
Egg whites are pure protein with virtually no fat or carbs, making them ideal for high-protein, low-calorie baking. When whipped to stiff peaks, they create an airy, cloud-like texture. Plant-based protein powder adds flavor and additional protein while keeping the recipe vegetarian-friendly. The optional dark chocolate M&Ms provide a small indulgence - because life is all about balance.

### Use Cases
- Post-workout protein snack
- Healthy dessert that satisfies sweet cravings
- Meal prep for the week (store in fridge)
- Low-calorie sweet treat when dieting
- Quick protein boost between meals

### Tips & Variations
- Make sure egg whites are at room temperature for best whipping results
- Don't overmix when folding in protein powder - gentle folds preserve the air
- Try different protein powder flavors (vanilla, strawberry, peanut butter)
- Add sugar-free chocolate chips instead of M&Ms for lower sugar
- Can be stored in refrigerator for up to 5 days
- Enjoy all 5 muffins for only 250 calories total

### FAQ
1. **Can I use whole eggs instead of just egg whites?** You can, but it will significantly increase the calorie and fat content. Egg whites keep these ultra-low calorie.
2. **What if I don't have a muffin pan?** You can make these as one large "muffin" in a small baking dish, just adjust cooking time as needed.
3. **Do I need to use plant-based protein?** No, any protein powder works - whey, casein, or plant-based all work well.

### Nutrition
Per muffin (1 of 5):
- Calories: 50
- Protein: Approximately 10g
- Carbohydrates: Approximately 2g
- Fat: Less than 1g
- Fiber: 0g

All 5 muffins: 250 calories total

Low carb and low fat

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes (bagels) + 5 minutes (eggs)
**Total Time:** 40 minutes
**Servings:** 1 complete breakfast
**Calories:** Approximately 750-850 calories per serving (depending on specific brands used)

### Description
This breakfast is a protein powerhouse without any meat, keeping you full for 6+ hours. It combines homemade protein bagels made in an air fryer with a hearty omelet topped with avocado. Don't skimp on breakfast - make it your most satiating meal of the day.

### Ingredients

**Chocolate Chip Protein Bagels:**
- 140g bread flour
- 180g Greek yogurt
- 1 scoop protein powder of choice
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp mini chocolate chips

**Omelet:**
- 3 whole eggs
- 50g egg whites
- A chunk of feta cheese
- Half an avocado
- Sea salt to taste

### Instructions

**For the Bagels:**
1. Mix together 140g bread flour, 180g Greek yogurt, 1 scoop protein powder, 1 tsp baking soda, and 1 tsp salt.
2. Fold in 2 tbsp mini chocolate chips.
3. Form dough into bagel shapes.
4. Place in air fryer at 350°F.
5. Cook for 10 minutes on one side.
6. Flip and cook for 10 minutes on the other side.
7. Total air fryer time: 20 minutes.

**For the Omelet:**
1. Whisk together 3 whole eggs and 50g egg whites.
2. Add a chunk of feta cheese to the egg mixture.
3. Cook omelet in a pan until set.
4. Top with half an avocado, sliced.
5. Sprinkle with sea salt.

### About the Ingredients
Greek yogurt provides protein and creates a chewy bagel texture without yeast. Bread flour gives structure while protein powder boosts the protein content significantly. Feta cheese adds salty, tangy flavor and extra protein to the eggs. Avocado provides healthy fats that help keep you satiated longer.

- High-protein vegetarian meal prep

### Tips & Variations

- Use whole wheat flour for extra fiber
- Make mini bagels for portion control
- Air fryer temperature and time may vary by model

### FAQ
1. **Can I make these bagels without an air fryer?** Yes, bake in a regular oven at 350°F for approximately 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway.
2. **How long can I store the bagels?** Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days, or freeze for up to 3 months.
3. **Can I prep this breakfast ahead?** Absolutely! Make bagels in advance and just cook the omelet fresh in the morning.

**Title:** Chocolate Chip Pumpkin Muffins
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20-25 minutes
**Total Time:** 30-35 minutes
**Servings:** 12 muffins
**Calories:** Approximately 140-160 calories per muffin

### Description
These chocolate chip pumpkin muffins are perfect for fall and incredibly easy to make. You only need one large bowl, making cleanup a breeze. Made with whole food ingredients including pumpkin puree, oat flour, and optional extra protein from egg whites.

### Ingredients

**Dry Ingredients:**
- 150g all-purpose flour
- 70g oat flour
- 1g baking soda (approximately 1/4 tsp)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp pumpkin pie spice
- Mini chocolate chips (to taste - "you'll know when it's enough")

**Wet Ingredients:**
- 1 whole egg
- 1 can pure pumpkin puree
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 50g egg whites (optional for extra protein - can substitute with 1 additional whole egg)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. In a large bowl, combine all dry ingredients: all-purpose flour, oat flour, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin pie spice.
3. Add mini chocolate chips to dry ingredients (add until it looks right to you).
4. In the same bowl, add all wet ingredients: egg, pumpkin puree, olive oil, maple syrup, egg whites (or additional egg), and vanilla extract.
5. Mix everything together in the bowl until well combined.
6. If using a non-stick muffin pan, no preparation needed. Otherwise, use liners or non-stick spray.
7. Divide batter evenly among 12 muffin cups.
8. Bake at 425°F for 20-25 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
9. Let cool and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients
Pure pumpkin puree provides moisture, fiber, and fall flavor while keeping these muffins naturally sweet. Oat flour adds whole grain nutrition and a subtle nutty flavor. The combination of all-purpose and oat flour creates the perfect texture. Pumpkin pie spice brings warm autumn aromatics. Egg whites are optional but add extra protein without changing the flavor.

### Use Cases
- Perfect fall dessert or breakfast
- Meal prep for the week
- Holiday gatherings and potlucks
- After-school snack for kids
- Coffee shop-style muffin at home
- Using up canned pumpkin after Thanksgiving

### Tips & Variations
- Invest in a food scale ($10) to make measuring easier and reduce cleanup from measuring cups
- If you don't have oat flour, blend rolled oats in a blender until fine
- Try adding chopped walnuts or pecans for crunch
- Swap chocolate chips for white chocolate or butterscotch chips
- Use pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling (which has added sugar and spices)
- Store in airtight container at room temperature for 3 days or refrigerate for up to a week
- These freeze beautifully for up to 3 months

### FAQ
1. **Can I make these without oat flour?** Yes, you can use all all-purpose flour (220g total), though you'll lose some of the whole grain nutrition.
2. **Why bake at such a high temperature?** The high heat creates a nice dome on top and sets the exterior quickly while keeping the inside moist.
3. **Can I reduce the sugar?** These are already lightly sweetened with just 2 tbsp maple syrup. The pumpkin and chocolate chips provide natural sweetness.

## Recipe Card

**Title:** High-Protein Peanut Butter Chocolate Truffles
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 5 minutes
**Cook Time:** 2 minutes (chocolate melting)
**Total Time:** 7 minutes + chilling time
**Servings:** Approximately 12-15 balls (depending on size)
**Calories:** Approximately 60-80 calories per ball

### Description
These high-protein PB balls are not only yummy but pack protein and come together in just 5 minutes. Made with peanut butter powder and coated in dark chocolate, they're the perfect healthy sweet treat that never misses.

### Ingredients
- 1 cup peanut butter powder (PB powder)
- 1/4 to 1/3 cup water (add gradually)
- 1 cup dark chocolate mini morsels
- 1 tsp coconut oil
- Sea salt (optional, for topping)

### Instructions
1. Mix peanut butter powder with water gradually until the mixture becomes pliable and dough-like.
2. Roll the PB mixture into balls and place in the refrigerator.
3. While PB balls are chilling, melt dark chocolate mini morsels in the microwave with 1 tsp coconut oil.
4. Heat in microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until fully melted and smooth.
5. Remove PB balls from refrigerator.
6. Roll each PB ball in the melted chocolate until fully coated.
7. Place chocolate-coated balls back in the refrigerator until chocolate sets.
8. Optional: Sprinkle sea salt on top before chocolate hardens to hit all the flavor notes.

### About the Ingredients
Peanut butter powder is dehydrated peanut butter with most of the fat removed, making it high in protein and low in calories compared to regular peanut butter. Dark chocolate provides antioxidants and satisfies chocolate cravings. Coconut oil helps the chocolate melt smoothly and creates a nice coating. Sea salt enhances the sweet and salty flavor profile.

### Use Cases
- Healthy dessert that fits macro goals
- Post-workout protein snack
- Meal prep sweet treats for the week
- Gift for health-conscious friends
- Satisfying chocolate craving while staying on track
- Quick energy boost with protein

### Tips & Variations
- Add the water to PB powder slowly - easier to add more than to fix if too wet
- Use quality dark chocolate (70% cacao or higher) for best flavor and health benefits
- Try adding vanilla extract to the PB mixture
- Roll in crushed peanuts instead of chocolate for variety
- Freeze for a firmer, truffle-like texture
- Make mini balls for portion control
- Store in refrigerator for up to 2 weeks or freeze for up to 3 months
- Experiment with different nut butter powders (almond, cashew)

### FAQ
1. **Can I use regular peanut butter instead of powder?** You can, but it won't have the same high-protein, low-fat profile. The balls will also be more difficult to form and coat.
2. **Why add coconut oil to the chocolate?** Coconut oil helps the chocolate melt smoothly and creates a thinner coating that's easier to work with. It also adds a slight sheen.
3. **Do I have to add sea salt?** No, it's completely optional, but the salt enhances the chocolate and peanut butter flavors significantly.

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes
**Cook Time:** 5-10 minutes
**Total Time:** 7-12 minutes
**Servings:** 1 serving
**Calories:** Approximately 450-550 calories per serving (depending on topping amounts)

### Description
This oatmeal is the perfect cozy food when you're not feeling your best or just need something warm and comforting. Made with a low liquid-to-oat ratio for a chunky, thick consistency that fills you up and magically makes you feel better.

### Ingredients

**Base:**
- 1 cup rolled oats
- 1 cup water (or milk/milk substitute for creamier texture)
- Pinch of sea salt

**Toppings:**
- Frozen wild blueberries
- Sliced almonds
- Creamy peanut butter (generous amount)
- Agave nectar (for sweetness)

### Instructions
1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil in a pan over medium heat.
2. Add 1 cup of rolled oats to the boiling water.
3. Add a pinch of sea salt.
4. Stir constantly to prevent oats from sticking together.
5. Cook on medium to low heat until liquid is fully absorbed and oats reach desired consistency (chunky and thick).
6. Transfer oatmeal to a bowl.
7. Top with frozen wild blueberries.
8. Add sliced almonds.
9. Add a generous amount of creamy peanut butter.
10. Drizzle with agave nectar for sweetness.
11. Enjoy with your coffee!

### About the Ingredients
Rolled oats are whole grain and provide sustained energy, fiber, and essential nutrients. Using a 1:1 ratio of liquid to oats creates a thick, chunky texture - if you prefer it runnier, add more liquid. Frozen blueberries provide antioxidants and create a nice temperature contrast. Peanut butter adds protein, healthy fats, and incredible flavor. Sliced almonds provide crunch and additional healthy fats. Agave nectar offers natural sweetness.

### Use Cases
- Comforting breakfast on cold mornings
- When recovering from illness and need something gentle
- Cozy weekend breakfast
- Quick weekday breakfast that keeps you full
- Post-workout meal with added protein from peanut butter
- New Year's morning comfort food

### Tips & Variations
- For runnier oatmeal: Increase liquid ratio (1.5-2 cups liquid per 1 cup oats)
- For even chunkier texture: Use steel-cut oats
- For smoother consistency: Grind oats into flour before cooking
- Use milk or non-dairy milk instead of water for creamier oatmeal
- Try different nut butters (almond, cashew, sunflower seed)
- Swap agave for honey, maple syrup, or brown sugar
- Add cinnamon or vanilla extract while cooking
- Top with banana slices, chia seeds, or coconut flakes
- Make overnight oats version by soaking oats in milk overnight

### FAQ
1. **Can I use instant oats?** Yes, but they'll cook much faster (1-2 minutes) and won't have the same chunky texture. Rolled oats or steel-cut are best for this recipe.
2. **Why use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?** Frozen blueberries are available year-round, often more affordable, and create a nice temperature contrast with hot oatmeal. Plus, they're flash-frozen at peak ripeness.
3. **How can I make this higher in protein?** Add a scoop of protein powder to the cooked oats, increase peanut butter amount, or stir in Greek yogurt after cooking.

**Title:** Bang Bang Brussels Sprouts
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20-25 minutes
**Total Time:** 30-35 minutes
**Servings:** 4 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 150-200 calories per serving

### Description
These bang bang Brussels sprouts pack a punch in the best way possible! They're easy to throw together and are perfect for those following vegan, vegetarian, or paleo diets. The combination of crispy texture with sweet and spicy flavors makes these an addictive side dish or appetizer.

### Ingredients

**Brussels Sprouts:**
- 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
- 2 tbsp olive oil or avocado oil
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp black pepper

**Bang Bang Sauce:**
- 1/3 cup vegan mayonnaise (or regular mayo)
- 2 tbsp sweet chili sauce
- 1-2 tsp sriracha (adjust to taste)
- 1 tbsp agave nectar or honey
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- Optional: sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C) or air fryer to 375°F.
2. Trim ends off Brussels sprouts and cut in half (quarter if very large).
3. Toss Brussels sprouts with oil, salt, and pepper.
4. For oven: Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway, until crispy and caramelized.
5. For air fryer: Place in air fryer basket in a single layer. Cook for 15-18 minutes, shaking basket halfway through.
6. While Brussels sprouts cook, make bang bang sauce: Whisk together mayo, sweet chili sauce, sriracha, agave, and rice vinegar.
7. When Brussels sprouts are crispy and golden, transfer to a large bowl.
8. Add bang bang sauce and toss to coat evenly.
9. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions if desired.
10. Serve immediately while crispy.

### About the Ingredients
Brussels sprouts are nutrient-dense cruciferous vegetables high in fiber, vitamins C and K, and antioxidants. When roasted or air-fried, they develop a delicious crispy exterior. Bang bang sauce combines creamy, sweet, and spicy elements for an addictive flavor profile that transforms vegetables into something crave-worthy.

### Tips & Variations

- Make bang bang sauce more or less spicy to taste
- Add sesame seeds and green onions for garnish
- For paleo version, use paleo-friendly mayo and sweetener
- Toss with sauce right before serving to maintain crispiness
- Double the sauce recipe if you love extra saucy vegetables

### FAQ
1. **Can I make these ahead?** You can prep and roast the Brussels sprouts ahead, but add the sauce just before serving to keep them crispy.
2. **What exactly is bang bang sauce?** It's typically a combination of mayonnaise, sweet chili sauce, sriracha, and a sweetener - creating a creamy, sweet, and spicy flavor.

# Sweet Potato Tapioca Balls (Mochi-Style)
Gluten-free sweet treat with a crispy outside and soft mochi-like inside - only 20 calories per ball!

**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Date:** Not Available
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D5r7Ltjd5I8

---

## Recipe Card

**Title:** Sweet Potato Tapioca Balls
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes (10 min each side)
**Total Time:** 35 minutes
**Servings:** Approximately 15-20 balls
**Calories:** Approximately 20 calories per ball

### Description
If you like mochi, you will LOVE these! The consistency is amazing with a nice crunch on the outside and a soft mochi texture inside. They're gluten-free, gut healthy, and incredibly low in calories at only 20 calories per ball. Made with tapioca flour from cassava root (known as yuca in Puerto Rico).

### Ingredients
- 1-2 sweet potatoes (depending on size)
- 30g tapioca flour
- 10g corn starch
- 20g sugar (monk fruit zero-calorie sweetener used in video)
- 1 tsp salt
- Cooking spray

### Instructions
1. Steam 1-2 sweet potatoes until completely soft.
2. Mash the steamed sweet potatoes thoroughly.
3. Add 30g tapioca flour to the mashed sweet potato.
4. Add 10g corn starch.
5. Add 20g sugar (or monk fruit sweetener).
6. Add 1 tsp salt.
7. Use your hands to incorporate all ingredients together - the mixture comes together very quickly.
8. Form mixture into ping pong-sized balls.
9. Spray balls with cooking spray on all sides.
10. Place in air fryer.
11. Cook for 10 minutes on one side.
12. Flip and spray other side with cooking spray.
13. Cook for 10 minutes on the second side.
14. Enjoy the crispy exterior and soft mochi interior!

### About the Ingredients
Tapioca flour comes from cassava root (yuca), a staple in Puerto Rican and Latin American cuisine. It creates a unique chewy, mochi-like texture when cooked. Sweet potato provides natural sweetness, fiber, and vitamins while keeping these treats wholesome. Corn starch helps bind the mixture and contributes to the crispy exterior. Using monk fruit sweetener keeps calories even lower while maintaining sweetness.

### Use Cases
- Low-calorie dessert you can enjoy guilt-free
- Gluten-free sweet treat
- Gut-healthy snack (sweet potato and tapioca are gut-friendly)
- Asian-inspired dessert
- Portion-controlled sweet (only 20 calories each!)
- Meal prep desserts for the week
- Alternative to traditional mochi

### Tips & Variations
- Make sure sweet potatoes are fully steamed and very soft for easy mashing

- ¼ red onion
- ⅓ cup egg whites
- 1⅓ cup bread crumbs (panko recommended)
- 1 tbsp low sodium soy sauce

**For Assembly:**
- Pesto
- Shaved parmesan cheese
- Arugula
- Sliced onion
- Cooking spray

### Instructions

**For the High Protein Buns:**
1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. In a large bowl, combine bread flour, baking powder, and salt.

- 1 fried egg (for protein)

### Instructions

**Make Crispy Rice:**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Spread leftover rice in a thin, single layer on a baking dish (as thin as possible).
3. Bake at 400°F for 15 minutes.
4. Switch to broil for the last 5 minutes to get a crispy top.
5. Remove from oven and set aside.

**Make Sweet Potato Tempura:**
1. Cut cooked sweet potato into thick slices or wedge shapes (doesn't have to be perfect).
2. Set up breading station: Place flour in one bowl, egg whites in another, and panko breadcrumbs in a third.
3. Dredge each sweet potato piece: first in flour, then egg white, then breadcrumbs.
4. Place breaded sweet potato pieces in air fryer.

**Make Chimichurri Sauce:**
1. Add all chimichurri ingredients to a food processor or blender.
2. Pulse 10-12 times until combined but still slightly chunky.
3. Set aside.

**Assemble the Bowl:**
1. Place a portion of crispy rice in a bowl.
2. Add sweet potato tempura pieces.
3. Top with a fried egg for protein.
4. Drizzle generously with chimichurri sauce.
5. Serve immediately and enjoy!

### About the Ingredients
This recipe is all about making leftovers exciting again. The thin layer of rice gets incredibly crispy when baked and broiled, creating texture similar to rice cakes. - Can pan-fry sweet potato instead of air frying if preferred
- Use whole eggs instead of just egg whites for richer flavor
- Make chimichurri ahead and store in fridge for up to 1 week
- Try with other leftover roasted vegetables like butternut squash or regular potatoes

- Substitute cilantro for parsley in chimichurri for different flavor

### Instructions
1. In a large bowl, combine room temperature butter and powdered sugar. Mix until well incorporated.
2. Add flour and continue mixing until a dough forms.
3. Shape the dough into a ball or disk.

### About the Ingredients
True shortbread requires only three ingredients: butter, sugar, and flour. The high butter content creates that signature crumbly, melt-in-your-mouth texture. The chilling time allows the butter to firm up and the gluten to relax, resulting in tender cookies.

- Add vanilla extract for classic vanilla shortbread
- Mix in lemon zest for citrus shortbread
- Dip cooled cookies in melted chocolate
- Add spices like cinnamon or ginger for spiced shortbread
- Press dough into a pan and cut into squares after baking for easier preparation
- Store in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks

### FAQ

# Chinese Soufflé Steamed Eggs (Advanced Tutorial)
Silky smooth Chinese-style steamed eggs with a flan-like texture - a decadent breakfast that takes patience but is worth the effort.

**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Date:** 2024
**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a2PEUdhoVbM
**Updated:** February 9, 2026

## Recipe Card

**Title:** Chinese Soufflé Steamed Eggs
**Author:** Holistic Bravo
**Prep Time:** 5 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15-20 minutes
**Total Time:** 25 minutes
**Servings:** 1-2 servings
**Calories:** Approximately 150 calories per serving

### Description
If you're bored of traditional scrambled eggs, try something new! These Chinese soufflé eggs have a texture similar to flan - very smooth and delicious on the palette. The hardest part is having patience to let the eggs cook at a slow rate. It took multiple attempts (5-10 times) to perfect this technique, but once you get it right, you'll have a decadent breakfast that's truly special.

### Ingredients
- 2 eggs
- ⅓ cup boiling water
- Soy sauce (for serving)
- Rice vinegar (for serving)
- LOVE ❤️

### Instructions
1. **Prepare the steamer:** Fill a pan with water and bring to a boil. Have a steamer rack ready.
2. **Mix the eggs:** Crack 2 eggs into a heatproof bowl.
3. **Add water:** Whisk ⅓ cup of the boiling water into the eggs until well combined.
4. **Remove air bubbles:** This is crucial! Use a spoon to carefully remove ALL the little bubbles from the surface. This ensures a perfectly smooth, silky top. Take your time with this step.
5. **Set up for steaming:** Place the steamer rack over your boiling water.
6. **CRITICAL STEP:** Turn off the heat completely and move the pan away from the burner before adding the eggs.
7. **Add egg mixture:** Carefully place your bowl with the egg mixture onto the steamer rack.
8. **Cover and steam:** Put the lid on tightly and let sit. The residual heat will cook the eggs slowly.
9. **Be patient:** This is where patience is key. If you cook too quickly, you'll end up with an omelette instead of silky steamed eggs. Let it steam for about 15-20 minutes.
10. **Check for doneness:** Eggs should be just set with a perfect jiggle in the center, similar to panna cotta or flan.
11. **Serve:** Drizzle with soy sauce and rice vinegar.
12. **Enjoy:** Cut into the eggs to reveal that perfect silky texture!

### About the Ingredients
This recipe is deceptively simple - just eggs, water, and love - but the technique is what matters. The ratio of eggs to boiling water creates the custard-like texture. Removing air bubbles ensures a smooth, beautiful surface. The key is slow, gentle cooking using residual heat rather than active boiling, which would overcook the eggs and create an omelette texture with bubbles.

### Use Cases
Perfect for a decadent breakfast when you have 20 minutes to spare. Ideal for anyone who loves soft-cooked eggs or custard-like textures. Great for exploring Asian breakfast traditions. Excellent for impressing brunch guests with your technique.

### Tips & Variations
- **Trust the process:** It may take several tries to get it right - don't give up!
- Remove ALL air bubbles for the smoothest surface
- Don't rush the cooking - patience is essential
- The eggs should jiggle slightly when done, like panna cotta
- If you don't have a steamer rack, use a higher bowl with less water in the pan, as long as you can cover with a lid
- Add toppings: green onions, sesame oil, crispy fried shallots
- For savory variation: mix in a bit of chicken broth instead of plain water
- Serve with congee (rice porridge) for a traditional breakfast
- Can be served warm or at room temperature

### FAQ
1. **Why did my eggs turn into an omelette?** The heat was too high or the cooking was too fast. Remember to turn OFF the heat before adding the egg bowl, and let residual heat do the cooking slowly.
2. **Why does my steamed egg have holes/bubbles?** You didn't remove enough air bubbles before steaming, or the heat was too high causing the eggs to bubble.
3. **How do I know when they're done?** The eggs should be just set but still jiggle in the center, similar to silken tofu or flan. They'll firm up slightly as they cool.

### Nutrition
Each serving provides approximately 150 calories with 12-14g protein from eggs, 10g fat, 3g carbohydrates, and essential vitamins and minerals including vitamin D, B12, selenium, choline, and iron.
