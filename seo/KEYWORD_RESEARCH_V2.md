## What Changed from V1

V1 relied on Ahrefs Keyword Difficulty (KD) scores to estimate competition. V2 uses the **actual Domain Ratings of sites ranking on page 1** from the Ahrefs SERP exports. This revealed a critical insight:

**Low KD does NOT always mean a new site can rank.**

For example:
- "vegetable side dishes" has KD:2 but the average DR of ranking sites is **82**. No site under DR:42 ranks.
- "vegan dinner recipes" has KD:3 but the average DR of ranking sites is **76**. No site under DR:40 ranks.

Meanwhile:
- "10 easy vegan recipes" has KD:0 and sites with DR **21** and **26** rank on page 1.
- "high protein pasta recipe vegetarian" has KD:1 and a site with DR **10** ranks.

**The new strategy: prioritize keywords where sites with DR < 40 actually appear on page 1.** That's the proof that a new site can compete.

## Revised Blog Post Strategy

### The V1 Mistake

V1 proposed 6 blog posts targeting broad category keywords like "easy vegetarian dinner recipes" and "vegetable side dishes." Those are long-play keywords. A new site should NOT lead with those.

### The V2 Strategy: Long-Tail First, Broaden Later

**Phase 1 (Launch - Month 2): Individual recipe pages + specific long-tail blog posts**

Optimize the 45 individual recipe pages first. Each targets its own long-tail keyword (e.g., "pumpkin gnocchi recipe", "crispy tofu lettuce wraps"). These are the easiest to rank for because they're ultra-specific.

Then write 3 blog posts targeting keywords where DR < 35 sites actually rank.

**Phase 2 (Month 3-6): Medium-tail listicle posts**

Write broader listicle posts as domain authority builds.

**Phase 3 (Month 6+): Head term posts**

Only then target "easy vegetarian dinner recipes" etc.

## Internal Linking Strategy

Every blog post should link to the individual recipe pages it mentions. This passes authority from the blog post to the recipe pages, helping them rank for their own keywords.

```
Blog Post: "10 Easy Vegan Recipes"
  └→ links to: /recipe/simple-two-ingredient-pasta
  └→ links to: /recipe/no-egg-pasta-pomodoro
  └→ links to: /recipe/crispy-tofu-lettuce-wraps
  └→ links to: ... (7 more)

Blog Post: "High-Protein Vegetarian Pasta"
  └→ links to: /recipe/high-protein-tofu-pasta
  └→ links to: /recipe/mushroom-ravioli-with-ricotta-filling
  └→ links to: ... (3 more)
```

Each recipe page should also link to relevant blog posts and to 2-3 related recipes (the "Related Recipes" section already planned for the recipe detail component).

## Key Takeaways

1. **KD alone is misleading.** Always check the actual DR of ranking sites.
2. **Start ultra-specific, go broad later.** Recipe pages → long-tail blog posts → broad listicles.
3. **Sites with DR:10-25 are ranking for our best keywords.** This proves a new site can compete on the right terms.
4. **The broad "easy vegetarian dinner recipes" world requires DR:40+.** Don't waste launch energy here.
5. **Internal linking is the multiplier.** Blog posts boost recipe pages. Recipe pages link to blog posts. This flywheel builds authority.
6. **Soups remain the biggest content gap.** Zero soup recipes, and soup keywords have low KD with decent volume.
